Cozy Chicken and Wild Rice Soup for Chilly Evenings

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This chicken and wild rice soup is like a warm hug in a bowl. Tender chicken, earthy wild rice, and fresh herbs simmer slowly to build deep, comforting flavors. Perfect for chilly spring nights or anytime you crave cozy comfort!

Pro Tips

Rinse the wild rice thoroughly before cooking.
Rinsing removes dust and excess starch from the wild rice, preventing the soup from turning cloudy or gluey. It also helps the grains cook evenly, preserving that signature chewy texture. Skipping this step often results in a less appealing mouthfeel.
Don’t rush the simmering process for the rice.
Wild rice takes longer to cook than white rice and benefits from a slow, gentle simmer. Rushing or boiling vigorously can cause the rice to break down and become mushy, which ruins the texture balance in the soup. Patience here ensures each grain stays intact and flavorful.
Add the cream off heat or on very low heat.
Cream can curdle if exposed to high heat or boiling. Adding it at the end over low heat preserves its silky texture and prevents separation. This step is key for creating that comforting, velvety mouthfeel without any graininess.
Use fresh herbs as a garnish rather than cooking them in.
Fresh herbs provide bright, fresh flavor and a pop of color when added just before serving. Cooking herbs for too long can cause them to lose their aroma and taste, making the soup less vibrant. Garnishing last keeps the flavors lively.
Use cooked chicken added near the end.
Adding pre-cooked shredded chicken late in the cooking process keeps it tender and juicy. Cooking raw chicken directly in the soup risks drying it out or uneven cooking, which affects texture and overall enjoyment.
Let the soup rest before serving.
Allowing the soup to rest off heat for 5-10 minutes lets the flavors meld and the wild rice finish cooking gently. This resting period intensifies the taste and improves the soup’s overall harmony.
Season gradually and taste often.
Soup flavors concentrate as it simmers, so adding salt and pepper in small increments ensures you don’t over-season. Tasting frequently helps balance the seasoning perfectly without overpowering the natural flavors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding wild rice without rinsing

Why it happens Many cooks skip rinsing to save time, not realizing it leaves excess starch and dust that cloud the soup and create a gummy texture.

Fix Always rinse wild rice thoroughly under cold water before cooking to remove starch and impurities. This simple step keeps the broth clear and maintains the rice’s chewy texture.

Boiling the soup too vigorously

Why it happens It’s tempting to turn up the heat to speed cooking, but high heat breaks down wild rice and vegetables unevenly.

Fix Maintain a gentle simmer to allow even cooking. Lower the heat once boiling starts and stir occasionally to prevent sticking and preserve texture.

Adding cream too early or boiling after cream is added

Why it happens Some add cream too soon or boil the soup after adding it, causing curdling or separation.

Fix Add cream at the very end and warm gently without boiling. Stir constantly to keep the soup smooth and creamy.

Overcooking chicken in the soup

Why it happens Adding raw chicken early can dry it out or make it tough by the time the rice is done.

Fix Use pre-cooked shredded chicken and add it near the end of cooking, just long enough to heat through.

Skipping the resting period before serving

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning leads to serving immediately, missing out on flavor melding.

Fix Let the soup rest covered off heat for 5-10 minutes to develop richer, more harmonious flavors.

Variations

Vegetarian Wild Rice Soup

Swap chicken broth for vegetable broth and omit chicken for a hearty vegetarian version. Add mushrooms or tofu for protein and extra umami. The soup will be lighter but still rich with creamy texture and herbs.

Note: Cooking times remain similar but ensure mushrooms or tofu are sautéed well to build flavor.

Spicy Chicken and Wild Rice Soup

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or red pepper flakes when sautéing the aromatics for a subtle spicy kick. This twist warms you up even more and adds a lively depth to the comforting base.

Note: Adjust spice level carefully to avoid overpowering the delicate flavors of herbs and cream.

Low-Fat Chicken Wild Rice Soup

Replace heavy cream with low-fat milk or omit cream entirely for a lighter soup. Use skinless chicken breast and reduce oil for a lower-calorie option that still delivers great flavor and texture.

Note: Soup will be less creamy and rich; consider blending part of the soup for added body.

Instant Pot Chicken and Wild Rice Soup

Use an Instant Pot to speed up the cooking process. Sauté veggies using the sauté function, then add broth, rice, and chicken. Pressure cook for 25 minutes and naturally release. This method saves time without sacrificing flavor.

Note: Avoid overcooking rice by following pressure cooker times carefully.

Herbed Lemon Chicken Wild Rice Soup

Add a splash of fresh lemon juice and zest at the end for a bright, fresh twist. It lightens the richness and adds a subtle zing that complements the herbs beautifully.

Note: Add lemon juice just before serving to preserve brightness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This soup can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to keep it safe and fresh.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The flavors deepen overnight but the wild rice may absorb more liquid, thickening the soup. Reheat gently and add a splash of broth or water if it’s too thick.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight, freezer-safe containers for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap containers tightly and remove as much air as possible. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating slowly on the stove.

Reheating

Reheat over medium-low heat on the stovetop, stirring occasionally. Add a little broth or water if the soup has thickened too much. Avoid microwaving at high heat to prevent cream from separating. Heat until just warmed through.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the soup base and cook the wild rice and veggies 1-2 days ahead, storing separately from the cream and chicken. Add cream and chicken just before reheating to keep textures fresh and flavors vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use white rice instead of wild rice?
Yes, but white rice cooks faster and becomes softer, so add it later in the cooking process to avoid mushiness. Wild rice gives a nuttier flavor and chewy texture that’s unique to this soup.
How do I store leftover soup?
Store leftovers in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 4 days or freeze for up to 3 months. Reheat gently on the stove, adding a splash of broth if needed to restore the soup’s consistency.
Can I make this soup vegan?
For a vegan version, substitute vegetable broth for chicken broth, omit the chicken, and use coconut milk or cashew cream instead of heavy cream. Add mushrooms or tofu for protein.
Why did my cream curdle when I reheated the soup?
Cream curdles if reheated too quickly or at high heat. Reheat slowly over low heat and stir constantly. Adding a little extra liquid when reheating helps maintain smoothness.
What’s the best way to cook wild rice?
Rinse wild rice well and simmer gently in broth or water for about 25-30 minutes until tender but still chewy. Avoid boiling vigorously to prevent the grains from breaking apart.
Can I cook the chicken directly in the soup?
You can, but it risks drying out the chicken or uneven cooking. Using pre-cooked shredded chicken added near the end keeps it tender and juicy.
How do I make the soup thicker?
Simmer the soup uncovered for a few minutes to reduce liquid. Alternatively, blend a portion of the soup and stir it back in for natural thickening without adding flour or starch.
Can I prepare this soup in advance?
Yes, prepare the base and cook the wild rice and veggies a day or two ahead. Add cream and chicken just before reheating to keep textures fresh and flavors vibrant.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 cups, 4 servings

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, room temperature
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 cup wild rice, rinsed
  • 6 cups chicken broth, low sodium
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onion, carrots, and celery, then sauté gently for about 7-8 minutes until the vegetables soften and the onion turns translucent. This step is crucial because it releases the natural sweetness of the veggies, forming a flavorful base for the soup. Avoid browning the vegetables too much, as it can change the soup’s delicate flavor. Stir occasionally to cook evenly and prevent sticking.
⏱ Time: 8
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender and onions are translucent without any browning
Step 2
Add minced garlic, dried thyme, and rosemary, stirring for about 1 minute until fragrant. Adding herbs at this point helps release their essential oils without burning them. Garlic cooks quickly, so watch carefully and stir constantly to avoid bitterness. This layer of aromatics is what gives the soup its comforting depth.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: You can smell the herbs and garlic releasing their fragrance
Step 3
Pour in the rinsed wild rice and chicken broth, then bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Once boiling, reduce heat to low and cover the pot. Let it simmer gently for about 25 minutes, stirring occasionally. Slow simmering allows the wild rice to absorb flavors and cook evenly without becoming mushy. Rinsing the rice beforehand removes excess starch to keep the texture pleasant.
⏱ Time: 25
✓ Doneness: Wild rice is tender but still slightly chewy, and broth is flavorful
Step 4
Stir in the shredded cooked chicken and continue simmering for another 5 minutes to heat through. Adding the chicken later prevents it from drying out and keeps it tender. This step also allows the flavors to meld together nicely. Be careful not to overcook the chicken to maintain moistness.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken is hot and tender, evenly distributed throughout the soup
Step 5
Lower the heat and stir in the heavy cream gently. Heat the soup just until warmed through but do not boil after adding the cream, or it might curdle. The cream adds a luscious texture and richness that balances the earthy rice and herbs. Stir constantly to combine smoothly.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Soup has a creamy consistency without separation or curdling
Step 6
Season the soup with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Seasoning at the end lets you adjust precisely because the flavors concentrate as the soup simmers. Taste carefully and add seasoning in small increments to avoid over-salting.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with no overpowering salt or blandness
Step 7
Turn off the heat and let the soup rest covered for 5-10 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the flavors to deepen and the rice to finish softening gently. Skipping this step can result in a less harmonious taste. It’s worth the wait!
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Soup tastes well-rounded and the texture feels cohesive
Step 8
Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley. The parsley adds a fresh, herbaceous note and brightens the dish visually and flavor-wise. Adding it last keeps its vibrant color and fresh taste intact.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Bright green parsley contrasts nicely with creamy soup
Step 9
Serve immediately with crusty bread or your favorite side. Enjoy the comforting warmth and hearty flavors of this soup while it’s fresh and hot. The texture should be creamy, with tender chicken and a slight chew from the wild rice, making it satisfying and wholesome.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Soup is hot, creamy, and comforting with well-balanced flavors
