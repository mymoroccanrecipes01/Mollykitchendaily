Fluffy Buttery Cheddar Biscuits Perfect for Any Meal or Breakfast

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 22, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These cheesy biscuits are soft and buttery with sharp cheddar melted right through. They come together quickly with buttermilk for that tender crumb. Great for breakfast or alongside dinner!

Pro Tips

Keep all ingredients cold until mixing.
Cold ingredients prevent butter from melting too soon, which is essential for creating flaky layers. Warm butter blends completely and produces dense biscuits instead of light, flaky ones.
Don’t overmix the dough once you add liquid.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes biscuits tough. Mixing just until combined keeps the texture tender and crumbly.
Use a sharp biscuit cutter and press straight down without twisting.
Twisting seals the edges and prevents biscuits from rising properly. Pressing straight down helps biscuits keep their shape and rise evenly.
Fold the dough gently 2-3 times before cutting biscuits.
Folding creates layers by laminating dough and butter. This technique adds flakiness without overworking gluten.
Brush tops with melted butter or buttermilk before baking.
This step promotes browning and adds a shiny, golden crust that tastes buttery and inviting.
Preheat the oven fully before baking.
A hot oven triggers immediate steam from the butter, helping biscuits rise quickly and develop a crisp crust.
Use fresh baking powder and baking soda.
Leavening agents lose potency over time, resulting in flat biscuits. Fresh powder ensures proper rise and texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overworking the dough

Why it happens Many home cooks try to mix or knead the dough too much, thinking it will improve texture.

Fix Mix just until the dough holds together and stop. Use gentle folding instead of kneading to keep biscuits tender.

Using warm butter

Why it happens Butter left out too long or handled too much melts before baking.

Fix Keep butter cold and cut into small cubes. Chill dough if it warms up before baking.

Twisting biscuit cutter when cutting

Why it happens Twisting seals edges and prevents biscuits from rising.

Fix Press cutter straight down and lift up carefully without twisting.

Skipping the folding step

Why it happens Some skip folding to save time, missing out on flakiness.

Fix Take a few extra minutes to fold dough 2-3 times for layered texture.

Not preheating the oven fully

Why it happens Rushing the baking step leads to uneven rise and pale biscuits.

Fix Always wait for oven to reach temperature before baking.

Variations

Herb and Garlic Cheddar Biscuits

Add 1 tablespoon each of finely chopped fresh rosemary and garlic powder to the dry ingredients for an herby twist. These biscuits pair beautifully with soups and roasted meats, adding a fragrant, savory note.

Note: Fresh herbs add moisture; adjust flour slightly if dough feels too wet.

Spicy Jalapeño Cheddar Biscuits

Mix in 1-2 finely diced jalapeños and a pinch of cayenne pepper for a spicy kick. These are great for breakfast with eggs or as a bold side for chili.

Note: Remove seeds to control heat level; moisture from peppers may require a touch more flour.

Bacon Cheddar Biscuits

Fold in 1/2 cup cooked, crumbled bacon for a smoky, meaty flavor. Perfect for brunch or paired with greens for a hearty meal.

Note: Drain bacon fat well to avoid greasy dough; add at the same stage as cheese.

Vegan Cheddar Biscuits

Use vegan butter and plant-based buttermilk (made with lemon juice and almond milk) plus vegan cheddar shreds. These biscuits are tender and flavorful without dairy.

Note: Vegan butter often melts faster; keep dough chilled and handle gently.

Whole Wheat Cheddar Biscuits

Replace half the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour for a nuttier taste and more fiber. These biscuits are heartier and pair well with soups and stews.

Note: Whole wheat flour absorbs more liquid; add a splash more buttermilk if dough feels dry.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store baked biscuits covered loosely with a clean kitchen towel at room temperature for up to 2 days. This keeps them from drying out while allowing some breathability to maintain softness.

Refrigerator

Keep biscuits in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. They may lose some freshness and become firmer, so warm gently before serving to restore softness.

Freezer

Wrap biscuits tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat in a warm oven for best texture.

Reheating

Reheat biscuits wrapped in foil in a 350°F oven for 10-12 minutes until warmed through. You can also microwave briefly but oven reheating better preserves crispness and flakiness.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough and cut biscuits up to 1 day ahead, then refrigerate on the baking sheet covered lightly with plastic wrap. Bake fresh the next day for best results.

FAQ

Can I substitute regular milk for buttermilk?
You can mix 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar into 1 cup of milk and let it sit for 5 minutes to mimic buttermilk’s acidity. This helps activate the baking soda for proper rise and adds the slight tang but isn’t quite the same as using real buttermilk.
Why are my biscuits dense and not fluffy?
Common causes include overmixing the dough, using warm butter, or an oven that isn’t hot enough. Be gentle mixing, keep butter cold, and preheat your oven fully to ensure fluffy biscuits.
Can I freeze the dough before baking?
Yes! After cutting biscuits, place them on a baking sheet and freeze until solid, then transfer to a freezer bag. Bake from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the baking time.
How do I store leftover biscuits?
Keep leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. Warm before serving to refresh texture.
What if I don’t have a biscuit cutter?
You can use a round glass or cookie cutter. Just press straight down without twisting to get the best rise.
Can I use pre-shredded cheese?
Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can affect melting. Freshly shredded cheese melts better and produces a smoother texture.
Why do I need both baking powder and baking soda?
Baking powder contains acid and base and works alone for leavening, but baking soda reacts with the acid in buttermilk to give an extra lift and a tender crumb.
Can I add herbs or spices?
Absolutely! Fresh or dried herbs like rosemary, thyme, or spices like garlic powder work well and add flavor. Just fold them in with the cheese.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 biscuits

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup buttermilk, cold
  • 1 1/2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, shredded

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This high temperature helps the biscuits rise quickly, giving them a lovely golden crust while keeping the inside tender. Don't skip lining the sheet — it prevents sticking and helps with even browning.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated to ensure proper biscuit rise
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking powder, salt, and baking soda. Sifting helps aerate the flour and evenly distributes the leavening agents, which is key for fluffy biscuits. Mixing dry ingredients first ensures consistent rising.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined without lumps
Step 3
Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, quickly cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized bits. The cold butter creates steam during baking, which forms flaky layers. Avoid overworking or melting the butter.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter pieces are evenly coated but still visible in small chunks
Step 4
Stir in the shredded sharp cheddar cheese evenly through the flour and butter mixture. Distributing the cheese now ensures every biscuit has pockets of melty cheese. Be gentle to keep the butter cold and prevent the dough from becoming greasy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheese is well mixed without clumping
Step 5
Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the cold buttermilk. Using a fork or spatula, gently mix until just combined. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable. Overmixing develops gluten and leads to tough biscuits, so stop as soon as it holds together.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough forms a cohesive ball without dry flour spots
Step 6
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Gently pat it into a 1-inch thick rectangle. Fold the dough over itself 2-3 times to create layers, then pat it back to 1-inch thickness. This folding technique helps create flaky layers by laminating butter and dough without overworking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is smooth, slightly sticky, and layered
Step 7
Use a floured biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out biscuits, pressing straight down without twisting. Twisting seals the edges and prevents proper rise. Gather scraps gently and repeat until dough is used.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are evenly cut with clean edges
Step 8
Place biscuits on the prepared baking sheet, close but not touching to allow heat circulation. For a golden top, brush the biscuits lightly with melted butter or buttermilk before baking.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are arranged evenly and brushed for browning
Step 9
Bake in the preheated oven for 15-18 minutes until golden brown on top and cooked through. Avoid opening the oven door mid-bake to keep the heat steady. When done, biscuits should have a slightly crisp top and soft, fluffy interior.
⏱ Time: 15-18 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown tops with a springy texture when lightly pressed

Final Thoughts

Give these buttery cheddar biscuits a try—you’ll love how easy and delicious they are. Don’t forget to share how yours turn out!
