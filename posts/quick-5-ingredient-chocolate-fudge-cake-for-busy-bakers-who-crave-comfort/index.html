Super Fudgy 5-Ingredient Chocolate Fudge Cake for Any Occasion

This rich chocolate fudge cake is a total lifesaver when you want something indulgent but quick. Made with just five simple ingredients, it’s insanely fudgy and perfect for cozy nights or last-minute celebrations.

Pro Tips

Always sift your dry ingredients.
Sifting removes lumps and aerates the flour and cocoa powder. This results in a smoother batter and a lighter texture, which is particularly important in a fudge cake to avoid bitter clumps and ensure even baking.
Let melted butter cool before mixing with eggs.
Hot butter can cook the eggs prematurely, causing the batter to separate and become grainy. Cooling the butter slightly allows it to emulsify smoothly with the eggs, creating a glossy batter that bakes into a tender crumb.
Use room temperature eggs for better mixing.
Room temperature eggs combine more easily and trap air better when whisked with sugar, which helps give the cake a subtle lift despite its dense texture. Cold eggs can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly.
Avoid overmixing the batter after adding dry ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make the cake tough and cakey rather than fudgy. Fold gently just until no dry streaks remain to keep the texture dense and moist.
Check for doneness with a toothpick but expect moist crumbs.
Fudge cakes should be slightly underbaked compared to regular cakes to stay moist and fudgy. A toothpick with moist crumbs (not wet batter) signals the perfect fudgy texture.
Use an 8-inch pan for the best thickness.
A smaller pan yields a thicker cake, which enhances the fudgy texture and rich mouthfeel. Larger pans will spread the batter too thin and dry out the cake.
Cool the cake in the pan before removing.
Cooling allows the cake to set so it doesn’t fall apart when unmolded. Removing it too early can cause it to break because the center is still very soft.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using hot melted butter directly.

Why it happens Many assume melted butter can be added immediately, but if it’s too hot, it cooks the eggs and causes the batter to separate.

Fix Let the melted butter cool to just warm before mixing it into the eggs. This prevents curdling and ensures a smooth, glossy batter.

Skipping sifting of dry ingredients.

Why it happens People often skip sifting to save time, not realizing lumps can ruin the texture.

Fix Always sift flour and cocoa powder to remove lumps and aerate the mixture for even baking and smooth texture.

Overmixing after adding flour.

Why it happens Trying to get a perfectly smooth batter can lead to overmixing, which develops gluten.

Fix Fold gently just until combined; a few small streaks are okay. This keeps the cake tender and fudgy.

Overbaking the cake.

Why it happens Waiting too long to test doneness causes the cake to dry out.

Fix Check at 18 minutes by inserting a toothpick; remove when it comes out with moist crumbs, not wet batter.

Using cold eggs.

Why it happens Cold eggs don’t blend well with melted butter and sugar, resulting in a curdled batter.

Fix Bring eggs to room temperature before starting for better emulsification and texture.

Variations

Nutty Chocolate Fudge Cake

Add 1/2 cup chopped toasted walnuts or pecans to the batter for a delightful crunch and nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with the rich chocolate. The nuts add texture and make the cake feel a bit more special for gatherings.

Note: Fold nuts gently into the batter at the end to avoid overmixing; toast nuts beforehand to boost flavor.

Espresso-Infused Fudge Cake

Stir 1 tablespoon of instant espresso powder into the dry ingredients to deepen the chocolate flavor with a subtle coffee kick. This variation adds complexity and pairs perfectly with a cup of coffee or milk.

Note: Mix espresso powder thoroughly with cocoa and flour to avoid clumps and ensure even distribution.

Gluten-Free Chocolate Fudge Cake

Swap the all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. This makes the cake accessible for gluten-sensitive eaters without sacrificing fudginess or flavor, though texture might be slightly different.

Note: Ensure your gluten-free blend contains xanthan gum or similar for structure; baking time may vary slightly.

Vegan Chocolate Fudge Cake

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use vegan butter or coconut oil instead of butter. This version keeps the rich fudgy texture while being plant-based and dairy-free.

Note: Let flax eggs thicken fully before mixing; baking time may increase by a few minutes.

Spicy Chocolate Fudge Cake

Add 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the dry ingredients for a warm, spicy twist that enhances the chocolate depth. Great for those who love a little heat with their sweets.

Note: Mix spices evenly with cocoa powder; start with a small amount of cayenne to avoid overpowering.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cake keeps well at room temperature for up to 24 hours if covered tightly with plastic wrap or in an airtight container. Leaving it uncovered can dry out the fudgy texture quickly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The cake will become firmer and less soft but still delicious. Bring to room temperature or warm gently before serving to soften.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil, or store in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave it overnight in the fridge or at room temperature for a few hours. Reheat gently to restore softness.

Reheating

Warm slices in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or cream before reheating can help retain moisture. Avoid overheating, which can dry out the cake.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a few hours ahead and keep it refrigerated before baking. Alternatively, bake the cake a day in advance, store it covered at room temperature, and serve the next day with fresh cream or ice cream.

FAQ

Can I use cocoa powder substitutes like Dutch-processed cocoa?
Yes, but Dutch-processed cocoa is less acidic and may affect the cake’s flavor and rise slightly. This recipe is designed for natural cocoa powder, which reacts better with eggs for the classic fudgy texture and slightly tangy chocolate flavor.
What if I don’t have an 8-inch cake pan?
You can use a 9-inch pan but expect the cake to be thinner and bake slightly faster—start checking doneness a few minutes earlier. A smaller pan yields a thicker, fudgier cake, but a 9-inch pan works fine in a pinch.
How do I know when the cake is perfectly baked?
Insert a toothpick into the center—if it comes out with moist crumbs but no wet batter, it’s done. The edges should look set and start pulling away from the pan. Overbaking dries it out and ruins the fudgy texture.
Can I make this cake dairy-free?
Yes, substitute the butter with coconut oil or vegan butter alternatives and use flax eggs instead of regular eggs for a vegan version. The texture will be slightly different but still deliciously fudgy.
Why is my cake dry or crumbly?
Common causes include overbaking, overmixing the batter, or using hot melted butter that cooked the eggs. Make sure to follow timing closely, fold gently, and let butter cool before mixing.
Can I add chocolate chips or nuts?
Absolutely! Fold in 1/2 cup of chocolate chips or chopped nuts after combining the batter for extra texture and flavor. Toast nuts first for a richer taste.
Is it better to use salted or unsalted butter?
Unsalted butter is recommended so you can control the salt level precisely. Salted butter varies in salt content and can affect the cake’s flavor balance.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, but bake in a larger pan or multiple pans to avoid a too-thick batter that won’t cook through evenly. Adjust baking times accordingly and check often.

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, sifted
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8-inch round cake pan, then line it with parchment paper. This step ensures your cake releases easily without sticking. Avoid skipping the parchment as the fudge cake is dense and can stick to the pan, making it hard to remove cleanly. Greasing alone might not be enough, so the parchment acts as a safety net.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased and parchment paper is securely fitted without wrinkles
Step 2
In a medium bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour and cocoa powder. Sifting helps remove lumps and aerates the dry ingredients, which is important for even mixing. It also prevents clumps of cocoa powder that can create bitter pockets in the cake. If you skip this, your cake texture might be uneven or gritty.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are smooth, lump-free, and well combined
Step 3
In a separate large bowl, whisk the eggs and sugar together until pale and slightly thickened, about 2-3 minutes. This step incorporates air, giving the cake a subtle lift despite its fudgy texture. Using room temperature eggs helps them combine better with the sugar, creating a smoother batter. Cold eggs can result in a curdled or separated mixture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Eggs and sugar form a pale, slightly fluffy mixture
Step 4
Slowly pour the melted butter into the egg mixture while whisking continuously to emulsify. Adding melted butter too hot can cook the eggs, so let it cool slightly before combining. This emulsification step ensures the fat is evenly distributed, which is key to the cake’s moist, fudgy texture. If you add it too quickly or hot, the batter might separate and become grainy.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter looks smooth, glossy, and homogenous without streaks
Step 5
Gently fold the sifted dry ingredients into the wet mixture using a spatula. Fold just until combined to avoid overworking the batter, which can develop gluten and make the cake tough. The batter should be thick but spreadable. If overmixed, the texture will be less fudgy and more cake-like.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: No visible pockets of flour or cocoa, batter is thick and smooth
Step 6
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Even spreading helps the cake bake uniformly. Uneven batter can cause some parts to dry out while others remain undercooked. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Surface is smooth and level, no large air bubbles
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 18-22 minutes. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out with moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking dries out the cake and reduces fudginess, so keep a close eye near the end. The edges should be set and slightly pulling away from the pan.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick with moist crumbs, cake edges firm and slightly pulled from pan
Step 8
Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 15 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Cooling slightly helps the cake set and makes it easier to remove without breaking. If you try to unmold it too soon, it may fall apart because it’s still very soft and fudgy inside.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake edges firm but center still soft, cake releases cleanly from pan
Step 9
Serve once completely cooled or slightly warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or whipped cream. The contrast between the warm fudgy cake and cold cream is heavenly. If you refrigerate leftovers, bring them to room temperature or warm slightly before serving for best texture.
⏱ Time: Varies
✓ Doneness: Cake is moist and fudgy, melts in your mouth with creamy accompaniment
