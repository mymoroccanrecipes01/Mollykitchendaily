Soft and Buttery Cheddar Biscuits Inspired by Red Lobster's Famous Recipe

These cheddar biscuits are insanely soft and buttery with a hint of garlic and cream cheese that makes them irresistible. Perfect for cozy dinners or a snack to share with friends!

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and cream cheese cold until mixing.
Cold fats create pockets of steam during baking, which helps the biscuits rise and become flaky. Warm fats blend too well into the flour, resulting in denser, tougher biscuits.
Don’t overmix the dough once you add the wet ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten, making the biscuits chewy instead of tender. Mixing just until combined keeps the texture light and soft.
Use freshly grated cheese instead of pre-shredded.
Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that prevent smooth melting and can affect biscuit texture. Freshly grated cheese melts evenly, enhancing flavor and crumb.
Bake biscuits on a hot baking sheet or preheated pan.
A hot surface jump-starts the rise by triggering baking powder quickly and helps form a golden crust, improving texture and appearance.
Space biscuits slightly apart on the baking sheet.
This allows heat to circulate and edges to crisp up rather than steaming and becoming soggy where biscuits touch.
Use a light touch when shaping and cutting the dough.
Pressing too hard compresses the dough, reducing flakiness and rise. Gentle handling preserves air bubbles and texture.
Brush tops with melted butter right after baking.
This adds extra richness and a beautiful sheen, enhancing flavor and making biscuits irresistibly soft on the outside.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted butter instead of cold butter

Why it happens Many assume butter should be soft to mix easily, but melted butter won’t create flaky layers.

Fix Always chill butter and cut it into small cubes. Use a pastry cutter or fingers quickly to keep it cold. This ensures steam pockets form during baking for flakiness.

Overmixing the dough after adding wet ingredients

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix until perfectly smooth, but this develops gluten and toughens biscuits.

Fix Mix just until the flour disappears. It’s okay if the dough looks a bit shaggy. Stop mixing early to keep biscuits tender.

Not preheating the oven fully before baking

Why it happens Rushing the process can lead to underbaked, dense biscuits.

Fix Preheat oven for at least 10-15 minutes until it reaches temperature. A hot oven ensures proper rise and crust formation.

Using old baking powder

Why it happens Baking powder loses potency over time, so biscuits won’t rise well.

Fix Test baking powder by adding a teaspoon to hot water. If it bubbles vigorously, it’s fresh. Replace if not active.

Cutting biscuits too close together on the pan

Why it happens Biscuits touching each other steam and don’t get crispy edges.

Fix Leave at least 1-inch space between biscuits to allow heat circulation and proper browning.

Variations

Herbed Cheddar Biscuits

Add 1 tablespoon of fresh chopped rosemary or thyme to the dry ingredients for a fragrant twist. These herbs complement the cheddar and garlic beautifully, making the biscuits perfect for holiday dinners or special occasions.

Note: Fresh herbs add moisture, so keep dough slightly firmer to maintain structure; reduce milk by a tablespoon if needed.

Spicy Jalapeño Cheddar Biscuits

Fold in 1/4 cup finely diced jalapeños and substitute sharp cheddar with pepper jack cheese for a spicy kick. Great for serving alongside chili or barbecue.

Note: Jalapeños add moisture; pat dry before adding. Increase baking time by 1-2 minutes if biscuits brown too quickly.

Bacon Cheddar Biscuits

Mix in 1/2 cup cooked and crumbled crispy bacon for smoky flavor. These make a hearty breakfast or snack when paired with eggs or soup.

Note: Drain bacon well to avoid soggy dough. Incorporate just before adding wet ingredients to distribute evenly.

Gluten-Free Cheddar Biscuits

Use a 1-to-1 gluten-free baking flour blend in place of all-purpose flour. Add 1 teaspoon xanthan gum if your blend lacks it to improve texture. These biscuits remain tender and cheesy without gluten.

Note: Gluten-free dough is stickier; handle gently and avoid overmixing to prevent crumbly results.

Vegan Cheddar Biscuits

Replace butter with vegan margarine and cream cheese with a plant-based alternative. Use unsweetened almond milk instead of dairy. Nutritional yeast adds cheesy flavor to mimic cheddar.

Note: Vegan fats melt differently; chill dough longer before baking to help with structure and rise.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store biscuits in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them covered to prevent drying out. They’re best eaten fresh but still tasty within this window.

Refrigerator

Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Cold biscuits may harden, so warm them slightly in the oven or microwave before serving to restore softness.

Freezer

Freeze biscuits in a zip-top bag or airtight container for up to 3 months. Wrap individually in plastic wrap for best texture. Thaw at room temperature or warm from frozen in the oven.

Reheating

Reheat biscuits in a 350°F oven for 5-8 minutes until warmed through. Avoid microwaving too long as it can make them rubbery. Adding a small pat of butter before reheating restores moisture.

Make Ahead

Prepare dough up to shaping, then wrap and refrigerate for 24 hours before baking. This allows flavors to meld and fits busy schedules. Bake fresh the day of serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use salted butter instead of unsalted?
Yes, but reduce or omit the added salt in the recipe to avoid overly salty biscuits. Salted butter varies in salt content, so taste the dough if possible before baking.
What if I don’t have cream cheese? Can I skip it?
You can omit cream cheese, but biscuits will be less moist and tender. To compensate, add a bit more butter or milk, but the unique texture and flavor from cream cheese will be missing.
How do I store leftover biscuits to keep them fresh?
Place cooled biscuits in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or refrigerate for up to 5 days. Reheat gently to restore softness before serving.
Can I freeze the biscuit dough before baking?
Yes! Shape the biscuits, then freeze them on a baking sheet. Once frozen, transfer to a freezer bag. Bake from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the baking time.
How do I make the biscuits fluffier?
Make sure your baking powder is fresh and don’t overmix the dough. Also, handle the dough gently to preserve air pockets and bake in a hot oven to get a good rise.
Is garlic powder necessary? What if I want to omit it?
Garlic powder adds subtle savory depth but can be omitted if you prefer plain cheddar biscuits. You might want to add a pinch more salt or herbs for extra flavor.
Can I make these biscuits vegan?
Yes, substitute butter and cream cheese with plant-based alternatives and use a non-dairy milk like almond or oat milk. Nutritional yeast can add a cheesy flavor.
Why are my biscuits dense instead of fluffy?
Common reasons include overmixing the dough, using warm butter, old baking powder, or not baking at a high enough temperature. Handle ingredients gently and keep fats cold.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 biscuits

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, grated
  • 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 3/4 cup whole milk

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This temperature is key for getting that golden, crispy outside while keeping the inside soft. If the oven isn’t hot enough, your biscuits might turn out dense instead of fluffy.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated and baking sheet ready
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking powder, garlic powder, and salt. This dry mix is the foundation of your biscuits, and sifting helps avoid lumps and ensures an even rise.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients combined evenly with no clumps
Step 3
Cut the cold butter into the flour mixture using a pastry cutter or your fingertips until it resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces. The cold butter creates steam while baking, which makes the biscuits flaky and tender. Avoid warming the butter with your hands here.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with small butter chunks visible
Step 4
Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese evenly into the crumbly mixture. This ensures every biscuit gets pockets of cheesy flavor. You want the cheese well distributed but not overmixed to keep texture light.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheese is mixed throughout but not melted or clumped
Step 5
In a separate small bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with the milk until smooth and slightly runny. This combo adds moisture and richness that keeps the biscuits soft without making the dough too wet.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream cheese and milk form a smooth, pourable liquid
Step 6
Pour the cream cheese mixture into the dry ingredients and gently fold with a spatula until just combined. Overmixing activates gluten and can toughen the biscuits, so stop as soon as you don’t see dry flour.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough holds together but still looks slightly shaggy and soft
Step 7
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently pat it into a 1-inch thick rectangle. Avoid kneading; just fold the dough over itself a couple of times to create layers for flakiness.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough forms a cohesive rectangle without sticking excessively
Step 8
Cut the dough into 8 equal squares or use a biscuit cutter for rounds. Place them spaced slightly apart on the baking sheet to allow rising. This spacing helps the edges crisp up nicely.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits evenly shaped and arranged on baking sheet
Step 9
Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes until golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Watch closely near the end to avoid overbaking, which dries them out.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are golden and springy to the touch
