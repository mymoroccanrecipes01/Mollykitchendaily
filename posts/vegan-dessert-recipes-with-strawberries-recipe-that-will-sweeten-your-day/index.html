Creamy Vegan Strawberry Coconut Dessert That’s Perfectly Sweet and Dairy-Free

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 27, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

If you’re craving something sweet but want to skip dairy and eggs, this vegan strawberry coconut dessert is a dream. The coconut cream adds a luscious texture, while fresh berries keep it light and refreshing. Great for summer nights or anytime you need a simple treat!

Pro Tips

Always chill your coconut cream overnight before whipping.
Chilling allows the fat to solidify and separate from the liquid, which is essential for achieving a thick, scoopable cream. Without this step, whipping will be ineffective, and the texture will be runny and disappointing.
Use a hand mixer or stand mixer to whip the coconut cream for best results.
Whipping by hand can be tiring and less consistent. A mixer incorporates air evenly, creating a fluffy texture that mimics traditional whipped cream. This aeration is key to the dessert’s light mouthfeel.
Don’t shake the can of coconut cream before opening.
Shaking mixes the cream and liquid back together, making it harder to separate the thick cream needed for whipping. Opening the can gently ensures you scoop only the solid cream.
Fold in the strawberries gently to keep the cream airy.
Vigorous stirring deflates the whipped cream, causing it to lose volume and become watery. Gentle folding keeps the texture light and maintains the dessert’s visual appeal.
Serve the dessert slightly softened after chilling.
Coconut cream can become firm and less flavorful when too cold. Letting it sit at room temperature for a few minutes before serving improves texture and taste.
Use powdered sugar instead of granulated sugar for a smoother texture.
Powdered sugar dissolves quickly and evenly, preventing graininess in the whipped cream. It ensures a silky mouthfeel without any gritty texture.
Add vanilla extract to enhance flavor complexity.
Vanilla brings warmth and depth, balancing the coconut’s natural flavor and complementing the strawberries. It elevates the dessert from simple to memorable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using coconut milk instead of coconut cream.

Why it happens Many confuse coconut milk with coconut cream, assuming they are interchangeable. Coconut milk has lower fat content and won’t whip properly.

Fix Always choose full-fat coconut cream in a can and chill it overnight. This ensures you get the thick, solid layer needed for whipping.

Shaking the can before opening.

Why it happens People often shake cans out of habit or to mix contents, not realizing it prevents cream separation.

Fix Place the can in the fridge overnight without shaking and open gently to scoop the thick cream on top.

Overwhipping the coconut cream.

Why it happens Trying to get more volume, some whip too long, causing the cream to break down and become grainy.

Fix Whip just until soft peaks form. Stop as soon as it’s fluffy and smooth to keep the right texture.

Mixing the watery liquid from the can back into the cream.

Why it happens Not separating properly leads to a runny mixture that won’t hold shape.

Fix Scoop only the solid cream layer and discard or save the liquid for smoothies.

Adding strawberries too early or mixing too hard.

Why it happens Heavy mixing breaks down berries and deflates the cream.

Fix Fold berries in gently at the end to keep texture and volume intact.

Variations

Mixed Berry Coconut Cream Dessert

Swap out strawberries for a mix of blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries for a colorful twist. The tartness varies with different berries, creating a more complex flavor profile that’s equally refreshing and creamy.

Note: Adjust sweetness slightly as mixed berries can be more or less tart than strawberries. Fold gently to preserve cream texture.

Chocolate Coconut Strawberry Delight

Add 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the coconut cream before whipping for a rich chocolate flavor. Top with strawberries for a decadent yet dairy-free treat that’s perfect for chocolate lovers.

Note: Sift cocoa powder to avoid lumps and increase whipping time slightly to incorporate it fully.

Lemon Zest Vegan Strawberry Cream

Add a teaspoon of fresh lemon zest to the whipped coconut cream for a bright citrus note that complements the strawberries beautifully. This adds a fresh, zesty dimension to the dessert.

Note: Fold zest in after whipping to avoid bitterness and preserve cream aeration.

Tropical Pineapple Coconut Cream

Replace strawberries with finely chopped fresh pineapple and a sprinkle of toasted coconut flakes. This variation brings a tropical vibe and a sweet, tangy flavor that pairs perfectly with coconut cream.

Note: Drain pineapple well to prevent excess moisture from thinning the cream.

Vanilla Almond Coconut Cream with Strawberries

Add a teaspoon of almond extract along with vanilla for a nutty twist. Top with sliced almonds and strawberries for crunch and flavor complexity.

Note: Reduce vanilla slightly to avoid overpowering almond flavor; fold in nuts just before serving.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dessert should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours as coconut cream can spoil. Cover with plastic wrap or a lid if you need to keep it out briefly during serving.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. The texture may firm up more, so let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes before serving to soften.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as coconut cream texture changes and can become grainy. If frozen, thaw slowly in the fridge and gently re-whip before serving.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled or slightly softened at room temperature for best taste and texture.

Make Ahead

You can whip the coconut cream and prepare the strawberry mixture 1 day ahead, then assemble just before serving to keep the berries fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use coconut milk instead of coconut cream?
Coconut milk is too thin and won’t whip into a thick cream. For best results, use full-fat coconut cream chilled overnight to get the right texture and richness.
What if I can’t find canned coconut cream?
You can refrigerate a can of full-fat coconut milk and scoop out the solid cream layer after chilling. Alternatively, some stores sell coconut cream separately.
How do I make this dessert less sweet?
Reduce the powdered sugar or maple syrup to your taste. The fresh strawberries add natural sweetness, so start with less and adjust after whipping.
Can I use frozen strawberries?
Frozen berries release more water when thawed, which can make the dessert watery. It’s best to use fresh strawberries or drain thawed berries thoroughly.
How long can I store leftovers?
Keep leftovers refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The texture may change slightly, so soften before serving.
Can I make this dessert nut-free?
Yes, this recipe is naturally nut-free unless you add nuts as garnish. Just ensure your sweetener and vanilla extract are nut-free as well.
Why is my coconut cream not whipping?
If the cream isn’t cold enough or if you included the watery part, it won’t whip. Make sure to chill the can overnight and only scoop the solidified cream.
Can I add other fruits?
Absolutely! Blueberries, raspberries, or mango work well. Just fold them in gently to keep the cream fluffy.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut cream, chilled overnight
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • Optional: fresh mint leaves for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by chilling your can of full-fat coconut cream in the fridge overnight. This step is crucial because the cold temperature helps the cream separate from the liquid, giving you that thick, scoopable texture. When you open the can, scoop out only the solidified cream on top, leaving the watery part behind. Using chilled coconut cream ensures your dessert will be rich and fluffy, similar to traditional whipped cream but completely plant-based. Avoid shaking the can before opening, as that mixes the cream back into the liquid and makes it harder to separate.
⏱ Time: Overnight
✓ Doneness: You should have a thick, creamy layer on top that holds its shape when scooped.
Step 2
Transfer the solid coconut cream into a large mixing bowl. Add powdered sugar or your sweetener of choice along with the vanilla extract. Using a hand mixer or whisk, beat the cream on medium-high speed until fluffy and smooth, about 2-3 minutes. This whipping step incorporates air, making the cream light and airy. If you rush it or skip whipping, the texture will be dense and less dessert-like. Taste and adjust sweetness if needed, keeping in mind the strawberries will also add natural sugar.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be light, smooth, and hold soft peaks when you lift the whisk.
Step 3
Gently fold in half of the sliced strawberries into the whipped coconut cream using a spatula. Folding, rather than stirring vigorously, helps keep the airy texture intact while evenly distributing the berries. This adds bursts of fresh flavor and a pretty appearance. Be careful not to overmix or the cream might deflate and become watery.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries are evenly mixed without breaking down the cream’s fluffiness.
Step 4
Spoon the creamy strawberry mixture into serving dishes or glasses. Layer with the remaining fresh strawberry slices on top for a beautiful, fresh look. This layering adds texture contrast and makes each bite exciting. It’s also visually appealing when serving guests. You can garnish with fresh mint leaves if you like, adding a pop of color and a hint of freshness.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert looks smooth and creamy with vibrant strawberry slices on top.
Step 5
Chill the assembled desserts in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld and the cream to firm up slightly, making it more refreshing and enjoyable. Skipping this step might result in a looser texture and less cohesive flavors. If you’re in a hurry, 15 minutes is okay, but longer chilling is better.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert feels cool and slightly set but still creamy when touched.
Step 6
When ready to serve, remove from the fridge and let sit at room temperature for 5 minutes to soften slightly for the best mouthfeel. Overly cold coconut cream can feel too firm and less flavorful. This brief rest helps bring out the creaminess and enhances the strawberry’s natural sweetness.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream is soft and smooth but still chilled.
Step 7
If you want to add a little extra flair, drizzle a touch of maple syrup or a sprinkle of toasted coconut flakes on top before serving. These add texture and an extra layer of flavor that complements the coconut cream and strawberries beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toppings are evenly distributed and enhance the dessert’s look and taste.
Step 8
Clean up any leftover coconut cream from the edges of the bowl to keep your presentation neat. Presentation matters, especially if you’re serving guests or sharing photos. A clean edge makes the dessert look professionally done even if it’s super simple to make.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Serving dishes look tidy and inviting.
Step 9
Enjoy your vegan strawberry coconut dessert immediately or keep refrigerated until serving. This dessert is best enjoyed fresh but can be stored for a day or two. Just give it a gentle stir before serving if it separates slightly.
⏱ Time: Immediate or chilled
✓ Doneness: Dessert tastes fresh, creamy, and balanced between sweet and tart.
