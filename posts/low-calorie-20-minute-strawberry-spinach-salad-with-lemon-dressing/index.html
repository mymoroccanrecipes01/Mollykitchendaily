Fresh Strawberry Spinach Salad with Zesty Lemon Dressing for Quick Lunches

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 10, 2026
10 min Prep Time
10 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This strawberry spinach salad is a bright, fresh mix perfect for a light lunch or side. The lemon dressing adds a zing that balances the sweet berries beautifully. Ready in 20 minutes, it’s a fresh pick-me-up any day!

Pro Tips

Toast the almonds just before assembling the salad.
Toasting nuts releases their essential oils and enhances flavor, adding a warm nuttiness that contrasts beautifully with the fresh ingredients. If you toast too early and store them, they can lose crispness and become stale, which lessens the impact on texture.
Dry the spinach leaves very well after washing.
Any excess water on the leaves dilutes the dressing and causes the salad to become soggy quickly. Using a salad spinner or patting dry with paper towels ensures the dressing clings properly and keeps the leaves crisp and fresh.
Add the dressing just before serving.
Dressing the salad too early causes greens to wilt and lose their crispness. Adding it last-minute keeps the texture fresh and prevents the salad from becoming limp, making every bite enjoyable.
Slice strawberries thinly for even flavor distribution.
Thin slices blend better with the spinach and other ingredients, providing sweet bursts without overwhelming the salad. Thick slices can make the salad uneven in texture and flavor balance.
Use fresh lemon juice instead of bottled for the dressing.
Fresh lemon juice offers a brighter, more vibrant acidity that bottled juice lacks. This brightness is crucial to balancing the sweetness of honey and strawberries, making the salad lively and fresh.
Whisk the dressing vigorously to emulsify.
Proper emulsification ensures the oil and lemon juice combine smoothly, preventing separation. This coats the salad evenly and delivers consistent flavor in every bite.
Adjust salt carefully after adding feta.
Feta cheese is naturally salty, so you want to add salt sparingly until tasting the finished salad. Over-salting can overpower the delicate flavors of spinach and strawberries.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using wet spinach leaves

Why it happens People often rinse greens and add them to salad while still damp, assuming moisture won’t affect texture.

Fix Use a salad spinner or pat leaves dry thoroughly before assembling. This prevents dilution of dressing and sogginess, keeping the salad crisp and fresh longer.

Skipping toasting almonds

Why it happens Some skip this step to save time, not realizing it affects flavor and texture.

Fix Take the extra few minutes to toast nuts in a dry pan until golden and fragrant. This adds a critical crunch and deeper flavor that raw nuts lack.

Adding dressing too early

Why it happens People often dress salads in advance to save time but don’t realize it causes wilting.

Fix Dress the salad just before serving to maintain crispness and freshness. Keep dressing separate if prepping earlier.

Using bottled lemon juice

Why it happens Convenience often leads to using bottled juice, which lacks fresh acidity.

Fix Always use fresh lemon juice for the best bright flavor. It really makes a difference in the dressing’s zing.

Over-salting the salad

Why it happens Feta cheese’s saltiness is often overlooked, leading to too much additional salt.

Fix Add salt gradually and taste after feta is added. Adjust carefully to avoid overpowering the salad’s delicate balance.

Variations

Add Grilled Chicken for a Heartier Salad

For a fuller meal, toss in sliced grilled chicken breast. The smoky, savory flavor pairs wonderfully with the sweet and tangy salad components, turning this light salad into a satisfying lunch or dinner.

Note: Grill chicken until cooked through and let rest before slicing to keep it juicy. Serve immediately after adding to salad to maintain freshness.

Swap Feta for Goat Cheese

Try creamy goat cheese instead of feta for a milder, tangy flavor. Goat cheese adds a soft texture that melts slightly into the salad, offering a different but equally delicious taste profile.

Note: Crumble goat cheese gently and add last to avoid breaking it down too much.

Use Mixed Greens Instead of Spinach

Replace baby spinach with a mix of arugula, baby kale, and baby lettuces for varied textures and a peppery bite. This adds complexity and makes the salad more colorful.

Note: Wash and dry all greens thoroughly; adjust dressing quantity as mixed greens can be more delicate.

Add Avocado for Extra Creaminess

Diced avocado adds richness and smooth texture that complements the lemon dressing and strawberries. It also boosts the salad’s nutritional value with healthy fats.

Note: Add avocado last and toss gently to prevent browning and maintain shape.

Make It Vegan by Omitting Cheese

Simply skip the feta and use maple syrup instead of honey in the dressing for a vegan-friendly salad that’s still sweet, tangy, and satisfying.

Note: Check all ingredients to ensure they fit your dietary needs and adjust seasoning to taste.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best served fresh and should not be left out at room temperature for more than 1 hour. Cover loosely with a clean cloth if waiting briefly before serving.

Refrigerator

Store leftover salad without dressing in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Dressing the salad right before eating keeps it fresh. Note that spinach may wilt and strawberries soften when refrigerated with dressing.

Freezer

Not recommended to freeze this salad as fresh greens and strawberries lose texture and become mushy upon thawing.

Reheating

This salad is best served cold or at room temperature. Reheating is not advised as it will wilt the spinach and soften the strawberries.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dressing and toast almonds 1-2 days ahead and store separately. Wash and dry spinach and slice strawberries the day of serving for best freshness. Assemble and dress salad right before eating.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries in this salad?
Frozen strawberries are not ideal because they release excess moisture when thawed, which can make the salad soggy. Fresh strawberries provide the best texture and flavor. If you only have frozen, thaw and drain them well, then pat dry before using.
How long can I store the salad after dressing it?
Once dressed, this salad should be eaten within 30 minutes to an hour for optimal texture and flavor. The dressing causes the spinach to wilt and strawberries to soften quickly, so it’s best to dress just before serving.
Can I substitute the almonds with another nut?
Yes, toasted walnuts or pecans work well as alternatives, providing similar crunch and flavor. Just toast them lightly to bring out their oils. Avoid nuts with very strong flavors that might overpower the salad.
Is this salad suitable for meal prep?
You can prep components in advance, like washing spinach, slicing strawberries, and making dressing separately. However, assemble and dress the salad just before eating to keep it fresh and crisp.
What can I use instead of feta cheese?
Goat cheese or a vegan cheese alternative are great substitutes. If avoiding cheese altogether, omit it and consider adding avocado for creaminess.
How do I keep the spinach from getting soggy?
Dry the spinach thoroughly after washing and add dressing only right before serving. Storing dressed spinach causes it to wilt and become soggy quickly.
Can I add protein to this salad?
Absolutely! Grilled chicken, shrimp, or chickpeas make excellent additions for a more filling meal. Just add them fresh or warm just before serving.
Why is Dijon mustard included in the dressing?
Dijon mustard acts as an emulsifier, helping the oil and lemon juice combine smoothly. It also adds a subtle tang that complements the other flavors.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach, washed and dried
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and thoroughly drying the baby spinach. Using a salad spinner helps remove excess water, which is crucial because wet greens dilute the dressing and make the salad soggy. Once dried, place the spinach in a large salad bowl. This creates the fresh, leafy base of your salad that's light and crisp.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Spinach leaves look fresh, vibrant, and dry to the touch with no water droplets.
Step 2
Hull and slice the strawberries thinly. Thin slices allow the berries to mingle evenly throughout the salad, giving bursts of sweetness in every bite. Avoid overly thick slices as they can overpower the greens and make the salad less balanced.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Strawberries are evenly thin and juicy without being mushy.
Step 3
Toast the sliced almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat until they turn golden brown and smell nutty, about 3-4 minutes. Stir frequently to prevent burning. Toasting enhances their flavor and adds a satisfying crunch that contrasts nicely with the soft spinach and strawberries.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Almonds are golden and fragrant but not burnt or bitter.
Step 4
In a small bowl, whisk together fresh lemon juice, olive oil, honey, and Dijon mustard until fully emulsified. This dressing combines acidity, sweetness, and a slight tang that brightens the entire salad. The emulsification helps the oil and lemon juice blend smoothly, coating the salad evenly.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Dressing is smooth, slightly thickened, and glossy.
Step 5
Season the dressing with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Salt balances the sweetness of the honey and strawberries, while pepper adds a subtle warmth. Taste and adjust seasoning gradually to avoid overpowering the delicate flavors.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Dressing tastes balanced with a mild tang and a hint of sweetness.
Step 6
Add the sliced strawberries and toasted almonds to the bowl with spinach. Gently toss them together to mix the textures and flavors without bruising the berries or crushing the nuts. This layering of texture is what makes the salad interesting to eat.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Salad ingredients are evenly distributed without damaged berries.
Step 7
Pour the lemon dressing over the salad just before serving. Toss gently to coat every leaf and piece evenly. Adding the dressing last keeps the spinach crisp and prevents it from wilting prematurely.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Leaves glisten lightly with dressing but remain crisp and fresh.
Step 8
Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese on top of the salad as a finishing touch. The salty creaminess complements the sweet strawberries and zesty dressing beautifully, creating a layered flavor profile.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Feta is evenly scattered and visible without overwhelming the salad.
Step 9
Serve immediately to enjoy the salad at its freshest. This salad is best eaten right away to savor the crisp spinach and juicy berries before they soften. If making ahead, keep the dressing separate and toss just before eating.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Salad looks vibrant with fresh colors and textures intact.
