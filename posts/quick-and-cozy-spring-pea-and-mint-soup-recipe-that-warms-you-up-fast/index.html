Fresh Pea and Mint Soup That Warms You Inside on Chilly Spring Nights

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This fresh pea and mint soup is super light but still cozy enough for cool evenings. The mint brings a fresh pop that makes it feel lively and bright. It’s perfect when you want something simple and satisfying without the heaviness.

Pro Tips

Use fresh peas if possible for the best flavor and color.
Fresh peas have a sweeter, more vibrant flavor and a better texture than frozen. They also give the soup a bright green color that frozen peas can’t always match. Using fresh peas elevates the freshness and overall appeal of the soup.
Don’t overcook the peas to avoid dull flavors and mushiness.
Overcooking peas breaks down their structure too much, causing loss of vibrant color and fresh taste. It also makes the soup taste starchy or pasty. Simmer gently just until tender for the perfect texture and flavor.
Add the mint just before blending to keep its flavor bright.
Mint’s essential oils are volatile and can become bitter if cooked too long. Adding mint right before blending preserves its fresh, bright flavor and aroma, which is key to the soup’s lively character.
Use an immersion blender for easy, mess-free pureeing.
An immersion blender lets you puree directly in the pot, making the process smoother and quicker. It also allows better control over texture, so you can stop blending when you reach your desired creaminess.
Add lemon juice at the end to balance flavors perfectly.
Lemon juice can lose its brightness if cooked too long. Adding it at the end ensures the soup has a fresh, lively finish that complements the sweetness of peas and the herbaceous mint.
Season gradually and taste frequently to avoid over-salting.
Broth and yogurt add saltiness, so seasoning little by little and tasting helps avoid an overly salty soup. It also allows you to adjust the lemon and pepper for balanced flavor.
Serve with a drizzle of good olive oil for extra richness.
Finishing with olive oil adds a silky texture and subtle fruity notes that enhance the soup’s flavor profile. It also makes the presentation more appetizing and inviting.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding mint too early and cooking it down.

Why it happens Many cooks assume all herbs should be cooked to extract flavor, but mint’s delicate oils burn or turn bitter with heat.

Fix Add fresh mint leaves just before blending or as a garnish to preserve their bright, fresh flavor. Avoid boiling mint in the soup.

Overcooking peas until mushy and dull-colored.

Why it happens Rushing the cooking or leaving peas simmering too long causes loss of texture and vibrant green color.

Fix Simmer peas gently just until tender and bright green, about 10 minutes. Remove promptly to avoid overcooking.

Adding yogurt while the soup is still boiling.

Why it happens High heat causes yogurt to curdle, ruining the soup’s smooth texture.

Fix Remove soup from heat before stirring in yogurt. Warm gently if needed, but do not boil afterward.

Skipping the lemon juice and ending up with a flat-tasting soup.

Why it happens Without acidity, the soup lacks brightness and balance.

Fix Always add fresh lemon juice at the end and adjust to taste for a lively finish.

Not blending the soup enough, leaving it grainy.

Why it happens Incomplete blending leaves pea skins and onion bits that affect texture.

Fix Blend thoroughly until smooth and creamy. Use an immersion blender or high-speed blender.

Variations

Add Creamy Coconut Milk for a Dairy-Free Twist

Swap out Greek yogurt for coconut milk to make this soup vegan and dairy-free. Coconut milk adds a subtle sweetness and creamy texture that pairs beautifully with peas and mint. The soup will have a slightly tropical flavor but remains fresh and comforting.

Note: Add coconut milk after blending and warm gently without boiling to preserve texture.

Spicy Kick with a Touch of Crushed Red Pepper

For those who love a little heat, add a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes when sautéing onions. This adds warmth without overpowering the fresh flavors. It’s a great way to customize the soup for cooler nights or spice lovers.

Note: Add red pepper flakes early with onions to infuse the oil and mellow the spice.

Serve Chilled as a Refreshing Summer Soup

Transform this soup into a cool summer starter by chilling it thoroughly and serving cold with a garnish of fresh mint and a drizzle of olive oil. The texture stays creamy, and the minty brightness is even more pronounced when cold.

Note: Omit the yogurt or blend it in after chilling to avoid separation.

Add Fresh Pea Shoots or Baby Spinach for Extra Greens

Stir in fresh pea shoots or baby spinach leaves just before blending for added nutrients and a deeper green color. This boosts the veggie content without changing the soup’s delicate flavor too much.

Note: Add greens at the end of cooking to preserve their color and nutrients.

Top with Crispy Bacon or Toasted Nuts for Crunch

For a textural contrast, sprinkle crispy bacon bits or toasted pine nuts on top just before serving. This adds a savory crunch that complements the smooth, creamy soup beautifully.

Note: Add toppings last to maintain crunch and prevent sogginess.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The soup can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered to prevent contamination. After this, transfer to the fridge promptly to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftover soup in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The flavors meld nicely over time, though the soup may thicken. Reheat gently on the stove or microwave, adding a splash of broth or water to loosen if needed.

Freezer

Freeze the soup in airtight, freezer-safe containers for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, leave some headspace and wrap containers tightly. Thaw overnight in the fridge and stir well before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on low heat in a saucepan or microwave in short bursts, stirring often. Add a little broth or water if the soup feels too thick. Avoid boiling after adding yogurt to prevent curdling.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the soup base (onions, broth, peas) 1-2 days ahead and refrigerate. Blend and add mint, lemon juice, and yogurt just before serving for the freshest flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peas instead of fresh?
Yes, frozen peas work well and are a great year-round option. Use good quality frozen peas without freezer burn and thaw them before cooking for best texture. The flavor may be slightly less sweet than fresh but still delicious.
How do I make this soup vegan?
To make it vegan, simply omit the Greek yogurt or replace it with coconut milk or a plant-based yogurt. These alternatives add creaminess without dairy, and the soup will still be flavorful and smooth.
Why does my soup taste bitter after adding mint?
Mint can become bitter if cooked too long or added too early. Always add fresh mint just before blending or as a garnish to keep its bright, fresh flavor. Avoid boiling mint leaves in the soup.
Can I prepare this soup in advance?
Absolutely! You can cook the soup base a day or two ahead and refrigerate it. Add mint, lemon juice, and yogurt just before serving to maintain freshness and vibrant flavor.
What if my soup is too thick after blending?
Simply stir in a bit more vegetable broth or water to loosen the consistency to your liking. Adjust seasoning afterward, as thinning can mellow the flavors.
Can I freeze this soup with yogurt added?
It's best not to freeze the soup after adding yogurt, as it may separate and change texture. Freeze the soup base without yogurt and add it fresh when reheating.
How do I prevent the yogurt from curdling when reheating?
Reheat the soup gently over low heat or in short microwave bursts. Avoid boiling once yogurt is added, as high heat causes curdling. Stir frequently and add yogurt off heat if possible.
What can I serve with this soup?
This soup pairs beautifully with crusty bread, a simple green salad, or light sandwiches. Garnish with extra mint leaves or a drizzle of olive oil for a pretty presentation.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 cups, 4 servings

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, extra virgin
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, loosely packed
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt (optional, for creaminess)
  • Juice of half a lemon

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté gently until it becomes soft and translucent, about 5-7 minutes. This slow cooking draws out the natural sweetness from the onion, forming a flavorful base for the soup. Avoid browning the onions here, as that would alter the delicate flavor profile we want. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft, glossy, and translucent without any brown spots.
Step 2
Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for another 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly. Garlic cooks quickly and can burn easily, which would introduce bitterness. Cooking it briefly releases its aroma and mellows its sharpness, enhancing the overall soup flavor without overpowering it.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Garlic is fragrant but not browned or burnt.
Step 3
Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Once boiling, add the peas and reduce the heat to a simmer. Cooking the peas in broth allows them to absorb extra flavor and soften thoroughly. Be careful not to boil too vigorously as it can cause the peas to break apart unevenly.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Peas are bright green and tender to the bite.
Step 4
Simmer the soup uncovered for about 10 minutes until the peas are tender throughout. This step is crucial for achieving that creamy texture after blending. If you skip this or undercook, the peas will remain gritty and the soup won’t be as smooth.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Peas are soft and easily pierced with a fork.
Step 5
Remove the pot from heat and add the fresh mint leaves. Using an immersion blender or transferring to a blender in batches, puree the soup until smooth and creamy. Adding mint before blending lets its flavor infuse throughout the soup evenly. Blend until there are no lumps, but don’t over-blend as it can make the soup gluey.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Soup is smooth, bright green, and velvety in texture.
Step 6
Return the blended soup to low heat and season with salt, black pepper, and lemon juice to taste. The lemon juice adds a subtle brightness that balances the richness of the peas and yogurt. Taste and adjust seasoning carefully here to avoid overpowering the fresh flavors.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Soup tastes balanced with a fresh, lively finish.
Step 7
If using, stir in the Greek yogurt just before serving to add creaminess and a slight tang. Don’t boil the soup after adding yogurt, as high heat can cause it to curdle. The yogurt enriches the mouthfeel and adds extra protein without heaviness.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Soup is creamy and smooth without separation.
Step 8
Serve the soup hot with a drizzle of olive oil or a few mint leaves on top for garnish. This simple finishing touch enhances the presentation and adds a fresh aroma as you dig in. It’s great with crusty bread or a light salad on the side.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Soup looks vibrant and inviting with garnishes.
Step 9
Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge. The flavors actually mellow and deepen after a day, making it even better reheated. When reheating, do so gently to preserve the fresh taste and creamy texture.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Soup reheats evenly without separating or losing vibrancy.
