One-Pot Instant Pot Chicken Stew for Busy Weeknights

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This comforting Instant Pot chicken stew is a lifesaver on hectic days. Tender chicken simmers with simple veggies and herbs, all in one pot. Ready in under an hour—ideal for cozy dinners or meal prep.

Pro Tips

Brown the chicken before pressure cooking.
Browning triggers Maillard reactions, which produce rich, savory flavors and a more appealing color. Skipping this step results in a milder, less complex stew. The crust also helps seal moisture inside the chicken, keeping it juicy during pressure cooking.
Sauté vegetables before adding broth.
Cooking onions, carrots, and celery until soft releases their natural sweetness and builds a flavorful base. This step prevents the stew from tasting raw or one-dimensional. Rushing this step may yield underdeveloped flavors and a less aromatic broth.
Use bone-in chicken thighs if you want extra flavor.
Bones add gelatin and richness to the broth, enhancing mouthfeel and depth. However, cooking time may need to be extended slightly to ensure meat is tender. Removing bones after cooking is easy and worth the flavor boost.
Add delicate ingredients like peas at the end.
Peas cook quickly and retain their bright color and sweetness when added after pressure cooking. Adding them too early results in mushy, dull peas. This preserves texture and freshness, making the stew more visually appealing.
Use natural pressure release when possible.
Allowing the pressure to release naturally helps the stew thicken slightly and settle, improving texture and flavor integration. Quick release can cause splattering or uneven cooking. However, for safety, follow recipe timing carefully.
Adjust seasoning after cooking.
Flavor intensity can diminish or change during pressure cooking. Tasting and adjusting salt or pepper at the end ensures the stew is balanced and flavorful without being over-seasoned.
Don’t overcrowd the Instant Pot.
Overfilling can prevent even cooking and proper pressure buildup. Keeping ingredients within recommended fill lines ensures consistent results and prevents burning or undercooking.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the browning step.

Why it happens Many assume pressure cooking alone is enough to develop flavor and skip browning to save time.

Fix Take the extra few minutes to brown the chicken first. This adds depth and caramelized flavor that pressure cooking alone can’t achieve. Use the 'Sauté' mode and don’t stir too much to let the chicken sear properly.

Adding peas or delicate veggies too early.

Why it happens People often add all ingredients at once for convenience.

Fix Add peas after pressure cooking is complete to keep them vibrant and prevent mushiness. Stir them in and let residual heat warm them through for best texture.

Using chicken breasts instead of thighs.

Why it happens Breasts are leaner and often preferred for health reasons, but they dry out quickly.

Fix Use boneless thighs for juicier meat that holds up well under pressure. If breasts are used, reduce cooking time and watch carefully to avoid drying.

Overfilling the pot beyond max line.

Why it happens Trying to make a larger batch without checking capacity.

Fix Respect the Instant Pot’s max fill line. Overfilling can cause safety issues and inconsistent cooking. Split into batches if needed.

Rushing the sauté step for vegetables.

Why it happens In a hurry, cooks may skip softening veggies before pressure cooking.

Fix Take time to sauté onions and carrots until soft. This develops flavor and sweetness essential to a rich stew.

Variations

Spicy Cajun Chicken Stew

Add 1 teaspoon Cajun seasoning and a pinch of cayenne pepper with the herbs for a spicy twist. Swap smoked paprika for regular paprika if preferred. This changes the flavor profile to bold and smoky with a kick. Expect a slightly redder broth and more heat.

Note: Adjust cayenne carefully to avoid overpowering. The spices will infuse during pressure cooking, so balance is key.

Creamy Coconut Chicken Stew

Stir in 1 cup coconut milk after pressure cooking and before adding peas for a creamy, tropical flavor. Omit smoked paprika and rosemary, and add a bit of ginger and lime zest. This makes the stew richer with a subtle sweetness and exotic aroma.

Note: Add coconut milk off heat to prevent curdling. The texture will be creamier and slightly thinner.

Vegetable-Heavy Chicken Stew

Increase carrots, celery, and potatoes by 50% and add chopped zucchini or bell peppers. This version is packed with extra veggies for more fiber and color. The stew will be chunkier and heartier, perfect for vegetable lovers.

Note: Add softer veggies like zucchini after pressure cooking to avoid mushiness.

Herb-Infused Chicken and Mushroom Stew

Add 8 ounces sliced mushrooms with the veggies and increase thyme to 1.5 teaspoons. Mushrooms add earthiness and a meaty texture, complementing the chicken. The broth becomes richer and more umami-forward.

Note: Sauté mushrooms with onions to release moisture and deepen flavor.

Low-Carb Chicken Stew

Replace baby potatoes with cauliflower florets and omit peas for a lower-carb option. The stew remains filling and flavorful but lighter on carbs. Cauliflower absorbs flavor well and offers a similar texture to potatoes.

Note: Add cauliflower after pressure cooking to maintain texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This stew can safely sit at room temperature covered for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly. Cover tightly with a lid or foil to avoid contamination.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The stew will thicken and flavors deepen overnight. It’s best reheated gently on the stove or microwave with a splash of broth or water to loosen the texture.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container or heavy freezer bag for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible before sealing. Thaw overnight in the fridge for best results before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Add a bit of broth or water if the stew is too thick. Avoid high heat which can toughen the chicken or cause uneven warming. Microwave reheating is also fine; stir midway to distribute heat.

Make Ahead

You can prep all ingredients and brown the chicken a day ahead, then refrigerate everything separately. On the day of serving, assemble and pressure cook fresh. This saves time and keeps textures optimal.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but chicken breasts cook faster and can dry out under pressure. If using breasts, reduce pressure cooking time to about 8 minutes and use the natural release method to avoid overcooking. Thighs are preferred for juiciness and flavor.
What if I don’t have an Instant Pot?
You can make this stew on the stovetop in a heavy pot or Dutch oven by simmering covered for about 45 minutes to 1 hour until chicken and potatoes are tender. Brown chicken and sauté veggies as directed, then add broth and spices. Adjust liquid as needed.
Can I add other vegetables?
Absolutely! Root vegetables like parsnips or turnips work well. Add delicate veggies like zucchini or peas after cooking to avoid mushiness. Mushrooms and bell peppers also make great additions for extra flavor and texture.
How do I thicken the stew if it’s too watery?
After pressure cooking, remove the lid and simmer on 'Sauté' mode to reduce liquid until desired thickness. Alternatively, mix 1 tablespoon cornstarch with 2 tablespoons cold water, then stir into the hot stew and cook until thickened.
Is it okay to use low-sodium broth?
Yes, low-sodium broth gives you control over salt levels and prevents the stew from becoming too salty. You can always add salt at the end to taste, which is safer than over-salting from the start.
Can I double the recipe?
You can double the ingredients but be careful not to exceed the Instant Pot’s max fill line. You might need to cook in batches or use a larger pressure cooker. Cooking times remain similar but check doneness carefully.
Why does my stew sometimes taste bland?
Blunt flavor usually means insufficient seasoning or skipping browning the chicken. Make sure to brown the meat and sauté veggies well. Also, taste and adjust salt and pepper after cooking to brighten flavors.
How do I store leftovers safely?
Cool the stew to room temperature within two hours, then transfer to airtight containers and refrigerate. Consume within 4 days. For longer storage, freeze in sealed containers and thaw in the fridge before reheating.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 1.5 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced
  • 1 cup baby potatoes, halved
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas (added at the end)

How To Do It

Step 1
Set your Instant Pot to 'Sauté' mode and add the olive oil. Once hot, add the chicken chunks in a single layer, making sure not to crowd the pot. Brown the chicken pieces for about 4-5 minutes, turning occasionally until they develop a golden crust. This step is crucial because browning adds depth and complexity to the stew’s flavor through the Maillard reaction. Avoid stirring too much so the chicken can properly sear rather than steam. Once browned, remove the chicken and set aside.
⏱ Time: 6 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown crust on chicken pieces, no pink spots
Step 2
Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery to the pot and sauté until the onions become translucent and soft, about 5 minutes. Stir frequently to prevent burning. This softening releases the vegetables’ natural sweetness and layers the flavor base. If you rush this step, the stew risks tasting raw or flat. Once softened, add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant—don’t let it burn as garlic turns bitter quickly.
⏱ Time: 6 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions translucent, carrots slightly softened, garlic aromatic
Step 3
Return the browned chicken to the pot along with any juices that accumulated. Add the halved baby potatoes on top. This layering helps the potatoes cook evenly without getting mushy. The chicken juices contribute extra flavor to the broth, so don’t discard them.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken and potatoes evenly distributed in pot
Step 4
Sprinkle the dried thyme, rosemary, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper evenly over the ingredients. Stir gently to combine the spices without breaking up the potatoes. These herbs and spices are key to giving the stew its aromatic, comforting profile. Be careful not to over-salt here, as the broth adds saltiness too.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Spices appear evenly distributed and fragrant
Step 5
Pour in the chicken broth, making sure it covers the ingredients but doesn’t exceed the max fill line of your Instant Pot. The broth is the cooking liquid that will tenderize the chicken and veggies while infusing flavor. Using too little broth can cause burning or inconsistent cooking; too much dilutes flavor.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Ingredients mostly submerged, broth level appropriate
Step 6
Seal the Instant Pot lid and set it to 'Pressure Cook' or 'Manual' mode on high pressure for 12 minutes. This timing is perfect for tender chicken thighs and fully cooked vegetables without turning them to mush. If you rush this or set time too low, the chicken might be tough and potatoes undercooked.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes cooking + natural release
✓ Doneness: Pressure reached, timer counts down, natural pressure release completes
Step 7
After cooking, carefully perform a quick release to let out remaining pressure. Open the lid and stir in the frozen peas. The residual heat will warm them through in about 2 minutes without overcooking. Adding peas last keeps their bright color and fresh flavor intact.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peas heated through, vibrant green, stew slightly thickened
Step 8
Taste the stew and adjust seasoning if needed. Sometimes a pinch more salt or pepper can brighten the flavors after cooking. Stir gently to combine. This final step ensures your stew is perfectly seasoned and balanced.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balanced seasoning, no single flavor overpowering
Step 9
Serve the stew hot, ideally with crusty bread or over rice for a complete meal. The stew should be thick but not dry, with tender chicken and vegetables bursting with flavor. Leftovers reheat beautifully, making this a great make-ahead dinner.
⏱ Time: Serve immediately
✓ Doneness: Stew steaming hot, hearty texture, inviting aroma

Final Thoughts

Give this Instant Pot chicken stew a try—you’ll love how simple and satisfying it is. Don’t forget to share your results and any tweaks you make!
