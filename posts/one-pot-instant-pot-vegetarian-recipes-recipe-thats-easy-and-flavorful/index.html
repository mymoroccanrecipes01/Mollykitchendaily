Hearty Vegetarian Instant Pot Stew for Busy Weeknights

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This vegetarian stew is a total lifesaver when you’re short on time but want something comforting. The Instant Pot really amps up the spices and melds flavors perfectly. Great for a cozy dinner any night of the week!

Pro Tips

Always sauté onions and garlic before pressure cooking.
Sautéing develops deep flavor through caramelization and softens the aromatics, which creates a rich base. Skipping this step can leave raw garlic taste and flat, underdeveloped flavors, weakening the stew’s overall taste.
Toast the spices briefly in the pot before adding liquids.
Toasting spices releases essential oils, boosting their aroma and flavor. It transforms ground spices from dull powders into vibrant, fragrant contributors that infuse the stew deeply during pressure cooking.
Use green or brown lentils instead of red lentils.
Green/brown lentils keep their shape under pressure cooking, giving the stew texture. Red lentils tend to become mushy and can make the stew overly thick and pasty, which changes the mouthfeel.
Let the pressure release naturally for at least 10 minutes.
Natural release gently finishes cooking and prevents lentils and potatoes from breaking apart. Quick release too early can cause texture issues and a watery stew, reducing overall quality.
Scrape the bottom of the pot before sealing the lid.
Removing any stuck bits prevents the Instant Pot’s burn warning, which can interrupt cooking. It also ensures flavors aren’t burnt and the stew cooks evenly.
Adjust salt only after pressure cooking is complete.
Salt intensifies during cooking, so seasoning at the end helps avoid over-salting. It allows you to balance flavors perfectly based on the stew’s final concentration.
Garnish with fresh herbs just before serving.
Fresh parsley or cilantro adds brightness and color that contrast the stew’s earthy tones. It lifts the dish visually and flavor-wise, making every bite more vibrant.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sauté step for onions and garlic

Why it happens Sometimes cooks want to save time and throw everything in at once, assuming pressure cooking will fix everything.

Fix Take the extra 5-7 minutes to sauté aromatics first. This step builds essential flavor foundation. Without it, the stew tastes flat and raw. Use the sauté function on your Instant Pot to do this easily.

Using red lentils instead of green or brown

Why it happens Red lentils cook faster and seem convenient, but they break down too much.

Fix Choose green or brown lentils that hold shape under pressure. They provide texture and avoid a mushy stew. Rinse lentils well before adding.

Not toasting spices before pressure cooking

Why it happens It’s easy to overlook this since it’s only a minute or two extra.

Fix Toast spices with onions and garlic until fragrant. This step unlocks flavor compounds that otherwise remain dormant, resulting in a bland dish.

Quick releasing pressure immediately after cooking

Why it happens Impatience or lack of knowledge about natural release benefits.

Fix Let pressure release naturally for at least 10 minutes to let the stew finish cooking gently. Then release remaining pressure quickly. This improves texture and flavor.

Not scraping the bottom of the pot before sealing

Why it happens Rushing or forgetting this step leads to stuck bits.

Fix Stir and scrape the bottom to avoid burn notices and uneven cooking. This ensures the stew doesn’t develop bitter burnt flavors.

Variations

Spicy Kick Vegetarian Stew

Add 1 diced jalapeño or 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes during the sauté step for a spicy twist. The heat balances the earthiness and adds warmth without overwhelming the flavors. Great for those who like a little zing.

Note: Monitor spice levels carefully; adding fresh jalapeño early lets its heat mellow and blend, while flakes add a more direct punch.

Creamy Coconut Lentil Stew

Stir in 1/2 cup coconut milk after pressure cooking for a creamy, tropical flavor. This adds richness and a subtle sweetness that complements the spices beautifully. Perfect for a different texture and flavor profile.

Note: Add coconut milk off heat to prevent curdling and preserve its creamy texture.

Root Vegetable Medley

Replace potatoes with a mix of diced sweet potatoes, parsnips, and turnips for a sweeter, more complex stew. This variation adds layers of flavor and color, making the dish visually appealing and nutritionally diverse.

Note: Adjust cooking time slightly if using denser vegetables to ensure tenderness.

Add Greens for Extra Nutrition

Stir in 2 cups of chopped kale or spinach just before serving. The greens wilt quickly in the hot stew and boost vitamins and color without changing the cooking time.

Note: Add greens after pressure cooking to keep them vibrant and prevent overcooking.

Quinoa Boosted Stew

Add 1/2 cup rinsed quinoa with the lentils for extra protein and a slightly nutty texture. Quinoa cooks well under pressure and makes the stew even heartier.

Note: Keep the total liquid volume the same; quinoa absorbs a lot of liquid during cooking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This stew can safely sit out covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so it’s best to refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store any leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The flavors deepen overnight but the texture may thicken. Reheat gently with a splash of broth or water to loosen it.

Freezer

Freeze the stew in airtight, freezer-safe containers for up to 3 months. Cool completely before freezing to preserve texture. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat on the stovetop or microwave.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over medium heat, stirring occasionally and adding a little broth if too thick. Avoid overheating which can cause lentils to break down too much. Microwave reheating is fine; cover to retain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the base (sautéed veggies and spices) 1-2 days ahead and refrigerate. Add lentils, broth, and potatoes when ready to cook. This saves time and keeps flavors fresh.

FAQ

Can I use red lentils instead of green or brown?
Red lentils cook faster and tend to break down into a mushy texture, which changes the stew’s consistency. If you prefer a smoother stew, red lentils can work, but for a chunkier, heartier dish, stick with green or brown lentils.
What if I don’t have an Instant Pot? Can I make this on the stove?
Yes! Sauté the onions, garlic, and spices in a large pot, then add the remaining ingredients and simmer covered for 40-50 minutes, or until lentils and potatoes are tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
How can I make this stew spicier?
Add fresh diced jalapeño, red pepper flakes, or cayenne pepper during the sauté step. Start with small amounts and adjust to your heat preference. Remember that pressure cooking can mellow some spice intensity.
Can I add other vegetables?
Absolutely! Root vegetables like parsnips, turnips, or sweet potatoes work well. Just cut them into similar-sized pieces and adjust cooking time if needed. Leafy greens are best added after cooking to maintain texture.
How do I prevent the Instant Pot from giving a burn notice?
Make sure to sauté the aromatics properly and scrape any browned bits off the bottom before sealing. Also, ensure there’s enough liquid in the pot. If the burn notice appears, release pressure, stir, and try again.
Can I make this vegan?
Yes! This recipe is naturally vegan as it uses vegetable broth and plant-based ingredients. Just double-check your broth to ensure it’s vegan-friendly.
How long will leftovers keep in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, leftovers remain good for up to 4 days. The flavors actually improve after a day or two, but be sure to reheat thoroughly before eating.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 cups, 4 servings

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, room temperature
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup green lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Set your Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat the olive oil. Add the diced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until it’s soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. This step is crucial because it helps develop a sweet, mellow base flavor that enriches the entire stew. Avoid rushing this part; if the onions brown too quickly or burn, it can give a bitter taste. Keep the heat moderate and stir often so they soften evenly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Onions should be soft, translucent, and slightly golden but not browned or burnt.
Step 2
Add the minced garlic, diced carrots, and celery to the pot. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the garlic is fragrant and the vegetables begin to soften. This short sauté helps release the garlic’s aroma and starts breaking down the veggies for better texture later. Skipping this will make the garlic taste raw and the veggies less tender.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: You’ll smell the garlic’s fragrance and see the veggies soften slightly but still hold shape.
Step 3
Stir in the smoked paprika, ground cumin, turmeric, and dried thyme. Cook the spices with the veggies for about 1 minute, stirring constantly. This brief toasting unlocks the essential oils in the spices, making the stew more aromatic and flavorful. Don’t skip this step or the spices will taste flat and dull.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Spices should smell fragrant and the mixture may darken slightly.
Step 4
Add the cubed potato, rinsed lentils, diced tomatoes with their juice, and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir well to combine all ingredients evenly. This ensures the lentils and potatoes cook uniformly and absorb the broth’s flavor. Make sure you scrape the bottom of the pot to loosen any browned bits — this prevents a burn notice during pressure cooking.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Ingredients are well combined and the bottom of the pot is clear of stuck bits.
Step 5
Secure the lid on the Instant Pot and set the valve to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 15 minutes. Pressure cooking allows the lentils to soften fully and the potatoes to become tender while infusing all the spices deeply into the stew. Don’t rush this step or reduce the time, or your lentils might stay undercooked and the flavors won’t be as developed.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: The Instant Pot timer counts down the full 15 minutes without error.
Step 6
Once cooking is complete, let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes before quick releasing any remaining pressure. Natural release gives the stew time to continue cooking gently and settle, improving texture and flavor. Quick releasing too early can cause lentils to split and the stew to be watery.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Steam stops escaping and the pressure valve drops down safely.
Step 7
Open the lid carefully and stir the stew. Taste and season with salt and pepper as needed. Adjusting seasoning at the end is important because the flavors concentrate during cooking and salt can enhance or balance the dish. If the stew is too thick, add a splash of broth or water and stir to loosen it.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Stew is thick but spoonable, with balanced seasoning and no raw flavors.
Step 8
If you want a creamier texture, use a potato masher or immersion blender to mash some of the potatoes and lentils right in the pot. This adds body and richness without extra ingredients. Be careful not to over-blend — you want some chunkiness for texture.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Stew has a creamy consistency with some visible chunks for texture.
Step 9
Serve the stew hot, garnished with fresh chopped parsley. The fresh herb adds brightness and color, balancing the earthiness of the spices. Pair it with crusty bread or a simple salad for a complete meal. Enjoy immediately for best flavor and texture.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Stew is steaming hot, garnished, and visually inviting.
