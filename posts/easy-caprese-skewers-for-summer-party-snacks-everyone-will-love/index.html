Fresh and Easy Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze for Summer Parties

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 14, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These Caprese skewers are a fresh, bite-sized take on the classic Italian salad. Juicy cherry tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil come together with a sweet balsamic drizzle. Perfect for summer gatherings or quick snacks!

Pro Tips

Use wooden skewers soaked in water for 10 minutes if grilling or serving outdoors.
Soaking wooden skewers prevents them from burning or catching fire, especially if you plan to grill or leave them out in warm weather. It also makes the skewers sturdier and easier to handle, ensuring your Caprese bites stay intact during serving.
Season the mozzarella and tomatoes before assembling the skewers.
Lightly salting and peppering the cheese and tomatoes enhances their natural flavors and brings out sweetness and depth. Skipping this step can result in a blander appetizer that doesn’t showcase the fresh ingredients as well.
Add the balsamic glaze just before serving to keep basil fresh.
Balsamic glaze is acidic and moist, which can wilt basil leaves if applied too early. Waiting to drizzle it right before serving preserves the bright color and aroma of the basil, keeping your skewers looking and tasting their best.
Choose small, uniform-sized cherry tomatoes and mozzarella balls for even bites.
Uniformity in size ensures each skewer is balanced in flavor and texture, making them easier to eat in one or two bites. Larger or uneven pieces can be awkward and throw off the presentation.
Serve the skewers chilled but not straight from the fridge.
Allowing them to sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before serving lets the flavors open up and the mozzarella soften slightly. Serving ice-cold cheese dulls its creamy texture and flavor.
Use a high-quality balsamic glaze or make your own reduction for best flavor.
Cheap or overly sweet store-bought glazes can overpower the delicate balance of the Caprese ingredients. Making your own balsamic reduction ensures a rich, balanced sweetness that complements rather than dominates.
Gently pat dry all ingredients after washing to avoid sogginess.
Excess water on tomatoes, basil, or mozzarella can dilute flavors and make the skewers soggy quickly. Drying them thoroughly helps the glaze stick better and keeps the texture fresh and crisp.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using watery mozzarella without draining

Why it happens Many cooks skip draining fresh mozzarella, assuming it’s ready to use straight from the container. This causes excess moisture that ruins the texture of the skewers.

Fix Drain mozzarella balls in a fine mesh strainer for at least 10 minutes and gently pat dry with paper towels before assembling. This removes excess water, preventing soggy bites and helping the glaze stick.

Applying balsamic glaze too early

Why it happens In a rush to finish, some drizzle the glaze before serving, not realizing it wilts basil and softens cheese.

Fix Wait to add the balsamic glaze just before serving to keep basil leaves fresh and vibrant, preserving the perfect texture and appearance.

Using large or irregularly sized tomatoes and cheese

Why it happens Not paying attention to size can make skewers uneven and awkward to eat.

Fix Choose small, uniform cherry tomatoes and mozzarella balls or cut larger ones into even pieces to create balanced, easy-to-eat skewers.

Not seasoning the ingredients

Why it happens Some think the fresh ingredients don’t need salt or pepper, but this can lead to bland flavor.

Fix Lightly season tomatoes and mozzarella with salt and pepper before assembly to enhance natural flavors and add depth.

Skipping drying of basil leaves

Why it happens Wet basil leaves from washing can cause the skewers to become soggy quickly.

Fix Dry basil leaves gently with a paper towel or salad spinner before assembling to maintain crispness and prevent sogginess.

Variations

Add Prosciutto for a Savory Twist

Wrap a small slice of prosciutto around the mozzarella ball before skewering for a salty, savory addition. This adds a nice protein element and deepens the flavor profile while keeping the fresh balance intact.

Note: Because prosciutto is delicate, assemble skewers gently to avoid tearing. Serve immediately to prevent drying out.

Use Pesto Instead of Basil Leaves

Swap fresh basil leaves for dollops of homemade or store-bought pesto between tomato and mozzarella. This variation brings a richer herb flavor and creamy texture that pairs beautifully with the balsamic glaze.

Note: Apply pesto sparingly to avoid making skewers messy. Use a small spoon or piping bag for precise application.

Make Mini Caprese Salad Cups

Instead of skewers, layer chopped tomatoes, mozzarella cubes, and torn basil in small serving cups, drizzled with balsamic glaze. This is a fun, mess-free way to serve Caprese at larger parties.

Note: Chop ingredients uniformly to keep texture consistent. Add balsamic glaze just before serving.

Spicy Caprese Skewers with Chili Flakes

Sprinkle a pinch of red chili flakes over the skewers before serving for a subtle heat that contrasts nicely with the creamy cheese and sweet glaze. Great if you like a little kick!

Note: Add chili flakes sparingly and toss gently to distribute evenly without overpowering.

Vegan Caprese Skewers with Plant-Based Cheese

Replace mozzarella with your favorite vegan cheese balls or marinated tofu cubes. The balsamic glaze and fresh basil still shine, making this a delicious dairy-free option.

Note: Choose firm vegan cheese that holds shape well on skewers and season lightly for best flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Caprese skewers can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to prevent drying. Beyond that, freshness and safety decline due to the cheese.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. The basil may wilt and mozzarella firm up when cold, so bring skewers to room temperature before serving.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as fresh mozzarella and basil lose texture and flavor. Best enjoyed fresh.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled or at room temperature for best flavor and texture. Avoid microwaving as it can melt the cheese and wilt the basil.

Make Ahead

You can wash, dry, and prep all ingredients a day ahead, storing them separately in airtight containers. Assemble skewers and add balsamic glaze just before serving for maximum freshness.

FAQ

Can I use regular mozzarella instead of fresh mozzarella balls?
Regular shredded mozzarella won’t work as well because it lacks moisture and creaminess. Fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini) provide the soft texture and mild flavor essential for authentic Caprese. If fresh balls aren’t available, try cutting a fresh mozzarella block into small cubes and draining excess liquid.
How do I make my own balsamic glaze at home?
To make balsamic glaze, simmer 1 cup of balsamic vinegar in a small saucepan over medium heat until it reduces by half and becomes syrupy, about 15-20 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent burning. Let it cool before drizzling over skewers for a rich, homemade flavor.
Can I prepare the skewers a day ahead?
It’s best to assemble the skewers the day of serving to keep basil fresh and prevent sogginess. You can prep and store washed tomatoes, drained mozzarella, and basil separately in airtight containers overnight. Add the balsamic glaze just before serving.
What if I don’t have balsamic glaze?
You can use balsamic vinegar as a substitute but drizzle sparingly since it’s thinner and more acidic. Another option is to mix balsamic vinegar with a little honey or sugar and reduce it slightly to thicken. This mimics the glaze’s sweetness and viscosity.
How do I keep basil from wilting on the skewers?
Make sure basil leaves are completely dry before assembling. Add balsamic glaze only right before serving, and keep skewers refrigerated if not serving immediately. Avoid handling basil too much to prevent bruising.
Can I add other ingredients to the skewers?
Absolutely! You can add olives, small cucumber slices, or prosciutto for variety. Just keep the flavors balanced so they don’t overpower the classic Caprese components.
What’s the best way to serve these skewers at a party?
Arrange them on a large platter with the balsamic glaze drizzled right before guests arrive. Provide small plates and napkins nearby for easy grabbing. They’re perfect finger foods that don’t require utensils.
Are these skewers suitable for kids?
Yes, kids usually love the colorful, bite-sized pieces. Just omit pepper or any spicy additions if serving to picky eaters. The simple flavors of tomato, mozzarella, and basil are generally kid-friendly.
Can I substitute fresh basil with other herbs?
Fresh basil is traditional and gives the signature flavor, but you can try fresh mint or oregano for a different twist. Keep in mind the flavor profile will change, so use sparingly.
How long do Caprese skewers last once assembled?
Once assembled, they’re best eaten within 2 hours at room temperature or stored in the fridge and eaten within 24 hours. Basil wilts and mozzarella texture changes if kept too long.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 skewers, 4 servings

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, washed and dried
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • Fresh basil leaves, about 24 leaves
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze
  • 24 small wooden skewers or toothpicks
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by rinsing the cherry tomatoes under cool water and patting them dry with a clean kitchen towel. This step is important because any excess moisture can make the skewers soggy and dilute the flavors. Choose firm, ripe tomatoes that are shiny and free of blemishes. If you want, you can slice the tomatoes in half for smaller bites, but whole tomatoes work perfectly as well. Drying them thoroughly helps the balsamic glaze stick better later on.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tomatoes should be clean, dry, and firm to the touch.
Step 2
Drain the fresh mozzarella balls in a fine mesh strainer to remove any excess liquid. This is key because watery mozzarella can make the skewers soggy and watery. If the balls are large, you can cut them in half to match the size of the cherry tomatoes. Season the mozzarella lightly with a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper to enhance the creamy flavor. This seasoning step might seem small, but it really builds depth in the final bite.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mozzarella should be dry to the touch and lightly seasoned.
Step 3
Lay out your basil leaves and gently rinse them if needed, then pat dry carefully. Fresh basil is delicate, so handle it gently to avoid bruising. Using fresh, vibrant leaves is essential because they provide that unmistakable aromatic flavor that makes Caprese so special. If the leaves are large, you can tear them in half to fit the skewers better. Make sure they’re dry so the glaze won’t slide off later.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Basil leaves should be fresh, bright green, and dry.
Step 4
Now, assemble the skewers by threading one cherry tomato, followed by a basil leaf, then a mozzarella ball, and finishing with another basil leaf or tomato depending on your preference. The order is flexible, but alternating colors and textures makes them visually appealing and balanced in flavor. Use small wooden skewers or toothpicks for easy handling. Don’t pack the ingredients too tightly; a little space helps each bite stay fresh and easy to eat.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers should be colorful, balanced, and easy to hold.
Step 5
Arrange the skewers on a serving platter in a single layer. This prevents them from squishing each other and keeps the basil leaves looking fresh and vibrant. Presentation matters here—seeing those beautiful reds, whites, and greens makes people excited to dig in. If you’re not serving immediately, cover the platter loosely with plastic wrap to keep everything fresh without wilting the basil.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers neatly arranged with no crushed ingredients.
Step 6
Drizzle the balsamic glaze generously over the skewers right before serving. The glaze adds a sweet, tangy finish that complements the creamy mozzarella and juicy tomatoes perfectly. If your glaze is too thick, you can thin it slightly with a few drops of water to make it easier to drizzle evenly. Avoid applying the glaze too early as it can make the basil wilt and the mozzarella soggy.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: A glossy, even coating of balsamic glaze on each skewer.
Step 7
Sprinkle a little extra freshly ground black pepper over the top for a subtle kick. This final seasoning enhances the overall flavor without overpowering the fresh ingredients. You can also add a pinch of flaky sea salt if you like a bit more texture and saltiness. Taste one skewer and adjust the seasoning if needed before serving the whole batch.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Balanced seasoning that highlights all flavors.
Step 8
Serve the Caprese skewers immediately to enjoy the fresh, vibrant flavors at their best. These are perfect for summer parties, picnics, or as a light appetizer before dinner. If you need to store them, keep them covered in the fridge and add the balsamic glaze right before serving to keep everything fresh and crisp.
⏱ Time: Serve immediately
✓ Doneness: Skewers look fresh with no wilted basil or soggy cheese.
Step 9
Clean up your prep area and consider making extra skewers for leftovers—they’re even great cold the next day! Just keep them tightly covered and add fresh balsamic glaze before serving again. These skewers are so simple, but they always impress and bring bright, fresh flavors to the table.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Prep area clean and skewers stored properly if not eaten.
