Fudgy Five-Ingredient Brownies: Rich, Chewy, and So Simple

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 14, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
9 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These brownies are pure chocolate bliss with just five ingredients and 30 minutes of your time. Super fudgy and chewy, they’re perfect for a quick dessert fix or sharing at casual gatherings.

Pro Tips

Let the melted butter cool slightly before mixing with eggs.
If the butter is too hot, it can cook the eggs when added, causing the batter to curdle and resulting in an uneven texture. Cooling to warm temperature ensures a smooth, cohesive batter that bakes evenly.
Don’t overmix the batter once the flour is added.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which toughens the brownies and makes them cakey instead of fudgy. Gentle folding just until combined keeps the crumb tender and moist.
Use parchment paper to line your pan.
Parchment makes it easier to remove the brownies cleanly without sticking or breaking. It also helps achieve neat edges and simplifies cleanup.
Check brownies a few minutes before the minimum baking time.
Ovens vary in heat, and pulling brownies out at the right moment ensures a moist, fudgy center. Use a toothpick test and look for moist crumbs rather than wet batter.
Cool brownies completely before slicing.
Cutting warm brownies causes them to crumble or stick. Cooling allows them to set and firm up, resulting in clean, neat squares.
Store brownies at room temperature in an airtight container.
This preserves moisture and keeps the brownies soft for several days without refrigeration, which can dry them out.
For extra fudginess, slightly underbake the brownies.
Removing them when the toothpick shows some moist crumbs but no raw batter results in a denser, chewier texture that many chocolate lovers prefer.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using hot melted butter directly with eggs.

Why it happens People often melt butter in the microwave and add eggs immediately without cooling, causing the eggs to cook and curdle the batter.

Fix Allow the melted butter to cool to warm temperature before whisking in eggs. This prevents curdling and ensures a smooth, even batter.

Overmixing the batter after adding flour.

Why it happens In a rush or trying to fully combine, many stir vigorously, which develops gluten and toughens the brownies.

Fix Use a spatula to fold gently just until no streaks of flour remain. Stop mixing immediately once combined.

Skipping the parchment paper or greasing poorly.

Why it happens Sometimes people skip lining the pan, resulting in brownies sticking and breaking apart when removed.

Fix Always line with parchment or grease thoroughly to ensure easy removal and clean edges.

Baking too long and drying out the brownies.

Why it happens Following the maximum time blindly can overbake, especially in hotter ovens or smaller pans.

Fix Start checking 5 minutes before time is up using the toothpick test. Pull out when moist crumbs remain.

Cutting brownies before they cool.

Why it happens Impatience leads to cutting warm brownies, causing them to crumble or stick.

Fix Let them cool completely on a wire rack before slicing for clean, neat squares.

Variations

Nutty Brownies

Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor contrast. It’s a simple way to elevate the classic fudgy brownie and add some delightful bite.

Note: Fold nuts in gently after mixing wet and dry ingredients. The nuts won’t affect baking time but watch for even distribution.

Espresso-Infused Brownies

Stir in 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder with the cocoa powder to deepen the chocolate flavor with a subtle coffee kick. Great for grown-up palates and chocolate lovers alike.

Note: Dissolve espresso powder in a teaspoon of hot water before adding to dry ingredients for even flavor distribution.

Gluten-Free Brownies

Replace all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking blend. The texture will be slightly different but still fudgy and rich, making the recipe accessible for gluten-sensitive eaters.

Note: Check your gluten-free blend for xanthan gum; if absent, add 1/4 teaspoon to help with structure.

Vegan Brownies

Swap eggs for flax eggs (1 tablespoon flaxseed meal + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use coconut oil instead of butter. These brownies stay fudgy but take on a slightly different flavor profile.

Note: Let flax eggs sit for 5 minutes before mixing to thicken properly; baking time may increase by a few minutes.

Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies

Drop spoonfuls of creamy peanut butter on top of the batter and swirl gently with a knife before baking. This adds a rich, nutty layer that pairs perfectly with chocolate.

Note: Swirl just before baking to keep the peanut butter distinct and prevent it from blending fully into the batter.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Brownies keep well at room temperature for up to 3 days if stored in an airtight container away from heat and direct sunlight. This keeps them moist and chewy without drying out.

Refrigerator

Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. They may firm up and lose some softness but warming briefly before serving can help restore texture.

Freezer

Freeze brownies wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer bag for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave at room temperature for several hours or overnight in the fridge to avoid condensation ruining texture.

Reheating

Warm brownies gently in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for about 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or butter before reheating helps restore moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh the next day for best texture. Alternatively, bake brownies and store them wrapped at room temperature or frozen to enjoy later.

FAQ

Can I use Dutch-processed cocoa powder instead of natural cocoa?
Dutch-processed cocoa is treated to reduce acidity, which changes how it reacts with leavening agents. Since this recipe doesn’t use baking soda or powder, you can substitute it, but flavor and texture might be slightly different—less sharp and potentially less fudgy.
What if I don’t have parchment paper?
You can grease the pan thoroughly with butter or non-stick spray and lightly dust with flour to prevent sticking. However, parchment makes removal and cleanup much easier, so it’s worth having on hand if possible.
Why are my brownies cakey instead of fudgy?
Overmixing the batter after adding flour or baking too long usually causes cakey brownies. To keep them fudgy, fold ingredients gently and watch baking time closely, pulling them out when the toothpick has moist crumbs.
Can I substitute the sugar with brown sugar?
Brown sugar adds moisture and a slight caramel flavor, which will make the brownies chewier but less crackly on top. It’s a delicious variation but expect a different texture and taste.
How do I store leftover brownies to keep them fresh?
Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, refrigerate or freeze them wrapped tightly. Avoid leaving them uncovered to prevent drying out.
Can I add chocolate chips or nuts to this recipe?
Absolutely! Fold in 1/2 cup of chocolate chips or chopped nuts after mixing the batter for extra texture and flavor. This doesn’t affect baking time much but adds a nice twist.
Why did my brownies crack on top?
A crackly top is a sign of properly dissolved sugar and the right baking temperature. It’s a hallmark of classic fudgy brownies and nothing to worry about—actually, it’s a good thing!
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, substitute all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking blend. Make sure the blend contains xanthan gum or add a small amount to help maintain structure and texture.
What’s the best way to cut brownies for neat squares?
Allow brownies to cool completely, then use a sharp knife wiped clean between cuts. Using a warm knife dipped in hot water and dried can also help achieve cleaner edges.
Can I double the recipe for a larger batch?
Yes, you can double the ingredients and bake in a larger pan, adjusting baking time accordingly. Keep an eye on the brownies and test with a toothpick starting at 30 minutes.

What You'll Need

9 brownies, approximately 3x3 inch squares

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8x8-inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper. This step ensures your brownies won’t stick and helps them bake evenly. Using parchment paper also makes it super easy to lift the brownies out later, which is great for clean slices. Don’t skip this prep because it impacts the texture and ease of serving.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is fully greased or parchment is snugly fitted with no wrinkles
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the melted butter and sugar until smooth and glossy. This step is key because the melted butter helps dissolve the sugar, creating a silky base that contributes to the fudgy texture. If the butter is too hot, it can cook the eggs later, so let it cool slightly before mixing in the eggs.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, slightly shiny, and sugar granules are mostly dissolved
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition to fully incorporate them. Then stir in the vanilla extract. This gradual mixing helps build structure in the batter and prevents it from curdling. The vanilla adds a subtle warmth that rounds out the chocolate flavor beautifully.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is thick, smooth, and shiny with no streaks of egg visible
Step 4
Sift together the cocoa powder, flour, and salt in a separate bowl. Sifting removes lumps in the cocoa and flour, which keeps your brownies smooth and prevents clumps. The salt balances the sweetness and enhances the chocolate flavor. Don’t skip sifting or you might end up with grainy spots.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are light, airy, and lump-free
Step 5
Gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture using a spatula. Fold just until combined—overmixing can develop the gluten in the flour and make the brownies tough. You want a thick, glossy batter with no visible flour streaks but still soft and fudgy.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is uniform in color and texture, thick but smooth
Step 6
Pour the batter into your prepared pan and smooth the top evenly with a spatula. This ensures even baking and a nice flat top for cutting. Give the pan a gentle tap on the counter to release any air bubbles trapped inside the batter.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Surface is smooth and even with no large air pockets
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 25–30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center. You want it to come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. This timing is crucial to get that perfect fudgy texture—too long and they dry out, too short and they’re gooey.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges pull away slightly from the pan and toothpick has moist crumbs
Step 8
Let the brownies cool completely in the pan set on a wire rack. Cooling lets the brownies set up so they cut cleanly and hold their fudgy texture. If you try to cut them warm, they’ll be gooey and messy. Patience here pays off.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies are firm to touch and no longer warm
Step 9
Use the parchment paper to lift the brownies out of the pan and cut into squares with a sharp knife. Wiping the knife clean between cuts helps keep the edges neat. Serve as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean, even squares with a crackly top and moist interior

Final Thoughts
