No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups That Are Light, Creamy & Ready Fast

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 20, 2026
15 min Prep Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These no bake strawberry cheesecake cups are delightfully creamy and fresh, made with a simple whipped cream and cream cheese mix. They come together quickly, making them perfect for summer or when guests drop by unexpectedly.

Pro Tips

Make sure your cream cheese is fully softened before mixing.
Softened cream cheese blends smoothly without lumps, which is essential for a silky filling. If it’s too cold, you’ll end up with clumps that ruin the texture and appearance, making the dessert less enjoyable.
Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold its structure better. Warm bowls or beaters slow down whipping and can cause the cream to become runny or not reach soft peaks.
Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture.
Folding preserves the airiness of the whipped cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring vigorously will deflate the whipped cream, resulting in a dense and heavy texture.
Press graham cracker crumbs lightly when layering the base.
Pressing too hard compacts the crumbs, making the base hard and difficult to eat. Light pressing keeps the crust crumbly but stable, providing a pleasant texture contrast.
Use fresh, ripe strawberries and dice evenly.
Fresh strawberries add natural sweetness and moisture without being soggy. Even dicing ensures consistent texture and an attractive layered look in the cups.
Let the assembled cups chill for at least 2 hours before serving.
Chilling allows the filling to set and flavors to meld, making the dessert easier to serve and more flavorful. Serving immediately can result in a runny filling that lacks structure.
Avoid over-whipping the heavy cream to stiff peaks.
Over-whipping causes the cream to separate into butter and buttermilk, ruining the light texture. Soft peaks hold air but still fold easily, perfect for mixing with cream cheese.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese without softening

Why it happens Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but it’s too firm and clumpy.

Fix Let cream cheese sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes before mixing. If short on time, microwave in 5-second bursts, stirring in between, until soft but not melted.

Over-whipping the heavy cream

Why it happens It’s easy to get carried away whipping cream, thinking stiffer is better.

Fix Watch the cream closely and stop whipping once soft peaks form. The cream should gently hold shape but still fold easily.

Stirring instead of folding the whipped cream into cream cheese

Why it happens Impatience or unfamiliarity with folding technique leads to vigorous mixing.

Fix Use a spatula and fold gently by cutting through the center and turning the bowl. This keeps the mixture airy and light.

Not chilling the assembled cups long enough

Why it happens Rushing to serve results in a runny filling that doesn’t hold shape.

Fix Plan ahead and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to let the filling firm up and flavors blend.

Using overripe or watery strawberries

Why it happens Buying berries without checking ripeness or moisture content.

Fix Choose firm, ripe berries and pat them dry before layering to prevent watery filling.

Variations

Blueberry No Bake Cheesecake Cups

Swap strawberries for fresh blueberries or a mix of berries. Blueberries add a slightly different sweetness and a lovely purple hue. You can also swirl in some blueberry jam for extra flavor.

Note: Blueberries can release more juice, so be sure to pat them dry to avoid soggy layers.

Lemon Zest No Bake Cheesecake Cups

Add 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon zest and 1 teaspoon lemon juice to the cream cheese mixture for a bright, citrusy twist. This variation is refreshing and perfect for spring or summer.

Note: Adjust sugar slightly if you like it sweeter to balance the tartness.

Chocolate No Bake Cheesecake Cups

Mix 1/3 cup melted and cooled dark chocolate into the cream cheese filling before folding in whipped cream. Top with chocolate shavings or cocoa powder for a rich, decadent dessert.

Note: Make sure chocolate is fully cooled to avoid melting the whipped cream.

Vegan No Bake Cheesecake Cups

Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped until fluffy. Sweeten with maple syrup and add vanilla extract. Use vegan graham crackers for the crust. The texture is slightly different but still deliciously creamy.

Note: Coconut cream needs to be chilled overnight and scooped carefully to whip properly.

Peach and Ginger No Bake Cheesecake Cups

Replace strawberries with diced fresh peaches and add a pinch of ground ginger to the cream cheese mixture. This combo adds a warm, spicy note that pairs wonderfully with the creamy filling.

Note: Peaches can be juicy; pat dry to maintain filling consistency.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These cups can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered to protect from dust and insects. Beyond that, the dairy filling risks spoilage, so refrigeration is best.

Refrigerator

Store in airtight containers or cover cups tightly with plastic wrap. They keep well for up to 3 days. The texture remains creamy but may firm slightly. Serve chilled or let sit 10 minutes at room temp before eating for softer texture.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as the delicate whipped cream texture can break down and strawberries become mushy after thawing. If freezing, wrap cups tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil, and consume within 1 month.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled straight from the fridge. If too firm, leave at room temperature 10-15 minutes before serving for best texture.

Make Ahead

Prepare the filling and crust layers 1 day ahead, assemble cups, and refrigerate overnight. This allows flavors to fully develop and saves time on serving day. Add fresh strawberries on top just before serving for freshness.

FAQ

Can I use low-fat cream cheese in this recipe?
You can, but the texture and flavor won’t be as rich or creamy. Low-fat cream cheese contains more water, which can make the filling less stable and sometimes watery. For best results, stick to full-fat cream cheese.
How long can I store these cheesecake cups in the fridge?
They keep well for up to 3 days in the refrigerator if stored in airtight containers. After that, the filling may start to lose its texture and the strawberries can become soggy.
Can I substitute the graham crackers with something else?
Yes, digestive biscuits, vanilla wafers, or even crushed cookies like shortbread work well. Just keep in mind the flavor will change slightly, and the texture might vary depending on the crumb size.
Is it okay to use frozen strawberries?
Fresh strawberries are best for texture and flavor. Frozen berries tend to release more water when thawed, which can make the filling watery. If you must use frozen, thaw and drain them well, then pat dry before adding.
Why is my filling runny after mixing?
This usually happens if the cream cheese was too soft or melted, or if the whipped cream was overmixed and deflated. Ensure cream cheese is softened but not warm, and fold whipped cream gently to keep the airiness.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes! Use vegan cream cheese and chilled coconut cream whipped until fluffy, along with vegan-friendly graham crackers. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty and creamy.
How can I make the cheesecake cups more stable for transporting?
Chill them thoroughly for at least 3 hours or overnight. You can also add a teaspoon of unflavored gelatin dissolved in warm water to the cream cheese mixture to help the filling set firmer.
Can I add other fruits besides strawberries?
Absolutely! Blueberries, raspberries, peaches, or mangoes work well. Just make sure to dice fruits evenly and avoid overly juicy varieties to maintain the filling’s texture.

What You'll Need

6 individual dessert cups

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, diced
  • 6 graham cracker sheets, crushed

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by beating the softened cream cheese in a large bowl until smooth and creamy. This step is important because it removes lumps and ensures a silky base for your cheesecake cups. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed and scrape down the sides occasionally. If you rush this or don’t soften the cream cheese enough, you’ll end up with a grainy texture rather than a smooth one.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The cream cheese should be completely smooth and lump-free.
Step 2
In a separate chilled bowl, whip the cold heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until soft peaks form. Whipping the cream to soft peaks means it holds its shape but still folds easily, which is crucial for the light, airy texture of the cheesecake filling. Over-whipping can turn it grainy or even into butter, so watch closely.
⏱ Time: 4-6 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soft peaks that gently hold shape but fold when the whisk is lifted.
Step 3
Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. The folding technique is key here—you want to combine them without deflating all the air you just whipped into the cream. Use a gentle motion, turning the bowl as you fold until just combined. Overmixing will make the filling dense and heavy, losing that light texture.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: No visible streaks of cream or cream cheese; mixture is smooth and airy.
Step 4
Crush the graham crackers into small crumbs. You can do this by placing them in a zip-top bag and using a rolling pin or pulsing in a food processor. The crumbs form the crunchy base and add texture contrast to the creamy filling. Try not to pulverize them into dust; a bit of chunkiness is nice.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly crushed crumbs, mostly fine with some small chunks.
Step 5
Dice the fresh strawberries into small pieces. Fresh fruit is essential here to keep the cups bright and juicy. If your strawberries are very juicy, gently pat them dry with paper towels to avoid making the filling watery.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Uniform small chunks of strawberries without excess moisture.
Step 6
Assemble the cups by spooning a layer of graham cracker crumbs into the bottom of each serving glass or jar. This base gives a nice crunch and classic cheesecake flavor. Press lightly with the back of the spoon to create a compact layer but don’t compress too hard or it will lose texture.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even, compacted crumb layer about 1/2 inch thick.
Step 7
Add a generous layer of the cream cheese filling on top of the crumbs, filling about half the glass. Use a spoon or piping bag for a neat presentation. The filling should be thick and hold shape but still look soft and fluffy.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth, fluffy layer filling half the cup without sagging.
Step 8
Sprinkle a layer of diced strawberries over the cream layer. This adds freshness and a pop of color. If you like, add a little more graham cracker crumbs on top for extra crunch. The layering is what makes these cups visually appealing and delicious.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly distributed strawberry pieces covering the cream layer.
Step 9
Repeat layering with remaining graham cracker crumbs, cream filling, and strawberries until the cups are filled. Finish with a few strawberry pieces on top for garnish. Then chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours to let the flavors meld and the filling firm up slightly.
⏱ Time: 2 hours chilling
✓ Doneness: Filling is set but still creamy; flavors blended and fresh.
