Cozy Loaded Baked Potato Casserole with Cheddar and Sour Cream

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 25, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This baked potato casserole is everything you love about a loaded spud but in a creamy, cheesy bake. The secret sour cream adds richness that makes it perfect for cozy family dinners or sharing at potlucks.

Pro Tips

Don’t overcook the potatoes; aim for fork-tender but not mushy.
Overcooked potatoes absorb too much moisture and break down completely, resulting in a watery, gluey casserole. Cooking them just until tender keeps some texture, which balances creaminess and structure, ensuring a perfect bite every time.
Use freshly shredded cheddar instead of pre-shredded.
Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can prevent smooth melting and cause a grainy texture. Freshly shredded cheese melts evenly and creates those luscious gooey pockets that make the casserole irresistible.
Let the casserole rest after baking before serving.
Resting allows the hot ingredients to settle and thicken slightly, which makes the casserole easier to slice and serve. It also lets flavors meld fully, enhancing the overall taste experience.
Grease the baking dish well with butter or spray.
A well-greased dish prevents sticking and helps develop a beautifully browned crust on the edges. This crust adds texture contrast, which elevates the dish from simple to spectacular.
Add green onions both inside and on top for flavor and color.
Stirring some green onions into the mix infuses flavor evenly, while sprinkling some on top before baking adds freshness and a vibrant pop of green that makes the casserole look as good as it tastes.
Use full-fat sour cream for best creaminess and flavor.
Lower-fat sour cream can make the casserole watery and less rich. Full-fat versions add silkiness and depth, ensuring the casserole stays creamy and indulgent without being heavy.
Bake at 375°F for even cooking and perfect browning.
This moderate temperature allows the casserole to heat through evenly while giving the cheese on top time to melt and brown without burning. Too high heat risks drying out the potatoes or scorching the cheese.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using waxy potatoes instead of starchy ones

Why it happens People often grab any potato on hand, not realizing different types affect texture drastically. Waxy potatoes hold shape too well, resulting in a dense casserole.

Fix Always choose russet or other starchy potatoes. They break down nicely during cooking, creating the creamy texture that defines this casserole.

Overcooking potatoes until mushy

Why it happens It’s tempting to cook potatoes until super soft to speed up mashing, but this leads to a watery, gluey casserole.

Fix Cook potatoes just until fork-tender but still firm. This ensures the final dish is creamy yet structured, not soupy.

Skipping the resting time after baking

Why it happens In excitement, some serve immediately, causing the casserole to be too loose and runny.

Fix Let the casserole rest 5-10 minutes off heat. This allows it to thicken and makes serving cleaner and easier.

Using pre-shredded cheese

Why it happens For convenience, pre-shredded cheese is often used but it doesn’t melt as well due to additives.

Fix Shred your own cheese fresh. It melts smoother and creates gooey, delicious pockets inside the casserole.

Not seasoning the potato mixture properly

Why it happens Some assume cheese adds enough salt, neglecting to season the potatoes themselves.

Fix Season the potato mixture with salt, pepper, and garlic powder before adding cheese. Taste and adjust so flavors are balanced.

Variations

Jalapeño Cheddar Potato Casserole

Add diced jalapeños to the potato mixture for a spicy kick. Swap regular cheddar with pepper jack cheese for extra heat. This version gives the casserole a lively, bold flavor perfect for spice lovers.

Note: Adjust seasoning carefully and consider reducing salt slightly due to the added cheese’s saltiness. Bake as usual.

Bacon and Mushroom Loaded Potato Casserole

Stir in sautéed mushrooms along with crumbled bacon for an earthy, savory boost. The mushrooms add moisture and umami, complementing the bacon’s smokiness beautifully.

Note: Sauté mushrooms until browned and moisture evaporates before adding to avoid a soggy casserole.

Vegetarian Spinach and Cheese Potato Casserole

Skip the bacon and fold in fresh spinach or kale for a veggie-friendly version. Use extra cheese or a blend with mozzarella to keep it rich and melty.

Note: Wilt spinach before mixing to reduce moisture and prevent watery casserole.

Smoked Gouda and Chive Potato Casserole

Swap cheddar for smoked gouda and replace green onions with fresh chives. This variation offers a smoky, creamy flavor with a delicate onion aroma, making the casserole feel gourmet.

Note: Smoked gouda melts well but bakes a little softer, so watch baking time to avoid overbaking.

Loaded Sweet Potato Casserole

Use peeled, diced sweet potatoes instead of russets for a sweeter, nutrient-rich dish. Pair with sharp cheddar and a pinch of smoked paprika for a unique twist on the classic casserole.

Note: Sweet potatoes cook faster; reduce boiling time slightly and check tenderness early to avoid mushiness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This casserole can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, it's best to refrigerate to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The casserole will thicken and flavors deepen but may lose some crispness. Reheat gently in the oven or microwave.

Freezer

Freeze in a tightly sealed container or wrapped well in foil and plastic wrap for up to 3 months. To thaw, move to the fridge overnight and reheat slowly to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes covered with foil to prevent drying. Add a splash of milk or sour cream if it seems dry. Avoid high heat microwaving which can make it rubbery.

Make Ahead

Prepare the potato mixture and assemble the casserole up to a day ahead. Keep covered and refrigerated, then bake fresh before serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen potatoes for this casserole?
Frozen diced potatoes can be used but they often release extra moisture, which may make the casserole watery. If using frozen, thaw and drain them well, then pat dry before mixing to maintain the right texture.
What can I substitute for sour cream if I don’t have any?
Greek yogurt is a great substitute for sour cream, offering similar creaminess and tang. Use the same amount, but be aware it may make the casserole slightly less rich. Avoid using milk alone as it lacks the thickness and flavor.
How do I make this casserole dairy-free?
Replace butter with a dairy-free margarine or oil, use a plant-based sour cream alternative, and swap cheddar for a vegan cheese that melts well. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty.
Can I make this casserole ahead and freeze it?
Yes! Assemble the casserole but don’t bake it. Wrap tightly and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and bake as directed, adding a bit more baking time if needed.
Why is my casserole watery after baking?
This usually happens if the potatoes were overcooked or if too much liquid was added. Also, not draining cooked potatoes well or adding watery ingredients like fresh vegetables without cooking first can cause excess moisture.
Can I add other vegetables to this recipe?
Absolutely! Try mixing in sautéed mushrooms, spinach, or roasted peppers. Just cook any vegetables that release moisture beforehand to avoid a soggy casserole.
Why isn’t my cheese melting evenly?
Using pre-shredded cheese or very cold cheese can cause uneven melting due to anti-caking agents or temperature. Shred cheese fresh and let it come to room temperature before mixing for best melt.
How do I get a crispy top on my casserole?
Make sure to sprinkle a generous layer of cheese on top and bake uncovered at 375°F. For extra crispness, broil for the last 1-2 minutes but watch closely to prevent burning.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 cups, 6 servings

  • 6 large russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup sour cream, full fat
  • 2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup milk, whole
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup green onions, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 6 slices cooked bacon, crumbled (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by peeling and dicing the russet potatoes into roughly one-inch cubes. Place them in a large pot and cover with cold water to prevent browning. Bring the water to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium and simmer until the potatoes are just tender when pierced with a fork, about 10-12 minutes. Be careful not to overcook; you want the potatoes soft but still holding their shape to avoid a mushy casserole later. Drain thoroughly and set aside to cool slightly.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Potatoes are fork-tender but not falling apart
Step 2
While the potatoes cook, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or non-stick spray. This prevents sticking and helps the casserole edges brown nicely. Prepare the other ingredients: shred the cheddar cheese, slice the green onions, and crumble the cooked bacon if using. Having everything ready ensures a smooth assembly once the potatoes are done.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated and baking dish greased
Step 3
In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked potatoes with the sour cream, milk, and butter. Use a large spoon or potato masher to gently mix until the potatoes are mostly smooth but still have some texture. This balance is key to a creamy yet hearty casserole. Stir in the garlic powder, salt, and pepper to season evenly. Taste a little to adjust seasoning before adding cheese and onions.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks creamy with small potato chunks visible
Step 4
Fold in about 1 1/2 cups of the shredded cheddar and half the green onions into the potato mixture. Mixing some cheese inside ensures gooey pockets throughout the casserole. The green onions add a subtle bite and freshness that complements the richness. If using bacon, stir most of it in now, reserving some for topping. Be gentle to keep the texture nice and fluffy.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cheese and onions evenly distributed but not overmixed
Step 5
Transfer the potato mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly. Sprinkle the remaining cheddar cheese, green onions, and bacon pieces on top. This layering creates a golden, bubbly crust during baking that’s irresistible. The contrast between the creamy interior and crispy topping is what makes this dish stand out.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Casserole surface evenly covered with cheese and toppings
Step 6
Place the casserole in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes. During baking, the cheese on top will melt and brown beautifully, while the inside warms through and thickens. Don’t rush this step or open the oven door too often, as consistent heat helps develop that perfect crust. You’ll know it’s done when the top is golden and bubbly and you can see slight browning around the edges.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Top is golden brown and bubbly; edges slightly crisp
Step 7
Once baked, remove the casserole from the oven and let it rest for about 5 minutes. This resting time allows the casserole to set, making it easier to serve and enhancing flavor melding. If you dig a little deeper, you’ll notice the creamy texture and the way the cheese and potatoes have married perfectly. Serve warm, garnished with extra green onions or a dollop of sour cream if you like.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Casserole is slightly firm and easy to scoop but still creamy
Step 8
For an extra touch, try serving this casserole alongside a fresh green salad or steamed veggies to balance the richness. It’s also a fantastic side for grilled meats or roasted chicken. This dish reheats well, making it a great make-ahead option for busy days or potlucks. Just cover leftovers tightly and store in the fridge.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: N/A - serving suggestion
Step 9
If you want to customize, try mixing in other add-ins like sautéed mushrooms, chopped jalapeños for heat, or swapping cheddar for smoked gouda for a different flavor profile. Just remember to adjust seasoning accordingly. Experimenting is part of the fun and lets you make this casserole your own signature comfort food.
