Easy Easter Dinner Recipes That Bring Family Together with Fresh Twists

Pro Tips

Use a meat thermometer to check ham doneness.
Relying on internal temperature (140°F) ensures the ham is safely cooked without drying out. Guessing by time alone can lead to under- or overcooking, affecting texture and safety.
Apply glaze in the last 30 minutes uncovered.
Glazing too early causes burning due to sugar content. Waiting until most cooking is done allows the glaze to caramelize perfectly without charred spots.
Rest ham after roasting before slicing.
Resting lets juices redistribute evenly, yielding moist slices. Cutting immediately causes juices to run out, making the meat dry.
Roast vegetables in a single layer with space.
Crowding pans causes steaming rather than roasting, preventing caramelization and crisp edges.
Use starchy potatoes for mashed potatoes.
Starchy potatoes like Russets break down easily for creamy texture. Waxy potatoes can turn gluey or lumpy.
Trim vegetables just before roasting.
Freshly trimmed veggies retain moisture and flavor better. Pre-cut vegetables can dry out or lose vibrancy.
Tent ham loosely with foil during resting.
Tent foil keeps ham warm without trapping steam, which could make the crust soggy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the resting period after roasting.

Why it happens Many rush to serve hot food immediately, not realizing resting affects texture.

Fix Allow the ham to rest 10-15 minutes loosely tented. This step lets juices redistribute, ensuring moist and tender slices.

Applying glaze too early.

Why it happens Impatience to see a shiny crust leads cooks to glaze at the start.

Fix Wait until the last 30 minutes of cooking to apply glaze. This prevents burning and promotes beautiful caramelization.

Overcrowding the vegetable pan.

Why it happens Trying to roast all veggies at once without enough space.

Fix Use multiple pans or roast in batches to allow air circulation for crisp, caramelized veggies.

Using the wrong potatoes for mashing.

Why it happens Using waxy potatoes out of convenience.

Fix Choose starchy potatoes like Russets or Yukon Gold for fluffy, creamy mashed potatoes.

Not using a thermometer for ham.

Why it happens Relying on time alone can be misleading due to ham size variations.

Fix Use a meat thermometer to check for 140°F internal temperature for perfectly cooked ham.

Variations

Maple Glazed Ham

Swap honey with pure maple syrup for a deeper, woodsy sweetness. This variation pairs beautifully with roasted sweet potatoes instead of carrots, adding a cozy autumn vibe to your Easter table.

Note: Maple syrup caramelizes similarly to honey but can brown faster; watch closely during glazing.

Herb and Garlic Roasted Ham

Add minced garlic and fresh rosemary to the glaze for an herbal twist. This version brings a savory depth that complements the natural sweetness of the ham and works well with garlic mashed potatoes.

Note: Fresh herbs can burn quickly; add them to the glaze just before applying to avoid bitterness.

Vegetarian Easter Side Salad

Replace roasted veggies with a fresh spring salad of mixed greens, radishes, and a lemon vinaigrette. It adds a crisp, refreshing contrast to the richness of the ham.

Note: Prepare salad just before serving to keep greens crisp and vibrant.

Honey Mustard Glazed Turkey Breast

Use turkey breast instead of ham, applying the same honey mustard glaze. This lighter protein option is perfect if you want a leaner Easter main dish with similar flavors.

Note: Turkey breast cooks faster; monitor internal temp carefully to avoid drying out.

Sweet Potato Mash Instead of Regular Potatoes

Swap traditional mashed potatoes for a creamy sweet potato mash with a hint of cinnamon and nutmeg. It adds color and a sweet-spicy twist to your Easter plate.

Note: Sweet potatoes require slightly less liquid when mashing to avoid a gummy texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

You can keep the ham and sides at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to prevent drying. Beyond that, refrigerate promptly to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in airtight containers for up to 4 days. The flavors meld nicely but the glaze may thicken. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the ham and veggies.

Freezer

Freeze ham slices and vegetables separately in airtight containers or freezer bags for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat ham and vegetables in a 325°F oven covered with foil to retain moisture, about 15-20 minutes. Add a splash of broth or water to mashed potatoes before microwaving to keep creamy. Avoid high heat to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

Prepare the glaze and trim vegetables 1-2 days ahead. Store glaze refrigerated and keep vegetables washed but raw. Roast everything fresh on the day of serving for best flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I use a pre-cooked ham for this recipe?
Yes! Most hams sold are pre-cooked. This recipe focuses on reheating and glazing, so adjust cooking time accordingly. Use a meat thermometer to avoid overcooking; aim for 140°F internal temperature.
What can I substitute for Dijon mustard in the glaze?
You can use yellow mustard if that’s what you have, but it’s milder and less tangy. For more depth, mix in a splash of apple cider vinegar or lemon juice to mimic Dijon’s acidity.
How do I know when the ham is done?
Use a meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part without touching bone. The safe internal temperature is 140°F for pre-cooked ham. The juices should run clear, and the meat should feel firm but juicy.
Can I prepare the mashed potatoes ahead of time?
Yes, you can make mashed potatoes a day ahead. Store them covered in the fridge and gently reheat with a splash of milk or cream to restore creaminess. Avoid over-stirring to keep texture smooth.
What if I don’t have a roasting rack?
You can place the ham on a bed of sliced onions or carrots in the roasting pan to lift it up. This helps air circulate and prevents sogginess, though a rack is ideal.
How do I prevent the glaze from burning?
Apply the glaze during the last 30 minutes of cooking and keep the oven temperature moderate. Watch closely when glazing and avoid leaving the ham unattended to catch any burning early.
Can I add other vegetables to roast with the carrots and asparagus?
Absolutely! Potatoes, parsnips, or Brussels sprouts work well. Just adjust roasting time accordingly and make sure to cut vegetables into similar sizes for even cooking.
Is it okay to use frozen vegetables for roasting?
Fresh vegetables roast better because they have less moisture. Frozen veggies tend to steam rather than roast, resulting in softer textures and less caramelization.

What You'll Need

Serves 4 generously

  • 1 whole roasted ham (about 4 pounds), bone-in
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 pound baby carrots, peeled
  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 cup mashed potatoes, prepared
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 325°F (165°C). Place the whole bone-in ham in a roasting pan with a rack to allow air circulation. This setup helps the ham cook evenly and prevents it from sitting in its own juices, which can make the bottom soggy. Cover it loosely with foil to keep the moisture in during the initial cooking phase. Slow roasting at this temperature ensures the meat stays tender and juicy without drying out. Avoid cranking up the heat, which can cause the glaze to burn before the ham is cooked through.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes prep + 1.5 hours roasting
✓ Doneness: Ham’s internal temperature reaches 140°F and juices run clear
Step 2
While the ham roasts, prepare the glaze by whisking together Dijon mustard, honey, brown sugar, and ground cloves in a small bowl. This combination balances tangy, sweet, and warm spice notes that will caramelize beautifully on the ham’s surface. About 30 minutes before the ham is done, remove the foil and brush the glaze evenly over the top and sides. Return to the oven uncovered to let the glaze thicken and develop a shiny, sticky crust. Keep an eye on it to prevent burning, especially because of the sugar content.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is bubbly and golden brown with slight caramelized edges
Step 3
While the ham finishes roasting, toss the baby carrots and asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Spread them out on a baking sheet in a single layer to ensure even roasting. Place them in the oven during the last 20 minutes of the ham’s cooking time. Roasting these veggies enhances their natural sweetness and adds a lovely texture contrast to the meal. Avoid overcrowding the pan, which can cause steaming instead of roasting.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender with lightly browned edges and a slight crisp
Step 4
Prepare your mashed potatoes if not already done. For a creamy, smooth texture, use starchy potatoes like Russets, boiled until very tender, then mashed with warm milk and butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste. The warmth of the potatoes will complement the hot ham and roasted veggies perfectly. Avoid over-mixing, which can make the potatoes gluey.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mashed potatoes are smooth, creamy, and fluffy
Step 5
Once the ham is fully cooked and glazed, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing. Resting allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making each slice juicy and tender rather than dry. Tent loosely with foil to keep warm. Resist the urge to cut right away, as this step is crucial for the best texture.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes resting
✓ Doneness: Ham feels firm but juicy when pressed lightly
Step 6
Slice the ham against the grain into thick, even pieces to ensure tenderness. Arrange the slices on a serving platter alongside the roasted vegetables and mashed potatoes. Sprinkle fresh chopped parsley over the vegetables and potatoes for a fresh, vibrant touch and a pop of color. This small addition really brightens the presentation and flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even, juicy slices with colorful plating
Step 7
Serve immediately while everything is warm and fresh. The combination of the sweet-spiced ham, earthy roasted vegetables, and creamy potatoes makes for a balanced and satisfying Easter meal. Encourage guests to go back for seconds — it’s that good! Pair with a light white wine or sparkling water with lemon for a festive touch.
⏱ Time: Immediate serving
✓ Doneness: Warm, inviting aroma fills the room
Step 8
For leftovers, slice the ham carefully and store in an airtight container. Leftover ham can be repurposed into sandwiches, salads, or added to soups, so don’t waste a bite. The roasted vegetables and potatoes also reheat well in the oven or microwave with a splash of water or broth to keep them moist.
⏱ Time: No cooking, storage instructions
✓ Doneness: Leftovers stay moist and flavorful after reheating
Step 9
If you want to prep ahead, you can make the glaze and prepare the vegetables a day in advance. Keep the glaze refrigerated and the vegetables washed and trimmed but raw, then roast them fresh on the day of serving. This cuts down on active cooking time and keeps everything tasting bright and fresh.
⏱ Time: Prep day before
✓ Doneness: Glaze holds well refrigerated, vegetables stay fresh
