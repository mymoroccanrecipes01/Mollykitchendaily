Soft Italian Easter Cookies Bursting with Almond and Anise Flavors

20 min Prep Time
15 min Time
24 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These soft Italian Easter cookies are a family favorite with delicate almond and anise notes. The colored sugar adds a festive touch. Perfect for celebrating spring or any cozy gathering!

Pro Tips

Chill the dough briefly if it feels too sticky to handle.
Chilling firms the dough, making it easier to scoop and shape without sticking to your hands. This prevents the cookies from spreading too much during baking, preserving their soft, rounded shape.
Use parchment paper instead of greasing the baking sheet.
Parchment paper prevents sticking without adding extra fat, which could cause the cookies to flatten. It also promotes even baking and easy cleanup, ensuring the cookies maintain their delicate texture.
Don’t skip the colored sanding sugar topping.
Besides adding a festive look, sanding sugar adds a subtle crunch that contrasts nicely with the soft cookie, enhancing the overall texture and eating experience.
Measure almond flour carefully by spooning into your measuring cup.
Almond flour can compact easily, leading to too much flour and dense cookies. Lightly spooning it in and leveling off ensures the right ratio for tender cookies.
Let cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring.
This resting time allows the cookies to set up and become firm enough to move without breaking, preserving their shape and texture.
Avoid overbaking; remove cookies when edges are just golden.
Overbaking dries out the cookies, losing their soft, melt-in-your-mouth quality. The centers will continue to cook slightly after removal from the oven.
Use fresh almond flour for best flavor and texture.
Older almond flour can become rancid or lose its nutty aroma, negatively affecting taste. Fresh flour ensures a bright, rich almond flavor in every bite.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the anise extract

Why it happens Sometimes people omit anise because they’re unsure about the flavor or don’t have it on hand.

Fix While you can omit it, the cookies lose their signature licorice note that makes them special. If you don’t have anise extract, try a small amount of fennel extract or simply use almond extract alone, but know the flavor will be less traditional.

Overbaking the cookies

Why it happens It’s easy to leave them in the oven too long, especially if you’re used to crisp cookies.

Fix Watch the edges carefully and remove the cookies when they’re just turning golden. They should feel set but still soft in the center to keep their tender texture.

Using coarse almond meal instead of almond flour

Why it happens Some people use whatever ground almonds they have without checking the grind size.

Fix Use finely ground almond flour to ensure a smooth, tender cookie. Coarser meal leads to a gritty texture and less cohesive dough.

Not spacing cookies properly on the baking sheet

Why it happens Sometimes cookies are placed too close together, causing them to merge.

Fix Space cookies at least 2 inches apart to allow slight spreading without touching, preserving their pretty shape.

Mixing the dough too vigorously

Why it happens Overenthusiastic mixing can happen when trying to combine sticky dough.

Fix Stir gently just until ingredients are combined. Overmixing can make the dough too soft, affecting texture and shape.

Variations

Lemon Zest Italian Easter Cookies

Add 1 tablespoon of finely grated lemon zest to the dough for a bright citrus twist. The lemon pairs beautifully with almond and anise, giving the cookies a fresh, springtime flavor. Expect a slightly zestier aroma and a subtle tang on the palate.

Note: Incorporate zest with dry ingredients and monitor baking time closely, as citrus oils can affect browning.

Chocolate-Dipped Italian Easter Cookies

Once cooled, dip half of each cookie in melted dark chocolate and let set. This adds a rich, bittersweet contrast to the soft almond cookie, making them extra special for celebrations.

Note: Allow cookies to cool completely before dipping to avoid melting or breaking.

Gluten-Free Almond and Anise Cookies

This original recipe is naturally gluten-free thanks to almond flour. For extra chewiness, add a tablespoon of tapioca starch. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and suitable for gluten-sensitive guests.

Note: Adjust baking time by a minute or two if tapioca starch is added, watching for doneness.

Spiced Italian Easter Cookies

Add 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon and 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves to the dry mix for a warm, cozy spice profile. These spices complement almond and anise for a festive flavor perfect for cooler spring days.

Note: Mix spices with dry ingredients thoroughly to ensure even distribution.

Honey-Sweetened Italian Easter Cookies

Replace half the granulated sugar with 1/4 cup honey for a natural sweetness and moist crumb. Honey adds floral notes that elevate the almond flavor, though the cookies will be softer and slightly stickier.

Note: Reduce oven temperature by 10°F and watch closely to prevent overbrowning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight and moisture to preserve their softness and prevent sugar from melting.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate the cookies for up to one week in an airtight container. They may firm up slightly but will soften to room temperature quickly. Avoid refrigeration if you prefer them very soft.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 3 months. Separate layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature for several hours before serving.

Reheating

If you want to refresh cookies after storage, warm them in a 300°F oven for 3-5 minutes to regain softness. Avoid microwaving as it can make them rubbery or dry out quickly.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Shape and bake the cookies the next day for freshest results. Alternatively, bake them and freeze fully baked cookies for easy thaw-and-serve treats.

FAQ

Can I substitute almond flour with regular flour?
Substituting almond flour with regular wheat flour isn’t recommended because the texture and flavor will change dramatically. Almond flour gives these cookies their moist, tender crumb and nutty taste. Using wheat flour would produce a drier, less flavorful cookie.
Why are my cookies too crumbly?
Crumbly cookies usually mean the dough is too dry or hasn’t been mixed enough to bind properly. Make sure to measure almond flour correctly and mix the dough until it just comes together. Adding a little extra egg can also help with binding if needed.
Can I make these cookies vegan?
These cookies rely on eggs for structure and moisture, so vegan substitutions would require significant adjustments. You might try flax eggs or commercial egg replacers, but the texture and flavor will differ. This recipe is best enjoyed as is or with minor tweaks.
How do I store the cookies to keep them soft?
Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature with a slice of bread or a damp paper towel to maintain moisture. Avoid refrigeration, which can dry them out. Proper storage keeps them soft for several days.
Can I freeze the dough?
Yes, you can freeze the dough shaped into balls on a tray, then transfer to a freezer bag. Bake from frozen, adding a couple of extra minutes to the baking time. This is great for making ahead and enjoying fresh cookies anytime.
Why do my cookies spread too much?
Spreading often happens if the dough is too warm or the baking sheet is greased. Use parchment paper and chill the dough briefly if it feels too soft. Also, avoid adding too much liquid or overmixing, which can cause spreading.
Can I add other flavors to the cookies?
Absolutely! Lemon zest, cinnamon, or even dipping in chocolate are great ways to customize. Just keep the core almond and anise flavors balanced to maintain the cookie’s character.
How long will these cookies keep their flavor?
Stored properly, these cookies keep their flavor and texture for up to a week at room temperature. Almond and anise flavors may intensify slightly over time, which many find delightful.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 2 1/2 cups almond flour, finely ground
  • 1 cup granulated sugar, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon anise extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Colored sanding sugar, for topping

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line two baking sheets with parchment paper. This ensures even cooking and prevents sticking, which is crucial since these cookies are soft and delicate. Using parchment also helps with easy cleanup and keeps the bottoms from browning too much. Avoid using greased pans, as that can cause the cookies to spread too much and lose their shape.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated and baking sheets ready with parchment paper
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the almond flour, sugar, and salt until well combined. Mixing these dry ingredients first helps distribute the sugar evenly, which is key for consistent sweetness and texture throughout the cookies. Almond flour tends to clump, so make sure to break up any lumps to get a smooth dough later on.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients look evenly mixed with no clumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, beat the eggs lightly, then stir in the anise and almond extracts. This step infuses the dough with aromatic flavors that are signature to this cookie. Be careful not to add too much anise extract, as it can become overpowering and mask the almond flavor.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Egg mixture is smooth and fragrant
Step 4
Slowly pour the egg mixture into the dry ingredients and stir with a wooden spoon or spatula until a sticky dough forms. It's important not to overmix here; you want the dough to be just combined to keep the cookies tender. The dough will be slightly sticky but manageable.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is sticky and cohesive but not runny
Step 5
Using a spoon or small cookie scoop, drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This spacing allows the cookies room to expand slightly without merging. If the dough is too sticky to handle, lightly wet your hands to shape the cookies gently.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly spaced dough mounds on baking sheets
Step 6
Sprinkle each dough mound generously with colored sanding sugar. This not only adds a festive, colorful finish but also a slight crunch that contrasts nicely with the soft cookie. Don’t skip this step if you want that classic Easter vibe!
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are topped with a bright, even layer of sugar
Step 7
Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 12 to 15 minutes. Keep a close eye starting at 12 minutes because these cookies should be lightly golden at the edges but still soft in the center. Overbaking will dry them out, losing their signature tenderness.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are lightly golden, centers look set but soft
Step 8
Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. This resting time lets them firm up slightly so they don’t fall apart when transferred. Then, gently transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling on a rack prevents sogginess from trapped steam.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are firm enough to move without breaking
Step 9
Once cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. They keep well for several days and the flavors even deepen after a day. Enjoy these with a cup of espresso or tea for the perfect springtime treat.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cookies are fully cooled and stored properly
