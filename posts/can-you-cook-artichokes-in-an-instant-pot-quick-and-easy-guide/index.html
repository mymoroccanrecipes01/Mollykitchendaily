Easy Instant Pot Artichokes: Perfectly Steamed in Under 15 Minutes

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 24, 2026
5 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These Instant Pot artichokes steam up tender and flavorful in just 15 minutes. I love how quick and fuss-free this method is, making it a perfect snack or side anytime you crave something fresh and healthy.

Pro Tips

Trim the artichokes thoroughly before cooking to remove prickly tips and tough leaves.
Removing the thorny tips and outer tough leaves ensures a more pleasant eating experience and allows steam to penetrate more effectively, resulting in evenly cooked, tender artichokes.
Use just enough water to create steam but not so much that artichokes sit in water.
Too much water leads to soggy artichokes and dilutes their flavor. The right amount of water creates a steam environment that cooks evenly without waterlogging the vegetable.
Allow a natural or quick pressure release carefully to avoid overcooking.
Releasing pressure too quickly or too slowly can affect texture. Quick release stops cooking immediately, preserving firmness and color, while natural release can soften them further if desired.
Serve artichokes with a simple dipping sauce like melted butter or lemon aioli.
The mild artichoke flavor pairs well with rich or tangy sauces, enhancing the eating experience without overpowering the natural taste.
Store leftover artichokes in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.
Proper storage maintains freshness and texture. Reheating gently with moisture prevents drying out and keeps them enjoyable.
Use kitchen scissors to snip thorny leaf tips safely.
This prevents accidental pricks and makes trimming easier and more precise, ensuring a better eating experience.
Add lemon and garlic to the steaming water to infuse subtle flavor.
Steam carries these aromatics gently into the artichoke, enhancing its natural taste without overpowering it.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the artichokes until mushy

Why it happens People often leave the Instant Pot on too long or don’t release pressure promptly, thinking more cooking equals better tenderness.

Fix Stick to the recommended 15 minutes and perform a quick release after cooking to stop the process. This keeps the artichokes tender but firm and preserves their vibrant color.

Using too much water in the Instant Pot

Why it happens Some assume more water means better steaming, but this can lead to soggy artichokes.

Fix Measure carefully and use only about 1 cup of water so the artichokes steam rather than boil. The goal is to produce steam, not submerge the vegetable.

Not trimming the artichokes properly before cooking

Why it happens Beginners may skip trimming to save time, unaware that thorns and tough leaves affect texture and eating comfort.

Fix Take a few extra minutes to trim the tops and snip thorny tips with scissors. This simple prep improves both cooking and eating experience significantly.

Skipping the lemon in the steaming water

Why it happens Some might forget or think it’s optional, missing out on flavor and color benefits.

Fix Always add lemon halves to the water to prevent browning and add fresh citrus notes that brighten the artichokes.

Eating the fuzzy choke without removing it

Why it happens New eaters may not know about the inedible fuzzy center and try to eat it, which is unpleasant.

Fix After cooking, scoop out the fuzzy choke gently with a spoon before serving or eating the heart. It’s easy and makes the dish enjoyable.

Variations

Garlic Herb Butter Artichokes

After steaming, brush the artichokes with a warm garlic herb butter made from softened butter, minced garlic, parsley, and a pinch of salt. This adds a rich, savory layer that makes a perfect appetizer or side dish.

Note: Apply butter immediately after cooking while artichokes are warm for best absorption and flavor.

Spicy Lemon Aioli Dip

Serve the steamed artichokes with a homemade lemon aioli spiced up with a dash of cayenne or smoked paprika. It adds a creamy, tangy kick that pairs beautifully with the tender vegetable.

Note: Prepare the aioli ahead and chill; the cool dip contrasts nicely with warm artichokes.

Mediterranean Style Artichokes

Add olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and feta cheese on the side. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle oregano for a Mediterranean twist that’s fresh and flavorful.

Note: Serve the additional ingredients at room temperature for best texture contrast.

Grilled Artichokes After Steaming

Steam the artichokes first, then cut in half and grill them briefly for a smoky flavor and char marks. Brush with olive oil and garlic before grilling for extra taste.

Note: Grill over medium heat for 3-5 minutes per side to avoid drying out.

Vegan Lemon Tahini Dressing

Mix tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and water to make a creamy vegan dressing perfect for dipping steamed artichokes. It adds a nutty, tangy flavor that’s different from traditional butter dips.

Note: Thin the tahini dressing with water to desired consistency before serving.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cooked artichokes can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to prevent drying. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store artichokes in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. They may lose some firmness but remain tasty. Reheat gently with a splash of water or serve cold in salads.

Freezer

Freeze cooked artichokes wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and reheat gently to avoid sogginess.

Reheating

Reheat artichokes on the stovetop in a steamer basket or microwave with a damp paper towel to retain moisture. Avoid high heat that dries them out. Adding a little lemon juice or butter refreshes flavor.

Make Ahead

You can prep and trim artichokes a day ahead, storing them in water with lemon juice to prevent browning. Cook them fresh or steam and refrigerate partially cooked to finish later.

FAQ

Can I use frozen artichokes for this recipe?
Frozen artichokes are usually pre-cooked and may not steam well in the Instant Pot. If using frozen, adjust cooking time to 5-7 minutes and skip the trimming step. They won’t have the same fresh texture but still work for quick meals.
What if my artichokes are smaller or larger than usual?
Smaller artichokes may only need 12 minutes of cooking, while very large ones might need up to 18. Adjust timing by checking tenderness with a knife or pulling a leaf gently to see if it comes off easily.
How do I know when the artichokes are done?
You’ll know they’re done when a leaf pulls off easily without resistance and the stem pierces easily with a knife. The heart should feel tender but not mushy.
Is it necessary to remove the choke before serving?
Yes, the fuzzy choke isn’t edible and can be unpleasant to eat. After cooking, scoop it out with a spoon before serving or eating the heart.
Can I steam artichokes without an Instant Pot?
Definitely! Steaming on the stove takes longer, usually 30-45 minutes. The Instant Pot just speeds things up and makes the process hands-off.
How do I prevent artichokes from browning after trimming?
Rub them with lemon juice immediately after trimming or submerge them in water with lemon juice. This acid prevents oxidation and keeps them looking fresh.
Can I eat artichoke leaves raw?
Raw artichoke leaves are tough and bitter. They’re best steamed or cooked to soften and bring out sweetness.
What dipping sauces go well with steamed artichokes?
Simple melted butter, garlic aioli, lemon vinaigrette, or tahini-based sauces all complement the mild flavor. Choose one that suits your taste and occasion.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 large fresh artichokes, trimmed
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • Salt, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing your artichokes: trim the top quarter of the leaves with a sharp knife, snip off the thorny tips with kitchen scissors, and remove any small tough leaves near the base. This prep ensures you get to the tender parts without any prickly bites. Rinse them under cold water to wash away any dirt trapped between the leaves. Proper trimming is key because it makes the artichokes easier to eat and cooks them evenly without any tough bits lingering.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes feel trimmed and clean, with no sharp tips or tough leaves remaining
Step 2
Pour 1 cup of water into your Instant Pot and add the smashed garlic cloves and lemon halves to infuse the steam with flavor. Place the steamer basket inside and arrange the artichokes upright, stem side down, so the steam circulates well around each one. The water and aromatics create a gentle steam that cooks the artichokes perfectly while adding a subtle background flavor. Avoid adding too much water; otherwise, the artichokes could become waterlogged.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Water is just below the basket, and you can smell the garlic and lemon aroma
Step 3
Seal the Instant Pot lid and set it to 'Manual' or 'Pressure Cook' on high pressure for 15 minutes. This timing is perfect for large artichokes; smaller ones might need slightly less time. Using the pressure cooker speeds up the steaming process by raising the boiling point of water and saturating the artichokes with steam quickly. Don’t rush this step or open the lid early, or your artichokes might end up tough.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Timer finishes, and pressure releases naturally or via quick release as preferred
Step 4
Once the cooking cycle ends, carefully perform a quick release of the pressure to avoid overcooking. Use tongs to lift the artichokes out of the basket and onto a plate. They should feel tender when you pull on a leaf and the stem should pierce easily with a knife. This quick release stops the cooking immediately, so your artichokes don’t turn mushy or lose their bright green color.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes feel tender but firm, bright green leaves
Step 5
Serve your artichokes warm with melted butter, aioli, or a simple lemon vinaigrette. To eat, pull off the outer leaves, dip the fleshy part in your sauce, and scrape the soft flesh with your teeth. When you reach the heart, remove the fuzzy choke with a spoon — this part isn’t edible but is easy to spot. The heart is the prized part: creamy, tender, and packed with flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Leaves pull off easily, and heart is soft and flavorful
Step 6
If you want to add an extra touch, sprinkle freshly chopped parsley or grated Parmesan over the artichokes before serving. It adds a fresh color and a little umami punch that pairs beautifully with the lemon and garlic notes. This step is optional but highly recommended for a dinner party or special occasion.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Herbs or cheese visibly coating the artichokes
Step 7
To store leftovers, cool the artichokes completely and place them in an airtight container in the fridge. They keep well for up to 3 days and can be reheated gently. Avoid reheating at high heat or they’ll dry out. Instead, steam or microwave briefly with a splash of water to revive their moisture.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Artichokes cool to room temperature before storage
Step 8
For a fun twist, try slicing the cooked artichoke hearts and tossing them into salads, pasta dishes, or on top of pizzas. Their tender texture and mild flavor add a lovely depth without overpowering other ingredients. This versatility is why I keep a batch ready in the fridge for quick meals.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Artichoke hearts are tender and easily sliced
Step 9
Clean your Instant Pot thoroughly after cooking to remove any lemon or garlic odors that might linger. A quick wipe with a damp cloth and mild soap keeps your pot fresh and ready for the next recipe. Proper cleaning also preserves the metal and silicone seals, ensuring your pot maintains a perfect seal every time.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Instant Pot surface is clean and odor-free
