Cozy Homemade Chicken Noodle Soup with Tender Chicken & Fresh Noodles

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 23, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This homemade chicken noodle soup is pure comfort in a bowl. Slow-simmered broth and fresh noodles make it perfect for chilly nights or when you need a little extra care. The tender chicken is the star here!

Pro Tips

Skim the foam from the broth early and often.
Removing foam prevents impurities from clouding the broth and keeps the flavor clean. If ignored, the broth can taste bitter or have a gritty texture, detracting from the soup’s overall quality.
Rest the noodle dough before rolling it out.
Resting relaxes the gluten strands formed during kneading, making the dough easier to roll and less likely to shrink or tear. Skipping this step can result in tough or uneven noodles.
Simmer broth gently, avoiding a rolling boil.
A gentle simmer extracts flavors slowly without toughening the chicken or breaking down the broth’s clarity. Boiling causes proteins to tighten and broth to become cloudy and harsh-tasting.
Use cold water when starting the broth.
Cold water extracts more flavor and collagen from chicken bones and skin than hot water. It also allows impurities to be released gradually, which you can skim off.
Cut noodles evenly for consistent cooking.
Uniform noodle size ensures they cook at the same rate, preventing some from being overcooked while others remain underdone. Uneven noodles can ruin texture.
Season the soup at the end of cooking.
Adding salt and pepper last lets you adjust seasoning precisely without over-salting. Early seasoning can concentrate too much during simmering, making the soup too salty.
Garnish with fresh herbs just before serving.
Fresh herbs lose flavor and color when cooked too long. Adding them last provides a fresh, bright contrast to the rich broth and enhances aroma.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Boiling the broth too hard

Why it happens Many cooks think boiling speeds up cooking and flavor extraction, but it actually toughens meat and clouds broth.

Fix Lower the heat to maintain a gentle simmer. Watch for small bubbles rising slowly and avoid vigorous boiling to keep broth clear and chicken tender.

Skipping the foam skimming

Why it happens It’s easy to overlook foam because it seems harmless, or cooks get impatient.

Fix Use a fine mesh skimmer or spoon to remove foam every 5 minutes during the first 20 minutes of simmering for a clean broth.

Not resting the noodle dough

Why it happens Impatience or unfamiliarity with dough handling leads cooks to skip resting.

Fix Cover the dough and let it rest at least 15 minutes; this makes rolling easier and prevents tough noodles.

Overcooking fresh noodles

Why it happens Fresh noodles cook fast, and cooks may not watch closely.

Fix Add noodles at the end and cook 3-5 minutes until just tender; test frequently to avoid mushiness.

Not shredding chicken properly

Why it happens Some chop chicken roughly or leave large pieces, affecting texture.

Fix Use two forks to shred chicken into bite-sized, uniform pieces for better mouthfeel and even flavor distribution.

Variations

Gluten-Free Chicken Noodle Soup

Swap all-purpose flour noodles for gluten-free pasta or spiralized vegetables like zucchini. The broth and chicken preparation remain the same, offering a lighter but still comforting soup. Expect a slightly different texture but equally delicious flavors.

Note: Cooking times for gluten-free noodles or veggies are shorter; add them just before serving to avoid overcooking.

Creamy Chicken Noodle Soup

Add a splash of heavy cream or coconut milk at the end for a velvety twist. This version feels richer and more indulgent, perfect for colder days. Keep the broth simmered gently to prevent curdling.

Note: Temper cream by gradually mixing hot broth into it before adding to the pot to avoid curdling.

Spicy Chicken Noodle Soup

Add a diced jalapeño or a pinch of cayenne pepper with the aromatics for a gentle heat kick. The spice warms you up and adds complexity without overpowering the classic flavors.

Note: Adjust spice level to taste and add fresh herbs at the end to balance heat.

Vegetable-Loaded Chicken Noodle Soup

Include additional veggies like peas, corn, or spinach in the last 10 minutes of cooking for extra nutrition and color. This boosts texture variety and makes the soup more hearty and wholesome.

Note: Add delicate greens last to avoid overcooking and retain bright color.

Slow Cooker Chicken Noodle Soup

Use a slow cooker to simmer chicken and aromatics for 4-6 hours, then add fresh noodles and cook until tender. This hands-off method is great for busy days and develops deep flavor.

Note: Add noodles towards the end to prevent them from becoming mushy in the slow cooker.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The soup can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigeration is recommended to keep it fresh and safe.

Refrigerator

Store the soup in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The noodles may absorb broth and soften over time, so reheat gently and consider adding a splash of broth or water to loosen the soup before serving cold.

Freezer

Freeze the soup for up to 3 months in a freezer-safe container, leaving some headspace for expansion. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly and remove excess air. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating slowly on the stove.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally. Add a little water or broth if the soup has thickened. Avoid boiling rapidly, which can break down noodles and toughen chicken. Microwave reheating is possible but uneven; stir halfway through.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the broth and shred the chicken 1-2 days ahead and store separately. Make the fresh noodles just before serving for best texture, or store dough wrapped tightly in the fridge for up to 24 hours.

FAQ

Can I use store-bought broth instead of making my own?
Yes, you can use store-bought broth for convenience, but homemade broth offers deeper flavor and richer texture. If using store-bought, choose low-sodium to control salt levels and consider simmering it with fresh aromatics to boost flavor.
How do I prevent the noodles from getting mushy?
Add fresh noodles near the end of cooking and watch them closely, as they cook quickly (3-5 minutes). Avoid overcooking by testing frequently and stirring gently to prevent sticking.
Can I make the soup vegetarian?
Yes, substitute the chicken with hearty vegetables or tofu and use vegetable broth. The flavor will be different but still comforting. Consider adding mushrooms or beans for protein and texture.
What if I don’t have time to make fresh noodles?
Dried egg noodles or pasta can be used instead. Add them in the last 7-10 minutes of cooking, adjusting timing based on package instructions. Fresh noodles offer better texture but dried is a fine shortcut.
Can I freeze the soup with fresh noodles?
Freezing soup with fresh noodles is possible but noodles often become mushy after thawing. For best texture, freeze broth and chicken separately and add fresh noodles when reheating.
How do I make the broth more flavorful?
Slow simmer the chicken and aromatics gently for at least 45 minutes and skim foam regularly. Adding a splash of apple cider vinegar helps extract minerals and collagen from bones, enriching flavor and mouthfeel.
Can I use chicken breasts instead of a whole chicken?
You can, but using a whole chicken or bone-in pieces yields more flavorful broth due to collagen and marrow. Chicken breasts alone produce a lighter broth and less gelatinous texture.
How do I store leftover soup to keep noodles from soaking up too much broth?
Store broth and noodles separately if possible. If mixed, reheat gently with added broth or water to loosen noodles. Noodles absorb liquid over time, so separating helps maintain texture.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 cups soup, 4 servings

  • 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 lbs), cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 3 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 8 cups cold water
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup water (for dough)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by placing the whole chicken pieces into a large pot and cover with 8 cups of cold water. Adding cold water helps extract more flavor from the chicken as it slowly heats up. Turn the heat to medium and bring to a gentle simmer. You’ll notice some foam rising to the surface—skim this off with a spoon to keep your broth clear and clean-tasting. Avoid boiling rapidly as it can toughen the chicken and cloud the broth. Simmer gently for about 20 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is opaque and easily pulls away from the bone; broth is clear with no foam.
Step 2
Add the sliced carrots, celery, quartered onion, and smashed garlic to the pot. These aromatics infuse the broth with savory depth. Keep the heat low and let everything simmer together for another 30 minutes. This slow cooking melds flavors beautifully and softens the vegetables without turning them mushy. If you rush this step by cranking the heat, the broth won’t develop that rich, layered taste we’re aiming for.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender but hold their shape; broth smells fragrant and savory.
Step 3
While the broth simmers, prepare the fresh noodles. In a bowl, mix 2 cups of all-purpose flour with 1 large egg and 1/2 cup water. Stir until a sticky dough forms, then knead on a floured surface until smooth and elastic—about 5-7 minutes. Kneading develops gluten, which gives the noodles their chewy texture. If your dough feels too sticky, sprinkle a little more flour. Cover with a towel and let it rest for 15 minutes to relax the gluten, making it easier to roll out.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is smooth, elastic, and not sticky to touch.
Step 4
Roll the rested dough out thinly on a floured surface, about 1/8 inch thick. Then cut into thin strips about 1/4 inch wide for classic noodle shapes. Fresh noodles cook quickly and absorb broth flavors better than dried ones. If you prefer, you can cut them wider or thinner depending on your preference. Dust the noodles with a little flour to prevent sticking and set aside.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Noodles are evenly cut and lightly floured to avoid clumping.
Step 5
Remove the chicken pieces from the broth and set aside to cool slightly. Strain the broth through a fine sieve into another pot to remove vegetables and impurities, leaving a clear, flavorful broth. This step ensures your soup has a beautiful golden color and smooth texture. Don’t skip straining; it elevates the final presentation and taste.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Broth is clear and free of solids; chicken is cool enough to handle.
Step 6
Once the chicken is cool enough, shred the meat off the bones using two forks. Discard skin and bones or save the bones for making stock later. Shredded chicken blends better into the soup and offers tender bites in every spoonful. Avoid chopping as it can make the texture less appealing.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is shredded into bite-sized pieces.
Step 7
Bring the strained broth back to a gentle simmer and add the fresh noodles. Fresh noodles cook quickly—about 3 to 5 minutes—so keep an eye on them to avoid overcooking, which makes them mushy. Stir gently to prevent sticking. When noodles float to the surface and feel tender but still slightly firm, they’re done.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Noodles are tender and float to the top.
Step 8
Add the shredded chicken back into the pot along with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Stir well to combine. Heat through for another 2-3 minutes to meld flavors. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Don’t rush seasoning; a well-balanced soup is all about layers of flavor.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup is hot and seasoned to your liking.
Step 9
Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and freshness. The herbs brighten the rich broth and add a subtle herbal note. Serve immediately for best texture and flavor. Leftovers reheat beautifully, but fresh noodles can soften more over time.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup looks vibrant with green parsley and steaming hot.
