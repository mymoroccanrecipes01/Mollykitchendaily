Hearty One-Pot Instant Pot Chicken and Rice for Busy Weeknights

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This one-pot Instant Pot chicken and rice meal is a total game changer. Using a simple layering trick, it locks in all the flavors and cooks evenly in under an hour. Perfect for those busy nights when you want comfort without the hassle!

Pro Tips

Always rinse your rice before cooking.
Rinsing removes surface starch that causes rice grains to stick together and become gummy. This step ensures the rice cooks up fluffy and separate, which is especially important in pressure cooking where moisture is trapped and can cause overcooking if the starch isn’t removed.
Don’t stir the rice and chicken layers before cooking.
Layering without stirring keeps textures distinct and prevents the rice from sticking to the bottom of the pot. Stirring mixes the starch into the broth, increasing the risk of burning on the bottom and uneven cooking. The chicken juices flavor the rice during cooking without needing to be mixed.
Use natural pressure release instead of quick release.
Allowing the Instant Pot to release pressure naturally lets the rice finish cooking gently and absorb flavors fully. Quick release can cause the rice to be undercooked or mushy and the chicken to dry out. This slower release also helps prevent splattering and keeps the texture perfect.
Cut chicken into uniform pieces for even cooking.
Uniform pieces cook at the same rate, avoiding some parts being overdone while others remain undercooked. This is especially important in pressure cooking where timing is fixed. It also helps flavor distribution and presentation.
Use room temperature broth and ingredients.
Cold ingredients can affect the Instant Pot’s pressure build-up time and cooking consistency. Using room temperature broth and chicken helps the pot reach pressure faster and cook ingredients evenly, resulting in better texture and flavor.
Add peas after cooking, not before.
Adding peas at the end prevents them from overcooking and turning mushy. The residual heat warms them through perfectly, preserving their sweetness and bright green color, which enhances the dish visually and texturally.
Season generously but taste before serving.
Seasoning during preparation layers the flavors, but broth and chicken vary in saltiness. Tasting before serving allows you to adjust salt and pepper perfectly, avoiding an overly salty or bland dish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Stirring rice and chicken before cooking

Why it happens Many cooks think mixing everything helps flavors blend better, but in pressure cooking, this causes rice to stick and burn.

Fix Layer the rice and broth first, then gently place the seasoned chicken on top without stirring. This technique prevents burning and ensures even cooking.

Using cold broth or chicken right from the fridge

Why it happens It's tempting to add ingredients straight from the fridge for convenience, but cold items slow down pressure build-up.

Fix Bring broth and chicken to room temperature before cooking. This ensures the Instant Pot reaches pressure quickly and cooks evenly.

Quick releasing pressure immediately after cooking

Why it happens Impatience or lack of awareness leads to quick venting, which can disrupt cooking.

Fix Allow a natural pressure release for at least 10 minutes to let rice finish cooking properly and avoid mushy textures.

Not rinsing rice before cooking

Why it happens Skipping rinsing saves time but leads to sticky rice due to excess starch.

Fix Always rinse rice under cold water until clear to ensure fluffy rice with separate grains.

Overcrowding the pot with chicken pieces

Why it happens Trying to cook too much at once leads to uneven cooking and steaming rather than pressure cooking.

Fix Use the recommended amount of chicken and spread pieces evenly on top of rice for best results.

Variations

Spicy Cajun Chicken and Rice

Add 1 teaspoon Cajun seasoning and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the chicken before cooking. This will give the dish a nice kick and a deeper, smoky flavor profile. Serve with a squeeze of lime for brightness.

Note: The spices may darken the chicken slightly; ensure not to overcook to keep it juicy.

Vegetarian Mushroom and Herb Rice

Replace chicken with 2 cups of chopped mushrooms and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. Add thyme and rosemary for an earthy flavor. This variation is hearty and perfect for meat-free meals.

Note: Mushrooms release moisture, so reduce broth by 1/4 cup to avoid soggy rice.

Lemon Garlic Chicken and Rice

Add zest and juice of one lemon along with minced garlic to the chicken before cooking. This brightens the dish and adds a fresh, zesty aroma that pairs well with the thyme.

Note: Lemon juice can slightly alter cooking time; monitor tenderness closely.

Creamy Coconut Chicken and Rice

Swap half the broth for coconut milk and add a teaspoon of curry powder to the chicken seasoning. This gives the dish a rich, tropical twist with creamy texture and warm spices.

Note: Coconut milk thickens the liquid, so avoid overcooking to prevent rice from becoming too soft.

Herbed Brown Rice and Chicken

Use brown rice instead of white and increase broth to 2 cups. Add a bay leaf and extra thyme for flavor. Brown rice takes longer to cook but adds nuttiness and more fiber.

Note: Increase pressure cooking time to 22 minutes and natural release to 15 minutes for perfect texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit out covered for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth may occur. If you plan to serve later, cool it quickly and refrigerate.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The rice may firm up when cold; reheat gently with a splash of broth or water to restore moisture and fluffiness.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain texture.

Reheating

Reheat on stovetop over low heat or in microwave with a splash of broth to keep rice moist. Avoid overheating which can dry out chicken or make rice mushy.

Make Ahead

You can prep the rice and broth mixture a day ahead and store in the fridge. Add fresh seasoned chicken on cooking day for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but chicken breasts cook faster and can dry out if overcooked. Reduce pressure cooking time by 2-3 minutes and use fresh breasts for best results. Keep an eye on texture to avoid toughness.
What if my rice turns out mushy?
Mushy rice often results from overcooking or not rinsing the rice before cooking. Make sure to rinse the rice to remove excess starch and follow the exact cooking times. Also, avoid stirring before cooking to prevent sticking.
Can I add vegetables before cooking?
Hard vegetables like carrots or bell peppers can be added on top of the chicken before cooking. Softer veggies like peas are best added after cooking to avoid overcooking and mushiness.
Is it necessary to use smoked paprika?
While smoked paprika adds a nice depth of flavor, you can substitute with regular paprika or a mild chili powder if you prefer. The smoky note enhances but isn’t essential.
How do I prevent rice from sticking to the bottom?
Rinse the rice well, layer it evenly with broth before adding chicken, and avoid stirring. Also, ensure your Instant Pot’s sealing ring and inner pot are clean to maintain proper pressure.
Can I double this recipe?
You can double the ingredients, but make sure not to exceed the Instant Pot’s max fill line. Cooking time remains the same, but layering evenly is even more important to ensure consistent cooking.
What type of rice works best?
Long-grain white rice is ideal for this recipe due to its fluffy texture after cooking. Avoid instant or quick-cooking rice as they can overcook and become mushy under pressure.
Can I use water instead of broth?
Yes, but using broth adds significant flavor to both the rice and chicken. If you use water, consider adding extra seasoning or bouillon to compensate.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, room temperature
  • 1 pound boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas, thawed
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by rinsing the rice under cold water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch — this helps prevent the rice from becoming gummy. Pour the chicken broth into the Instant Pot and add the rinsed rice, spreading it evenly across the bottom. Not stirring at this point is key because it avoids the rice sticking to the pot and burning during pressure cooking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Rice is evenly spread, broth covers the rice completely
Step 2
In a separate bowl, toss the chicken chunks with olive oil, smoked paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper until well coated. This seasoning mix adds a smoky, earthy flavor that penetrates the chicken during cooking. Layer the chicken on top of the rice gently, without mixing. This layering technique allows the chicken juices to seep down and flavor the rice while keeping the textures distinct.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces fully coated and arranged evenly on rice
Step 3
Close the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. It’s important not to rush this step because the pressure cooking ensures the rice and chicken cook perfectly together. If you open the pot too soon, the rice may be undercooked or the chicken dry.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pressure cooking cycle completes without error
Step 4
Allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes before carefully turning the valve to venting to release any remaining steam. Natural release helps the rice finish cooking gently and prevents it from sticking or becoming mushy. Quick release can cause rice to be unevenly cooked and chicken to dry out.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pressure fully released, lid unlocks easily
Step 5
Open the lid and gently fluff the rice with a fork, being careful not to break up the chicken pieces. Stir in the thawed peas, which will warm through from the residual heat and add a fresh, sweet contrast. The peas also brighten the dish visually and nutritionally.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peas are warmed, rice is fluffy and separated
Step 6
Taste and adjust seasoning with extra salt and pepper if needed. This step is essential because the broth and chicken can vary in saltiness, so tasting ensures a balanced final flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balanced seasoning with no overpowering saltiness
Step 7
Spoon the chicken and rice onto plates and garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and fresh herbal note. The parsley adds brightness and elevates the dish’s presentation.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish looks vibrant and inviting with garnish
Step 8
Serve immediately with a simple side salad or steamed vegetables for a complete meal. The warm, hearty nature of this dish pairs perfectly with crisp greens to balance the flavors and textures.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Meal plated and ready to enjoy
Step 9
Clean the Instant Pot insert promptly after cooking to prevent any stuck-on rice or chicken bits. Using warm soapy water and a gentle scrubber helps maintain the pot’s non-stick surface for future meals.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pot is clean with no residue
