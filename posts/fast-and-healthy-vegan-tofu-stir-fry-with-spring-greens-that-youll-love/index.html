Easy Tofu Stir-Fry with Fresh Spring Greens and Tangy Sauce

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This tofu stir-fry bursts with fresh spring greens and a tangy sauce that’s both light and satisfying. Pressing the tofu until crispy is the game changer here. Ideal for a quick weeknight dinner when you want something healthy but tasty!

Pro Tips

Press the tofu for at least 15 minutes using a heavy object.
Pressing tofu removes excess water, which is crucial for getting a crispy texture when frying. Without pressing, the tofu will steam instead of sear, resulting in a soft, soggy bite that lacks the satisfying crunch this dish needs.
Use a non-stick or well-seasoned skillet for frying tofu.
A non-stick surface prevents the tofu from sticking and tearing when you flip it. This helps maintain the tofu’s shape and ensures even browning on all sides, which is key to a perfect crispy exterior.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when cooking tofu cubes.
Crowding traps steam and lowers the pan temperature, preventing proper browning. Cooking in batches if necessary keeps the heat high and allows the tofu to crisp up beautifully.
Add garlic and ginger to the oil before vegetables.
Sautéing aromatics first flavors the oil, layering the dish with depth and complexity. This technique infuses every bite with fragrant notes of ginger and garlic without overpowering the fresh veggies.
Cook vegetables just until tender-crisp.
Overcooking vegetables makes them mushy and dulls their vibrant colors and flavors. Stir-frying quickly over medium-high heat preserves nutrients, texture, and the fresh taste that balances the dish.
Use a small bowl to mix the sauce ingredients before adding.
Pre-mixing the sauce ensures even distribution and prevents uneven seasoning. It also allows the flavors to meld slightly before hitting the pan, creating a more harmonious taste.
Toss tofu gently when combining with sauce and veggies.
Tofu can be delicate after frying; rough stirring can break it apart. Gently folding preserves the crispy cubes and keeps the dish visually appealing and texturally balanced.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not pressing tofu enough

Why it happens Many cooks underestimate how much water tofu holds and skip pressing to save time.

Fix Set aside at least 15 minutes to press tofu with a heavy object. Use multiple layers of towel to absorb moisture effectively. This step is non-negotiable for crispy tofu.

Overcrowding the pan

Why it happens Trying to cook all tofu at once can seem faster but leads to steaming.

Fix Cook tofu in batches if needed, giving each piece space to brown. This keeps the pan hot and dry for perfect crispiness.

Burning garlic or ginger

Why it happens High heat or leaving aromatics unattended causes bitterness.

Fix Use medium heat and stir constantly when sautéing garlic and ginger. Remove from heat if they start to brown too quickly.

Overcooking vegetables

Why it happens Leaving veggies in the pan too long softens them too much.

Fix Cook for just a few minutes until tender-crisp. They should still have a bright color and slight crunch.

Using soft or silken tofu

Why it happens Some think any tofu works, but soft types fall apart.

Fix Choose extra firm tofu for this recipe. It holds shape and crisps nicely.

Variations

Spicy Sriracha Tofu Stir-Fry

Add 1-2 teaspoons of sriracha to the stir-fry sauce for a spicy kick. You can also toss in thinly sliced jalapeños with the vegetables. This variation brings a fiery edge that pairs well with the tangy sauce and crispy tofu.

Note: Spicy ingredients can burn quickly; add them mid-cooking and stir frequently to avoid bitterness.

Peanut Sauce Tofu Stir-Fry

Swap the soy-vinegar sauce for a creamy peanut sauce made with peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, and a touch of honey. This gives the dish a rich, nutty flavor with a hint of sweetness, perfect for a comfort meal.

Note: Peanut sauce thickens as it cooks; add a splash of water if it becomes too thick to coat ingredients evenly.

Tofu Stir-Fry with Cashews and Broccoli

Add roasted cashews and steamed broccoli florets to the stir-fry for extra crunch and nutrition. This variation is heartier and adds a nice contrast between the nutty cashews and crisp tofu.

Note: Broccoli cooks faster than some greens, so add it after garlic and ginger to avoid overcooking.

Miso-Ginger Tofu Stir-Fry

Incorporate 1 tablespoon of white miso paste into the sauce for a deeper umami flavor. The miso adds complexity and a subtle sweetness that enhances the fresh vegetables and tofu.

Note: Dissolve miso paste in a bit of warm water before adding to the sauce to ensure smooth mixing.

Sweet and Sour Tofu Stir-Fry

Mix pineapple chunks and bell peppers with a sweet and sour sauce made from vinegar, ketchup, and brown sugar. This playful twist adds a tropical flair and tangy sweetness to the crispy tofu and veggies.

Note: Cook pineapple last to keep it juicy and prevent it from becoming mushy.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This tofu stir-fry should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours to avoid bacterial growth. Cover loosely with foil or a clean towel if serving buffet-style for short periods.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The tofu may soften slightly when chilled but reheating gently restores some crispness. Serve warm for best texture and flavor.

Freezer

Freeze the stir-fry in a sealed container for up to 1 month. To prevent freezer burn, wrap the container in foil or place it in a freezer bag. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a skillet over medium heat with a splash of water or oil to revive the tofu’s crispness and prevent drying. Avoid microwaving at high power, which can make tofu rubbery.

Make Ahead

You can press and cube the tofu up to 1 day ahead and store it wrapped in the fridge. Chop vegetables the day before as well. Assemble and cook the stir-fry fresh for the best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use soft tofu instead of extra firm?
Soft tofu is not ideal for this recipe because it holds too much water and breaks apart easily when cooking. Extra firm tofu maintains its shape and crisps up nicely. If you only have soft tofu, consider using it in soups or smoothies instead.
How do I know when the tofu is crispy enough?
The tofu should be golden brown on all sides and feel firm to the touch. If it’s pale or soft, it likely needs more cooking time or higher heat. Don’t stir too often; let the tofu sear undisturbed to develop a crust.
Can I skip pressing the tofu?
Pressing is key for removing excess moisture to achieve crispiness. Skipping this step will result in tofu that steams rather than fries, leading to a soggy texture. If pressed tofu isn’t an option, pat dry thoroughly and cook at higher heat to compensate.
What can I substitute for soy sauce if I’m gluten-free?
Tamari is a great gluten-free alternative to soy sauce that offers a similar umami flavor. Coconut aminos is another option, though it’s slightly sweeter and less salty, so adjust amounts accordingly.
How long can leftovers be stored safely?
Leftovers keep well for up to 3 days in the refrigerator in an airtight container. Beyond that, the texture and flavor begin to degrade. Reheat gently to restore some crispness to the tofu.
Can I add other vegetables to this stir-fry?
Absolutely! Bell peppers, broccoli, snap peas, or carrots all work well. Just adjust cooking times so that firmer vegetables go in earlier and softer ones later to maintain a nice texture balance.
Is it okay to use olive oil instead of vegetable oil?
You can use olive oil, but it has a lower smoke point and stronger flavor that might alter the dish slightly. Vegetable or peanut oil is preferred for high-heat frying to get that crisp tofu without burning.
Why does my tofu stick to the pan?
If the pan isn’t hot enough or lacks sufficient oil, tofu can stick. Use a non-stick skillet and heat oil until shimmering before adding tofu. Don’t move the tofu cubes too early; let them form a crust first.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 14 oz extra firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 cups mixed spring greens (baby spinach, bok choy, snow peas)
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, sliced

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by pressing your tofu well to remove excess water. Wrap the tofu block in a clean kitchen towel and place a heavy object on top for at least 15 minutes. This step is essential because it firms up the tofu, allowing it to crisp nicely when cooked rather than steaming and turning soggy. Without pressing, the tofu will release water in the pan, making it difficult to achieve that golden crust we want. Don’t skip this step; it really changes the texture.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu feels firm and dry to the touch, no water pooling when pressed
Step 2
Cut the pressed tofu into 1-inch cubes, then toss them lightly with 1 teaspoon of cornstarch. This thin coating helps create a crispy outer layer once fried. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add tofu cubes in a single layer and cook without moving them for about 4-5 minutes until the bottom side is golden brown. Flip carefully to brown all sides evenly. Rushing this step or overcrowding the pan causes steaming and prevents crispiness.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu cubes are evenly golden and visibly crisp on all sides
Step 3
Remove the crispy tofu from the pan and set it aside on a paper towel to drain excess oil. This keeps the tofu from becoming greasy and soggy later. Leave about 1 tablespoon of oil in the pan for stir-frying the veggies. If there’s too little oil, the vegetables might stick or cook unevenly, so adjust as needed.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu is dry to the touch and oil in pan shimmers gently
Step 4
Add minced garlic and ginger to the hot pan and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant. This step flavors the oil, infusing the dish with aromatic notes that bring the stir-fry to life. Be careful not to burn the garlic, which turns bitter quickly. Stir constantly and keep heat moderate.
⏱ Time: 30 seconds
✓ Doneness: Garlic smells fragrant and lightly toasted but not browned
Step 5
Toss in the sliced red bell pepper and cook for 2-3 minutes until it starts to soften but still retains some crunch. Then add the mixed spring greens and stir-fry for another 2 minutes. The vegetables should be bright and tender-crisp, not mushy. Stirring continuously keeps them cooking evenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Veggies are vibrant in color and tender but still firm
Step 6
In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, and sesame oil. Pour this sauce over the vegetables and stir well to coat everything evenly. The balance of salty, sweet, and tangy in the sauce brings a delicious contrast to the dish. Let it cook for 1-2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is glossy and coats the vegetables uniformly
Step 7
Return the crispy tofu to the pan and gently toss everything together to combine. Heat through for another minute so the tofu absorbs some sauce but stays crisp. Avoid stirring too vigorously to keep tofu cubes intact.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Tofu is warmed, coated with sauce, and remains crisp
Step 8
Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds and sliced green onions over the top for a final touch of flavor and crunch. These garnishes add a toasty nuttiness and fresh bite that elevate the dish. Serve immediately while hot.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Sesame seeds look toasted and green onions are bright
Step 9
Plate your stir-fry over steamed rice or noodles if desired, and enjoy! This dish tastes best fresh but can be stored and reheated gently. The contrast of crispy tofu with bright greens and tangy sauce makes it a satisfying meal any day of the week.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish looks vibrant and appetizing on the plate
