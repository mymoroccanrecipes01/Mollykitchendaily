No-Bake Strawberry Cheesecake in a Jar That Everyone Will Love

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 14, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This no-bake strawberry cheesecake in a jar is creamy and fresh with a delightful graham cracker crunch. Super easy to make, it’s perfect for summer parties or a quick sweet treat when you don’t want to turn on the oven.

Pro Tips

Always soften cream cheese to room temperature before mixing.
Cold cream cheese resists blending and creates lumps, which ruins the smooth texture of the filling. Softened cream cheese mixes easily with sugar and vanilla, resulting in a silky, creamy base that's essential for a great cheesecake.
Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps the heavy cream whip faster and hold stiff peaks better. Warm bowls slow down the process and can prevent the cream from reaching the right consistency, affecting the lightness of the filling.
Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the jar bottoms.
Firmly packing the crust ensures it holds together and provides a satisfying crunch. If the crust is loosely packed, it will crumble apart when eating and won’t contrast well with the creamy filling.
Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture.
Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring vigorously deflates the mixture, making the cheesecake dense and less enjoyable.
Chill the assembled jars for at least 2 hours before serving.
Chilling allows the filling to set and the crust to firm up. Serving too soon results in a runny filling and crumbly crust, losing the signature cheesecake texture.
Use fresh strawberries instead of frozen for the topping.
Fresh berries retain their texture and vibrant color, adding a juicy burst that frozen berries can’t match. Frozen strawberries release excess water when thawed, which can make the crust soggy and the dessert watery.
Add a swirl of strawberry jam to the filling for extra flavor.
A gentle swirl of jam adds sweetness and a pretty marbled effect without overpowering the fresh berry flavor. It enhances the strawberry note, making the dessert more vibrant and visually appealing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese straight from the fridge.

Why it happens Many people forget to plan ahead and soften their cream cheese, assuming they can mix it cold. This leads to lumps and a gritty texture.

Fix Let the cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. If you're short on time, cut it into small cubes to soften faster. This ensures a smooth, creamy filling.

Overwhipping the heavy cream.

Why it happens It’s easy to keep whipping once stiff peaks form, but going too far turns cream into butter.

Fix Watch the cream carefully and stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks hold shape but still look glossy. This keeps the filling light and airy. If you go too far, start over with fresh cream.

Not packing the crust firmly.

Why it happens Rushing or being too gentle with the crust layer leads to a crumbly base that falls apart when eating.

Fix Take your time pressing the graham cracker crumbs firmly into the jar bottoms with the back of a spoon. A compact crust holds up well and creates a satisfying texture contrast.

Skipping the chilling step.

Why it happens Impatience or last-minute serving plans make people serve the cheesecake too soon.

Fix Chill the jars for at least 2 hours or overnight to let the filling set and the crust firm up. This step is essential for the right texture and flavor melding.

Using frozen strawberries for topping.

Why it happens Frozen berries are convenient but release water when thawed, making the dessert watery.

Fix Use fresh, ripe strawberries for topping. If you must use frozen, drain any excess liquid and pat dry before adding to keep the crust and filling intact.

Variations

Mixed Berry Cheesecake Jars

Swap out strawberries for a mix of blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries. The combination adds a variety of tart and sweet flavors that complement the creamy filling beautifully. Garnish with a few mint leaves for a fresh touch.

Note: Different berries release varying amounts of juice; be sure to pat them dry to prevent soggy crusts.

Chocolate Swirl No-Bake Cheesecake

Incorporate a swirl of melted dark chocolate or chocolate ganache into the cream cheese filling. This adds a rich depth of flavor and makes the dessert feel indulgent without baking.

Note: Allow the chocolate to cool slightly before swirling to avoid melting the whipped cream.

Lemon Zest and Blueberry Cheesecake

Add fresh lemon zest and a tablespoon of lemon juice to the filling for a bright citrus twist. Top with fresh blueberries for a refreshing and tangy dessert perfect for spring and summer.

Note: The acidity of lemon juice may slightly soften the filling; fold it in gently and chill well.

Vegan No-Bake Strawberry Cheesecake

Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks instead of heavy cream. The crust can be made with vegan butter or coconut oil. This version is creamy and delicious without dairy.

Note: Coconut cream whips differently than dairy cream; chill thoroughly and whip slowly for best results.

Peanut Butter Swirl Cheesecake Jars

Swirl creamy peanut butter into the filling for a nutty twist. Top with chopped peanuts and sliced strawberries for a fun and flavorful combo that’s a crowd-pleaser.

Note: Warm peanut butter slightly to make swirling easier and prevent clumps.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dessert should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the cream cheese and dairy content. Cover tightly if you must keep it out briefly to avoid drying out or contamination.

Refrigerator

Store the cheesecake jars in an airtight container or covered in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture might firm up more when cold, so you can eat it straight from the fridge or let it sit a few minutes at room temperature for a creamier feel.

Freezer

You can freeze the cheesecake jars for up to 1 month. Wrap each jar tightly with plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving. The texture may be slightly less creamy after freezing but still delicious.

Reheating

No reheating is needed or recommended for this dessert. Serve chilled or at room temperature. If the filling feels too firm from the fridge, let it sit out for 10-15 minutes before enjoying.

Make Ahead

Make the crust and filling up to 2 days ahead, store them separately, then assemble jars and add fresh strawberries just before serving to keep the fruit fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I make this cheesecake without heavy cream?
Heavy cream is essential for whipping and creating the light, fluffy texture of the filling. If you omit it, the cheesecake will be dense and less creamy. However, you can try substituting with coconut cream for a dairy-free version, but the texture and flavor will differ slightly.
How long can I store the no-bake cheesecake in the fridge?
This cheesecake keeps well for up to 3 days when stored in airtight containers. After that, the crust may start to soften and the fresh strawberries can lose their texture. For best taste and texture, enjoy within the first couple of days.
Can I use a different crust instead of graham crackers?
Absolutely! You can use crushed digestive biscuits, vanilla wafers, or even Oreo crumbs for a different flavor profile. Just be sure to combine with melted butter to help the crust set properly.
Why is my filling too runny?
Runny filling usually means the cream cheese wasn’t fully softened or the whipped cream wasn’t stiff enough. Also, insufficient chilling time can cause the filling not to set. Make sure to whip the cream to stiff peaks and refrigerate the assembled jars for at least 2 hours.
Can I prepare these cheesecake jars in advance for a party?
Yes! You can prepare the crust and filling a day or two ahead and assemble the jars the day of your event. Add fresh strawberries just before serving to keep them from getting soggy or discolored.
What if I don’t have jars? Can I use a different container?
You can use small glasses, ramekins, or even a large trifle bowl. Jars are convenient for portion control and portability, but any small container that can be covered and chilled works well.
Is it okay to add gelatin to help set the cheesecake?
This recipe doesn’t require gelatin because chilling firms up the crust and filling sufficiently. Adding gelatin can change the texture, making it more like a mousse or jelly. If you prefer a firmer cheesecake, you can add gelatin, but it’s not necessary.
Can I swap the strawberries for another fruit?
Definitely! Blueberries, raspberries, or peaches work beautifully. Just be sure to adjust sweetness if needed and use fresh fruit to avoid excess moisture that can affect the crust.

What You'll Need

4 individual jars

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs, crushed
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream, cold
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons strawberry jam (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by mixing the graham cracker crumbs with melted butter in a bowl until the mixture resembles wet sand. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs together, creating a firm, crunchy base for your cheesecake. If you skip or skimp on the butter, the crust won’t hold up well and might fall apart when you serve it. Press the crumb mixture evenly into the bottom of each jar, using the back of a spoon or your fingers for a compact layer. This creates a sturdy foundation that contrasts nicely with the creamy filling later.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust is tightly packed and holds its shape when pressed
Step 2
In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. This step ensures the filling is lump-free and sweetened just right. Take your time beating to avoid any graininess; a smooth base means a better texture overall. If your cream cheese is too cold, it won’t mix well, so be sure it’s softened to room temperature beforehand.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and creamy without lumps
Step 3
In a separate chilled bowl, whip the cold heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This is what makes the filling light and fluffy instead of dense and heavy. Be careful not to overwhip or you’ll start turning it into butter. Using cold cream and a cold bowl helps speed up the whipping process and gives you better volume.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream holds stiff peaks and is glossy
Step 4
Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Folding preserves the airiness of the whipped cream, giving the cheesecake its signature light texture. Stirring too vigorously will deflate the mixture and make it dense. Take your time and fold carefully until just combined.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, airy, and uniformly mixed
Step 5
If you like a hint of extra strawberry flavor, gently swirl in the strawberry jam into the filling now. This adds a touch of sweetness and a beautiful pink tint. Don’t overmix or the jam will lose its texture and color.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Jam is gently swirled through without fully blending
Step 6
Spoon or pipe the cheesecake filling evenly over the graham cracker crust in each jar. Filling the jars carefully keeps the layers neat and makes the dessert look pretty when served. Leave a little space at the top for the strawberry topping.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling layer is smooth and level in each jar
Step 7
Top each jar with a generous spoonful of chopped fresh strawberries. These add a juicy, fresh burst that balances the creamy filling. If you want, add a small mint leaf for color and aroma. The fresh fruit also makes the dessert feel lighter and less heavy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries look fresh and vibrant on top
Step 8
Cover each jar tightly with a lid or plastic wrap and chill in the fridge for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling step is key to let the cheesecake set and the flavors meld. If you try to eat it too soon, the filling will be too soft and the crust won’t have firmed up.
⏱ Time: 120 minutes or overnight
✓ Doneness: Filling is firm to the touch and crust is set
Step 9
Before serving, check the jars for any condensation and wipe the outside clean. Serve chilled straight from the fridge. These jars keep the dessert neat and portable, perfect for picnics or casual gatherings. Enjoy the creamy, crunchy, fresh combo in every spoonful!
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert is chilled, clean, and ready to serve
