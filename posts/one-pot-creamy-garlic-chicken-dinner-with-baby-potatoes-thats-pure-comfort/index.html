One-Pot Creamy Garlic Chicken with Baby Potatoes for Cozy Nights

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 18, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This cozy one-pot dinner features tender chicken and baby potatoes simmered in a dreamy garlic cream sauce. It’s super simple, with easy cleanup, making it perfect for busy weeknights or relaxed weekends.

Pro Tips

Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken.
Crowding traps steam and prevents proper browning, which is essential for flavor development. Browning creates complex flavors through the Maillard reaction, so leaving enough space ensures a crispy, golden crust rather than steamed, pale chicken.
Use a heavy-bottomed pan or Dutch oven for even heat distribution.
Heavy cookware retains heat better and distributes it evenly, preventing hot spots that can burn garlic or unevenly cook potatoes. It also helps maintain a steady simmer, which is key for tender chicken and sauce thickening.
Simmer gently with the lid on to cook chicken evenly.
Covering traps moisture and heat, cooking the chicken through without drying it out. A gentle simmer ensures the sauce thickens slowly and the potatoes become tender without breaking apart or becoming mushy.
Reserve the final reduction step to thicken the sauce.
Removing the lid near the end lets excess liquid evaporate, concentrating flavors and thickening the sauce naturally. Skipping this step leads to a thinner sauce that lacks the luscious texture that makes this dish so comforting.
Season in layers throughout cooking.
Adding salt and pepper at different stages builds depth and balances flavors. Early seasoning seasons the chicken and potatoes internally, while final adjustments fine-tune the sauce’s taste, preventing it from being flat or overly salty.
Use fresh herbs as a garnish instead of cooking them in the sauce.
Fresh parsley adds a bright, fresh note and vibrant color without losing its flavor to heat. Cooking herbs too long can make them bitter or dull their aroma.
Let the dish rest for a few minutes before serving.
Resting allows the sauce to settle and thicken slightly, and the flavors to meld. It also makes serving easier as the chicken and potatoes firm up gently, preventing them from falling apart on the plate.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sear on the chicken

Why it happens Many cooks rush to get everything in the pot quickly and skip searing, thinking it’s unnecessary.

Fix Always take the time to sear the chicken first. It locks in juices and creates essential flavor through caramelization. Use medium-high heat and don’t move the chicken too soon to develop a proper crust.

Burning the garlic

Why it happens Garlic cooks very quickly and can burn if left unattended or cooked on too high heat.

Fix Add garlic after the potatoes have softened somewhat and cook on medium or lower heat. Stir constantly and remove from heat if you notice browning too fast.

Overcooking the potatoes

Why it happens Not monitoring cooking time or using large potatoes without cutting them can cause mushy or undercooked potatoes.

Fix Use baby potatoes or cut larger ones small. Simmer gently and test doneness with a fork regularly to avoid overcooking.

Adding cream too early

Why it happens Some add cream at the start, causing it to curdle or burn.

Fix Add cream towards the end of cooking to prevent separation and maintain a smooth, rich sauce.

Not seasoning properly

Why it happens Beginners often under-season or season only at the end, resulting in bland dishes.

Fix Season the chicken and potatoes before cooking and adjust seasoning gradually throughout to build balanced flavors.

Variations

Spicy Creamy Garlic Chicken

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a diced jalapeño when sautéing garlic for a gentle heat that complements the creamy sauce. This variation adds a nice kick without overpowering the garlic flavor.

Note: Monitor the spice level closely as the cream mellows heat; adjust to taste by adding more or less chili.

Mushroom and Spinach Addition

Sauté sliced mushrooms and fresh spinach along with the garlic for an earthy, green twist. The mushrooms add umami and the spinach brightens the dish with its fresh flavor and color.

Note: Add mushrooms before potatoes to allow them to release moisture and brown properly; add spinach in the last 5 minutes to avoid overcooking.

Lemon Herb Cream Sauce

Stir in fresh lemon juice and chopped rosemary or tarragon at the end for a bright, herbal note that cuts through the richness of the cream sauce, giving the dish a refreshing lift.

Note: Add lemon juice gradually and taste to avoid overpowering the delicate cream base.

Dairy-Free Version

Use full-fat coconut milk instead of heavy cream and vegetable broth instead of chicken broth for a dairy-free version. The coconut milk provides a creamy texture with a subtle tropical twist.

Note: Simmer gently to prevent coconut milk from separating; flavor will be slightly different but still rich and satisfying.

Herbed Parmesan Twist

Stir in grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil at the end for a cheesy, fragrant upgrade. Parmesan adds umami depth and a slightly nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with garlic and cream.

Note: Add cheese off heat to prevent curdling and maintain a smooth sauce.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to avoid contamination. Beyond that, refrigerate to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and potatoes absorb more liquid, so reheat gently with a splash of broth or cream to loosen the sauce. It’s best served warm rather than cold for optimal texture and flavor.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap the container with an extra layer of foil or plastic wrap. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to preserve texture.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally and adding a splash of chicken broth or cream to restore creaminess. Avoid high heat to prevent the cream sauce from separating. Microwave reheating is possible but do so in short bursts, stirring frequently.

Make Ahead

You can sear the chicken and par-cook the potatoes 1-2 days ahead, storing them separately. Assemble and add cream just before simmering on the day of serving for the freshest flavor and best texture.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but chicken breasts tend to dry out more easily during simmering. To keep them juicy, reduce the cooking time and consider pounding them to an even thickness. Alternatively, sear quickly and finish cooking off the heat with residual heat or in the oven.
What if I don’t have baby potatoes?
You can substitute with fingerling potatoes or small Yukon Gold potatoes cut into bite-sized pieces. Just be mindful that larger pieces may take longer to cook, so adjust simmering time accordingly to ensure tenderness.
How do I prevent the cream sauce from curdling?
Add the cream towards the end of cooking and keep the heat low to avoid curdling. Avoid boiling the sauce after cream is added; a gentle simmer is best to maintain a smooth texture.
Can I make this recipe vegetarian?
You can omit the chicken and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. Add hearty vegetables like mushrooms or cauliflower to replace the protein. Keep the cream or use a plant-based alternative for richness.
Is this recipe freezer-friendly?
Yes, it freezes well for up to 2 months. Freeze in airtight containers and thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating. The texture of potatoes may soften slightly after freezing, but the flavors remain delicious.
What sides go well with this dish?
A simple green salad, steamed green beans, or roasted vegetables complement this rich dish nicely. You can also serve it with crusty bread to soak up the creamy garlic sauce.
Can I use garlic powder instead of fresh garlic?
Fresh garlic is best for flavor and aroma, but if you must use garlic powder, add it sparingly at the start of cooking. It won’t provide the same depth or freshness but will still add garlic flavor.
How do I thicken the sauce if it’s too runny?
Simmer uncovered on medium heat to reduce excess liquid and thicken the sauce naturally. Alternatively, stir in a small slurry of cornstarch and water, cooking until sauce thickens.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1.5 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, trimmed
  • 1 pound baby potatoes, halved
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat olive oil in a large deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs generously with salt, pepper, and dried thyme. Place them skin side down (if skin-on) or just one side down if skinless, and sear until golden brown, about 4-5 minutes per side. This step is crucial because searing creates that flavorful crust through the Maillard reaction, locking in juices and building depth in the final dish. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure even browning. Once browned, remove the chicken and set aside.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken has a deep golden crust and releases easily from the pan
Step 2
Add the halved baby potatoes to the same pan and sauté for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. This gets the potatoes starting to soften and lightly caramelize, which enhances their natural sweetness. Don’t rush this step; the slight browning adds texture and flavor. If the pan looks dry, add a splash of olive oil. The potatoes should be golden on the edges but still firm in the center.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Potatoes are slightly browned but still hold their shape
Step 3
Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant but not browned. Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter, so watch it closely. The garlic infuses the oil and potatoes with its aromatic flavor, which will carry through the sauce.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Garlic releases a strong, pleasant aroma without browning
Step 4
Pour in the chicken broth and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits stuck to the bottom of the pan. These bits are packed with flavor and will enrich the sauce. Bring the broth to a gentle simmer to reduce slightly, about 3-4 minutes. This concentrates the flavor and starts to soften the potatoes further.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Broth is simmering gently and slightly reduced
Step 5
Return the seared chicken thighs to the pan, nestling them among the potatoes. Pour the heavy cream over everything. The cream adds richness and helps thicken the sauce as it cooks. Stir gently to combine but don’t disturb the chicken’s position too much. Season with a little more salt and pepper if needed.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Cream evenly coats chicken and potatoes, sauce looks silky
Step 6
Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. Let everything simmer gently for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally. This slow simmer allows the chicken to cook through and the potatoes to become tender, while the sauce thickens naturally. Avoid lifting the lid too often as it lets heat escape and slows cooking. The chicken should reach an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).
⏱ Time: 25
✓ Doneness: Chicken is cooked through and potatoes are fork-tender
Step 7
Once cooked, remove the lid and increase heat to medium-high for 2-3 minutes to thicken the sauce further if needed. Stir gently to avoid breaking the potatoes. This final reduction intensifies the flavors and gives the sauce a lovely velvety texture.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Sauce coats the back of a spoon thickly without running
Step 8
Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. If you want a little brightness, a squeeze of lemon juice or a sprinkle of fresh parsley brightens the dish beautifully. Garnish with chopped parsley before serving for a pop of color and fresh flavor.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Balanced seasoning with fresh herb aroma
Step 9
Serve the creamy garlic chicken and potatoes hot, straight from the pot. It pairs wonderfully with a simple green salad or steamed veggies for a complete meal. The leftovers reheat beautifully, making this a great recipe for meal prep too!
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Dish is hot and creamy with tender chicken and potatoes
