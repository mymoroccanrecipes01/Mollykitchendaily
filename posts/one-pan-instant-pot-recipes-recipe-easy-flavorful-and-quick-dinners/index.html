One-Pot Instant Pot Chicken and Rice for Busy Weeknights

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This one-pot Instant Pot chicken and rice is my go-to for busy nights. It’s packed with cozy flavors, cooks fast, and cleanup is a breeze since it’s all in one pot. Great for a comforting weeknight dinner!

Pro Tips

Always rinse your rice before cooking.
Rinsing removes excess starch that causes rice to stick together and become gummy. This simple step ensures your rice cooks up fluffy and separate, key to a great texture in this dish.
Use chicken thighs instead of breasts.
Thighs have more fat and connective tissue that breaks down during cooking, keeping the meat juicy and flavorful. Breasts tend to dry out quickly, especially under pressure cooking.
Don’t skip the sauté steps.
Sautéing onions, garlic, and chicken develops complex flavors through caramelization and Maillard reactions. This builds a deeper flavor base that pressure cooking alone can’t achieve.
Use natural pressure release for at least 10 minutes.
This allows the rice to finish cooking gently and absorb steam slowly, preventing it from being undercooked or mushy. Quick release too early can ruin texture.
Add frozen veggies after pressure cooking.
Adding peas and carrots after cooking keeps them vibrant and slightly crisp instead of mushy. The residual heat is enough to warm them through perfectly.
Deglaze the pot thoroughly after sautéing.
Scraping browned bits prevents the Instant Pot’s burn warning and adds flavor to the dish. It also ensures the rice doesn’t stick to the bottom.
Season lightly at first, then adjust at the end.
Broth and chicken can vary in saltiness. Tasting and seasoning after cooking helps avoid over-salting and balances flavor perfectly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not rinsing the rice before cooking.

Why it happens Many cooks skip rinsing to save time or don’t realize its importance, assuming the rice will cook fine as is.

Fix Always rinse your rice under cold water until the water runs clear to remove surface starch. This prevents clumping and gummy texture, resulting in fluffy rice.

Using chicken breasts instead of thighs.

Why it happens Breasts are often seen as healthier or more convenient, but they dry out easily with pressure cooking.

Fix Use boneless skinless chicken thighs for juicier, more flavorful meat. If using breasts, reduce cooking time slightly and consider adding a bit more broth to keep moisture.

Skipping the sauté step.

Why it happens Impatience or unfamiliarity with the Instant Pot’s sauté function leads to dumping everything in at once.

Fix Take the time to sauté onions, garlic, and brown chicken. This step creates flavor layers and prevents flat-tasting results.

Quick releasing pressure immediately after cooking.

Why it happens Eager to eat, cooks often open the lid right away.

Fix Allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes before quick release. This helps rice finish cooking and avoids mushy or undercooked grains.

Adding frozen vegetables before pressure cooking.

Why it happens Some think adding all ingredients at once saves time.

Fix Add frozen peas and carrots after cooking to keep them vibrant and prevent them from turning mushy.

Variations

Spicy Cajun Chicken and Rice

Add 1 tsp Cajun seasoning and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the chicken before sautéing. Swap peas and carrots for diced bell peppers and corn for a southern twist. Expect a bolder, spicier flavor with a colorful veggie mix.

Note: Make sure to balance spice levels to your taste and keep the rice texture intact by not increasing liquid too much.

Creamy Mushroom Chicken and Rice

Stir in 1/2 cup heavy cream and sautéed mushrooms after pressure cooking. This variation yields a richer, creamier texture with earthiness from the mushrooms. Perfect if you want a comforting, indulgent meal.

Note: Add cream after cooking to prevent curdling and maintain silky texture.

Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice

Add zest of one lemon and fresh chopped herbs like parsley and thyme after cooking. This brightens the dish with fresh, zesty notes and a lighter feel. Great for spring or summer dinners.

Note: Add lemon zest and herbs off-heat to preserve freshness and aroma.

Vegetarian Instant Pot Rice Pilaf

Skip the chicken and use vegetable broth with sautéed mushrooms, diced bell peppers, and chickpeas. This version is hearty and flavorful while fully plant-based. Texture will be slightly different but still satisfying.

Note: Increase broth slightly to account for chickpeas and avoid dryness.

Brown Rice and Chicken Version

Use brown rice instead of white, increasing cooking time to 22 minutes. This makes the dish nuttier and higher in fiber but requires longer pressure cooking and more broth.

Note: Adjust liquid to 1 3/4 cups and increase pressure cook time for brown rice.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can be safely left out at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Cover it loosely with foil or a clean towel to protect from contaminants. Beyond that, refrigeration is recommended to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The rice may firm up and the chicken can dry slightly. Reheat gently to preserve moisture, adding a splash of broth or water if needed. It’s still tasty cold but best warmed.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating. Texture may soften slightly after freezing but remains delicious.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat or in the microwave. Add a splash of chicken broth or water to loosen the rice and cover to trap steam. Avoid high heat which can dry out the chicken and rice. Stir occasionally for even warming.

Make Ahead

You can prep the chicken and sauté the onions and garlic a day ahead, storing them in the fridge separately. Rinse the rice and keep it ready to add. Assemble and pressure cook the day of serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but chicken breasts cook faster and can dry out under pressure cooking. If using breasts, reduce cooking time by 2-3 minutes and keep an eye on texture. Adding a bit more broth can help maintain moisture.
What if I don’t have an Instant Pot?
You can make this on the stovetop in a heavy pot or Dutch oven. Brown the chicken and onions, then add rice and broth, cover, and simmer on low until rice is tender and chicken cooked through, about 20-25 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
Can I add other vegetables?
Absolutely! Diced bell peppers, corn, or mushrooms work well. Add harder veggies like carrots or peppers before pressure cooking and delicate ones like peas after, to keep their texture vibrant.
How do I prevent the burn error on the Instant Pot?
Make sure to deglaze the pot well after sautéing by scraping any browned bits from the bottom. Also, ensure enough liquid is added and avoid thick sauces that can stick and burn.
Is rinsing rice really necessary?
Yes, rinsing removes excess starch that causes rice to clump and become gummy. It helps achieve fluffy, separate grains, especially important in pressure cooking where texture can be tricky.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, this recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth. Always check labels to be sure no gluten-containing additives are present.
How do I reheat leftovers without drying out the chicken?
Reheat gently on low heat or in the microwave with a splash of broth or water. Cover while reheating to trap steam, which keeps chicken moist and rice from drying out.
Can I double this recipe for more servings?
You can double the ingredients but pressure cooking time remains about the same. Make sure your Instant Pot is large enough to hold the increased volume without exceeding the max fill line.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup long grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by setting your Instant Pot to sauté mode and heating the olive oil. Add the diced onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 3-4 minutes. This step is crucial because it builds the flavor base for the dish. If you skip it or rush it, the onions won’t have a chance to sweeten and deepen the overall taste. Stir occasionally to avoid burning and watch for the edges turning golden, which signals perfect caramelization.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Onions should be soft, translucent, and slightly golden at the edges.
Step 2
Add the minced garlic to the sautéed onions and cook for about 30 seconds to 1 minute until fragrant. Garlic burns quickly, so keep stirring and don’t leave it unattended. This step releases the garlic’s essential oils, layering the flavor without overpowering the dish. If you rush or skip this, you lose that subtle garlic aroma that makes the dish inviting.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: You’ll smell a fresh, garlicky aroma but the garlic remains pale, not browned.
Step 3
Add the chicken chunks to the pot, sprinkle with paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper, and sauté for 4-5 minutes until the pieces are lightly browned on all sides. Browning the chicken adds a rich, savory depth through the Maillard reaction, which enhances flavor. Don’t overcrowd the pot or stir too often; let the chicken develop a nice crust.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces should have a golden-brown surface but not cooked through.
Step 4
Pour in the rinsed rice and chicken broth, scraping any browned bits off the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon. This deglazing step prevents the dreaded burn warning on the Instant Pot and infuses the rice with flavor. Make sure the rice is evenly spread and submerged in liquid for even cooking.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Rice should be submerged and broth slightly simmering.
Step 5
Close the Instant Pot lid, seal the vent, and set it to Manual or Pressure Cook on high for 10 minutes. Pressure cooking cooks the rice and chicken perfectly together, locking in moisture and flavor. Resist the urge to open early, as the pressure release is important for texture.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Instant Pot display shows cooking complete; rice looks plump and chicken is tender.
Step 6
Once cooking is done, allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes before switching to quick release. This gradual release lets the rice finish steaming gently and prevents it from sticking or drying out. If you rush this step, the rice might be undercooked or mushy.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Pressure valve drops and you can safely open the lid.
Step 7
Open the lid carefully, stir in the frozen peas and carrots, and let the residual heat warm them through for 3-4 minutes. Adding veggies at the end keeps them bright and fresh without overcooking. Stir gently to avoid breaking up the rice grains.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Peas and carrots are tender but still vibrant in color.
Step 8
Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. This final seasoning tweak balances the flavors perfectly. Sometimes broth or chicken varies in saltiness, so tasting at this stage is key to avoid over or under-seasoning.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Balanced, savory flavor with no bland or overly salty notes.
Step 9
Serve hot, optionally garnished with fresh parsley or a squeeze of lemon for brightness. The fresh herbs or citrus add a lovely contrast to the rich, comforting dish. Plus, it makes the presentation look inviting and homemade.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Dish looks colorful and smells fresh; ready to enjoy.
