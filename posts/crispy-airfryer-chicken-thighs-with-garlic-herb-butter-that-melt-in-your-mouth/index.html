Crispy Airfryer Chicken Thighs with Garlic Herb Butter Sauce

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 12, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These chicken thighs get perfectly crispy in the airfryer and finish with a rich garlic herb butter that locks in juiciness. Super quick and full of flavor, they’re perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner.

Pro Tips

Always pat your chicken thighs dry before seasoning.
Removing excess moisture from the skin is essential for crispiness. Moisture traps steam, which prevents the skin from browning and getting crunchy. This simple step transforms the texture and helps seasonings stick better.
Let the chicken rest at room temperature before cooking.
Bringing the chicken to room temperature ensures even cooking throughout. Starting cold chicken directly in the airfryer can cause uneven cooking, with the exterior overdone before the inside is cooked through.
Use a meat thermometer to check doneness.
Relying on time alone can lead to under or overcooked chicken. A thermometer guarantees the internal temperature reaches 165°F, ensuring food safety and juicy meat without drying out.
Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket.
Crowding restricts airflow and prevents the skin from crisping evenly. Cooking in batches if needed ensures every piece gets that perfect golden crust.
Melt garlic gently in butter over low heat.
High heat burns garlic quickly, causing bitterness. Low and slow cooking softens garlic, releasing sweet, mellow flavor that enriches the butter sauce.
Add fresh herbs at the end of melting the butter.
Herbs release their essential oils best in warm butter but degrade if cooked too long or at high heat. Adding them last preserves their bright flavor and aroma.
Rest the chicken after cooking before serving.
Resting allows juices to redistribute through the meat, resulting in tender, moist chicken. Cutting too soon causes the flavorful juices to spill out, drying the meat.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the drying step before cooking

Why it happens Many cooks underestimate how much moisture is on raw chicken skin, assuming seasoning alone is enough.

Fix Always pat chicken skin dry with paper towels before seasoning. This prevents steaming and helps the skin crisp up properly in the airfryer.

Overcrowding the airfryer basket

Why it happens Trying to cook all pieces at once to save time leads to reduced airflow and soggy skin.

Fix Cook chicken in batches if needed. Make sure pieces have space around them so hot air circulates evenly for crispiness.

Cooking at too low temperature

Why it happens Some fear high heat will burn the chicken before it cooks through.

Fix Use a high temperature like 400°F to get the Maillard reaction for crisp skin, and check internal temperature with a thermometer to ensure doneness.

Burning the garlic in the butter

Why it happens Garlic cooks very quickly and can turn bitter if heated too high or too long.

Fix Melt butter over low heat and add garlic last, cooking gently just until fragrant and softened without browning.

Not resting chicken after cooking

Why it happens Excited cooks often cut into chicken immediately, causing juices to run out.

Fix Let chicken rest for 5 minutes after cooking to keep it juicy and tender.

Variations

Spicy Paprika and Chili Butter Chicken

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or chili flakes to the paprika seasoning and mix some chili powder into the garlic herb butter for a spicy kick. Expect a bolder, warmer flavor profile that pairs well with simple sides like rice or steamed veggies.

Note: Adjust spice levels carefully to avoid overpowering the garlic butter flavor; start with small amounts and taste.

Lemon Herb Butter Chicken Thighs

Add fresh lemon zest and a squeeze of lemon juice to the garlic herb butter for a bright, tangy finish. This variation feels lighter and fresher, perfect for spring or summer meals.

Note: Add lemon juice off heat to prevent the butter from separating and maintain a smooth sauce.

Honey Garlic Butter Chicken

Stir a tablespoon of honey into the garlic herb butter for a sweet and savory glaze. This variation caramelizes beautifully on the skin and adds a delightful depth of flavor, great with roasted potatoes.

Note: Add honey after melting the butter and keep heat low to avoid burning the sugars.

Dijon Mustard Herb Butter Chicken

Whisk a teaspoon of Dijon mustard into the garlic herb butter for a tangy, sharp edge that complements the richness of the chicken. This twist adds complexity and works well with mustard-friendly sides like sautéed greens.

Note: Incorporate mustard after removing butter from heat to preserve its bright flavor.

Crispy Skinless Chicken Thighs

For a lighter option, use skinless chicken thighs and pan-sear them before finishing in the airfryer. The garlic herb butter adds flavor since you lose the crispy skin, but expect a different texture—still juicy but less crunchy.

Note: Searing before airfrying helps develop some crust since skin is absent.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cooked chicken thighs can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Keep them loosely covered with foil or a clean cloth to prevent drying but avoid airtight covers that trap moisture and soften the skin.

Refrigerator

Store leftover chicken in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The skin will lose crispness in the fridge and become softer. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the meat, preferably in an oven or airfryer to help restore some crispness.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken thighs wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer bag for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat chicken in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes until warmed through. To help restore crispness, place directly on a wire rack. Avoid microwaving as it makes the skin soggy and the meat rubbery. Add a splash of water or broth if the meat seems dry.

Make Ahead

You can season the chicken thighs and keep them refrigerated for up to 2 hours before cooking. The garlic herb butter can be made a day ahead and reheated gently before spooning over the chicken. This helps save time on busy nights.

FAQ

Can I use boneless chicken thighs instead?
Yes, boneless thighs can be used but they cook faster and won’t have the same juicy texture as bone-in. Reduce cooking time by about 5-7 minutes and watch closely to avoid drying out. Also, the skin crisps better with bone-in pieces.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake the chicken thighs in a preheated oven at 425°F (220°C) for about 30-35 minutes, flipping halfway. To get crisp skin, finish under the broiler for 2-3 minutes watching carefully. The airfryer speeds up cooking and crisps better but oven works fine too.
Can I make the garlic herb butter ahead of time?
Absolutely! Prepare the butter mixture up to 2 days ahead and store it covered in the fridge. Reheat gently before drizzling over the cooked chicken. Just avoid cooking the garlic too far in advance to keep it fresh and flavorful.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
The best way is to use a meat thermometer. Insert it into the thickest part of the thigh without touching bone. The safe internal temperature is 165°F (74°C). Cooking by time alone can be unreliable due to size variations.
Why isn’t my chicken skin getting crispy?
Common reasons include moisture on the skin, overcrowding the airfryer basket, or cooking at too low a temperature. Make sure to pat the chicken dry, leave space between pieces, and cook at 400°F or higher for best crispiness.
Can I substitute dried herbs for fresh?
You can, but fresh herbs provide brighter flavor and aroma. If using dried, reduce the quantity by about half since dried herbs are more concentrated. Add dried herbs earlier in the butter melting process to allow flavors to bloom.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can use salted butter but reduce or omit added salt in the seasoning to avoid over-salting. Unsalted butter gives you more control over the overall saltiness of the dish.
How do I reheat leftovers without losing crispiness?
Reheat in an airfryer or oven rather than microwave. Place chicken on a wire rack in the oven at 350°F for 10-15 minutes. This helps restore crisp skin while warming the meat evenly.
What sides go best with this chicken?
Roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, steamed green beans, or a simple mixed green salad all pair wonderfully. The garlic herb butter sauce complements mild, fresh sides well.
Can I double the recipe for a larger group?
Yes, just cook in batches if your airfryer basket can’t fit all at once. Avoid overcrowding to maintain crispiness. Keep cooked batches warm in a low oven while finishing the rest.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs, rinsed and patted dry
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by patting the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin will steam it instead of crisping it up. Once dry, season both sides generously with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. The paprika adds a subtle smoky flavor and a nice color. Let the chicken sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes while you prep the airfryer to ensure even cooking.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skin looks dry to the touch and seasoning is evenly distributed.
Step 2
Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down in the basket without overcrowding them — this allows heat to circulate and crisp the skin evenly. Cook for 15 minutes, then flip thighs skin-side up and cook for another 10-15 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) and skin is golden brown and crispy. Avoid opening the airfryer too often to keep heat consistent.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin is deeply golden and crisp; juices run clear when pierced.
Step 3
While the chicken cooks, prepare the garlic herb butter. Melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat, then add minced garlic, fresh parsley, and thyme. Cook gently for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and the garlic softens but doesn’t brown. This herb butter will add a fresh, aromatic richness when drizzled over the chicken.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter is melted and infused with bright garlic and herb aroma.
Step 4
Once the chicken is done, transfer it to a serving plate and immediately spoon the warm garlic herb butter over each thigh. This step allows the butter to soak slightly into the crispy skin and meat, enhancing flavor and juiciness. Serve hot with your favorite sides.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter glistens on the chicken and aroma is inviting.
Step 5
For extra crispiness, you can briefly broil the chicken for 1-2 minutes after airfrying, but watch closely to avoid burning. This step is optional but can help if your airfryer doesn’t crisp as much. Just keep the chicken skin-side up and don’t walk away.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skin is very crisp and golden without dark spots.
Step 6
If you want to prep ahead, you can season the chicken and keep it covered in the fridge for up to 2 hours before cooking. This helps the flavors penetrate the meat more deeply. Just bring it back to room temperature for 10 minutes before airfrying to ensure even cooking.
⏱ Time: Variable
✓ Doneness: Chicken is seasoned and rested but not cold when cooking starts.
Step 7
Make sure to check the internal temperature with a meat thermometer rather than relying on time alone. Chicken thighs vary in size, and this is the best way to ensure safety and juiciness. Insert the thermometer into the thickest part without touching bone.
⏱ Time: During cooking
✓ Doneness: Internal temp reads 165°F (74°C).
Step 8
Rest the chicken for 5 minutes after cooking before slicing or serving. This allows juices to redistribute inside the meat, keeping it moist and tender rather than spilling out onto the plate.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels slightly firmer but still juicy when pressed.
Step 9
Serve the chicken thighs with your choice of sides—roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a crisp salad all pair beautifully. The garlic herb butter sauce also works great drizzled over your sides for extra flavor.
⏱ Time: Serve immediately
✓ Doneness: Dish looks inviting and balanced.
