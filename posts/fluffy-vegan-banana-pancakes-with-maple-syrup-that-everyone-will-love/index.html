Fluffy Vegan Banana Pancakes with Aquafaba for a Cozy Brunch

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 14, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These vegan banana pancakes are delightfully fluffy and naturally sweet thanks to ripe bananas and aquafaba. They whip up light and airy, perfect for a relaxing weekend brunch or whenever you want a comforting plant-based treat.

Pro Tips

Always whip the aquafaba to soft peaks before folding into the batter.
Whipping aquafaba traps air, which is essential for creating a fluffy texture in vegan pancakes. If you skip this or don’t whip it enough, the pancakes will turn out dense and flat because they lack structure and lift.
Use very ripe bananas for natural sweetness and moisture.
Ripe bananas have more sugars and softer texture, which sweetens the batter naturally and keeps pancakes moist. Using underripe bananas can make pancakes dry and less flavorful.
Sift your flour to avoid lumps and ensure even mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and removes clumps, which helps the batter mix smoothly and prevents dense pockets in the pancakes. This contributes to a lighter, more tender final texture.
Fold the whipped aquafaba gently into the batter.
Folding preserves the air bubbles created during whipping. Stirring vigorously deflates the foam, resulting in heavier pancakes. Gentle folding keeps the batter airy and yields fluffier pancakes.
Cook pancakes on medium heat with a lightly greased skillet.
Medium heat allows pancakes to cook through evenly without burning the outside. Too high heat burns the crust before the center cooks, while too low heat makes pancakes tough and dry.
Don’t press down on pancakes when cooking or flipping.
Pressing squeezes out air pockets, making pancakes dense and chewy. Let them cook undisturbed to maintain fluffiness and tenderness.
Keep cooked pancakes warm in a low oven if making batches.
This prevents pancakes from cooling and drying out while you finish cooking the rest. It helps maintain texture and warmth for serving everyone together.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not whipping aquafaba enough

Why it happens Many think aquafaba is just a liquid to mix in, not realizing its role as a foam to provide structure.

Fix Use a mixer to whip aquafaba until soft peaks form before folding it gently into the batter to ensure fluffiness.

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens In a rush, it’s tempting to stir vigorously, but this develops gluten and deflates whipped aquafaba.

Fix Mix dry and wet ingredients until just combined with some lumps, then fold in aquafaba gently to keep batter airy.

Using under-ripe bananas

Why it happens Sometimes people use bananas that are too firm or green, leading to less sweetness and moisture.

Fix Choose bananas with brown spots or very soft texture for natural sweetness and better moisture in the pancakes.

Cooking pancakes on too high heat

Why it happens Trying to speed up cooking can burn pancakes outside while leaving inside raw.

Fix Cook over medium heat and watch for bubbles and set edges before flipping to ensure even cooking.

Pressing down on pancakes during cooking

Why it happens Pressing seems like it helps them cook faster but actually squeezes out air and moisture.

Fix Let pancakes cook undisturbed until ready to flip, preserving their fluffy texture.

Variations

Blueberry Vegan Banana Pancakes

Add a half cup of fresh or frozen blueberries to the batter for a burst of juicy sweetness and a pretty pop of color. The blueberries complement the banana flavor and add antioxidants.

Note: Fold in blueberries gently after mixing the batter to avoid breaking them and discoloring the batter.

Chocolate Chip Banana Pancakes

Stir in 1/3 cup vegan chocolate chips for a decadent twist. The warm melted chocolate pairs wonderfully with the natural banana sweetness and fluffy texture.

Note: Add chocolate chips last and fold gently to distribute evenly without deflating the batter.

Gluten-Free Banana Pancakes

Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend instead of regular flour. Expect a slightly different texture but still delicious and fluffy enough for a satisfying treat.

Note: You may need to add a teaspoon of xanthan gum if your flour blend doesn’t include it for better binding.

Spiced Banana Pancakes

Add 1 teaspoon cinnamon and 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg to the dry ingredients for warm, cozy flavor notes. Perfect for fall mornings or when you want extra depth.

Note: Mix spices thoroughly with dry ingredients to ensure even flavor distribution.

Vegan Banana Pancakes with Nut Butter Swirl

Swirl in a tablespoon of your favorite nut butter into the batter before cooking for a rich, nutty flavor and creamy texture in every bite.

Note: Gently fold the nut butter into the batter without overmixing to maintain fluffiness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Pancakes can sit safely at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftover pancakes in an airtight container or wrapped tightly in plastic wrap for up to 3 days. They may firm up when cold but reheat nicely in a toaster or microwave with a splash of plant milk.

Freezer

Freeze pancakes in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 2 months. Use parchment paper between layers to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature or reheat directly from frozen.

Reheating

Reheat pancakes in a toaster oven or skillet over low heat for a few minutes until warm and slightly crisp. Avoid microwaving too long to prevent sogginess. Adding a little plant milk or vegan butter helps restore moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter up to 4 hours ahead and keep it refrigerated, but fold in the whipped aquafaba just before cooking to maintain fluffiness. Cook pancakes fresh for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use fresh chickpea water instead of canned aquafaba?
Yes, you can make your own aquafaba by cooking dried chickpeas and saving the cooking liquid. Just be sure it’s unsalted and cooled. The consistency should be similar to canned aquafaba for best whipping results. Homemade aquafaba may vary in strength, so you might need to adjust whipping time.
What if I don’t have aquafaba? Can I use flax eggs instead?
Flax eggs can work as a binder but won’t whip up to create the same fluffy texture as aquafaba. Your pancakes may be denser and less airy. If you prefer flax eggs, try adding a bit more baking powder and be prepared for a different texture.
How do I know when the pancakes are cooked through?
Look for bubbles forming on the batter surface and edges starting to look set before flipping. After flipping, cook until the other side is golden brown. The pancake should spring back slightly when pressed gently.
Can I make the batter ahead of time?
You can mix the dry and wet ingredients except the whipped aquafaba up to 4 hours in advance and refrigerate. Whip and fold in aquafaba just before cooking to keep the pancakes fluffy and light.
What’s the best flour to use for these pancakes?
All-purpose flour works best for structure and texture. You can substitute with whole wheat or gluten-free blends, but expect some changes in density and flavor. If using gluten-free flour, consider adding a binder like xanthan gum.
Why are my pancakes flat and dense?
Common causes are not whipping aquafaba enough, overmixing the batter, or using old baking powder. Make sure to whip aquafaba to soft peaks, fold gently, and use fresh leavening agents for the best rise.
Can I add other mix-ins like nuts or chocolate chips?
Absolutely! Fold in ingredients like nuts, chocolate chips, or berries gently after mixing the batter to avoid deflating it. This adds flavor and texture without compromising fluffiness.
How do I store leftover pancakes?
Cool pancakes completely before storing in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days or freeze for up to 2 months. Reheat in a toaster or skillet to restore texture.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 pancakes, 4 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup aquafaba (liquid from canned chickpeas), whipped to soft peaks
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons melted coconut oil or vegetable oil

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by whipping the aquafaba using a hand mixer or stand mixer until soft peaks form, just like you would with egg whites. This step is crucial because aquafaba acts as a binder and leavening agent, trapping air in the batter to give pancakes a fluffy texture. If you don’t whip it enough, the pancakes will be dense and flat. Watch for the aquafaba to hold shape when you lift the whisk but still look shiny and slightly glossy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soft peaks that hold shape but are still glossy and smooth
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. These dry ingredients provide structure and help the pancakes rise evenly. Make sure to sift the flour to avoid lumps, which can lead to uneven texture. Baking powder starts reacting as soon as it hits moisture, so mix dry ingredients well before adding liquids.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly combined dry ingredients with no clumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until smooth but still slightly chunky. Bananas add natural sweetness and moisture, so ripe ones are best for flavor and texture. Overripe bananas with brown spots work perfectly. The chunks give a nice texture in the pancakes.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth mashed bananas with some small chunks remaining
Step 4
Add the mashed bananas, almond milk, vanilla extract, and melted coconut oil to the dry ingredients. Stir gently to combine everything into a batter. Don’t overmix here; a few lumps are okay and help keep the pancakes tender. Overmixing activates gluten in the flour, making pancakes tough.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter with some small lumps, not completely smooth
Step 5
Carefully fold the whipped aquafaba into the batter using a spatula. This folding technique is important because it keeps the air trapped in the aquafaba from deflating, which would make pancakes dense. Fold just until you don’t see large streaks of white anymore. Take your time and be gentle.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly combined batter with light and airy texture
Step 6
Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil or vegan butter. You want a hot surface so pancakes cook evenly and develop a golden crust without sticking. Test by sprinkling a few drops of water — if they dance and evaporate quickly, it’s ready.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is hot enough when water droplets sizzle immediately
Step 7
Pour about 1/4 cup of batter per pancake onto the skillet, spacing them apart. Cook without moving until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2-3 minutes. This indicates the bottom is cooked and the pancake is ready to flip without breaking.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bubbles on surface and slightly dry edges
Step 8
Flip the pancakes gently with a spatula and cook for another 2 minutes until the other side is golden brown and cooked through. Avoid pressing down on the pancakes; this squeezes out air and makes them dense. The color should be a warm golden, not dark brown or burnt.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown on both sides and cooked through
Step 9
Transfer cooked pancakes to a warm plate and repeat with remaining batter. Keep finished pancakes warm in a low oven (about 200°F) if needed. Serve immediately with your favorite toppings like maple syrup, fresh fruit, or nut butter.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pancakes remain fluffy and warm until served

Final Thoughts

Give these vegan banana pancakes a try—you’ll love how fluffy and naturally sweet they are. Don’t forget to share how yours turn out!
