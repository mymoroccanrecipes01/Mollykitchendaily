Tangy-Sweet Rhubarb and Strawberry Jam for Lazy Weekend Mornings

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 1, 2026
20 min Prep Time
45 min Time
6 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This rhubarb and strawberry jam is a bright, fresh twist on classic preserves. The perfect balance of tart and sweet makes it ideal for toast or yogurt on relaxed weekend mornings. I love how the rhubarb adds a subtle zing!

Pro Tips

Let the fruit and sugar sit for at least an hour before cooking.
This maceration allows the sugar to dissolve and draws out the fruit’s natural juices. It jumpstarts the thickening process and results in a jam with better texture and more concentrated flavor.
Use a heavy-bottomed pot to cook the jam.
A thick pot distributes heat evenly and prevents hot spots that can scorch the fruit mixture. Scorching ruins flavor and color, so this is crucial for a clean, bright jam.
Stir constantly once the jam reaches a boil.
Continuous stirring prevents the jam from sticking and burning at the bottom of the pot. It also helps break up fruit evenly and ensures uniform thickening.
Skim off any foam during cooking.
Foam can cause an undesirable texture and cloudiness in the finished jam. Removing it yields a clearer, more appealing spread.
Test the jam’s set on a chilled plate before removing from heat.
This quick test prevents undercooking or overcooking. Jam that wrinkles on the plate has reached the right gel stage, ensuring ideal spreadability.
Sterilize jars and lids thoroughly before filling.
Proper sterilization prevents contamination and spoilage, extending the shelf life of your jam safely.
Process sealed jars in a boiling water bath for at least 10 minutes.
This kills any bacteria and creates a vacuum seal that preserves freshness and prevents spoilage over time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the maceration step.

Why it happens Rushing the recipe or underestimating the importance of sugar dissolving can lead to skipping this.

Fix Always plan ahead and let the fruit and sugar sit for at least an hour. This step is essential for juice release and proper thickening, so don’t skip or shorten it.

Not stirring enough during boiling.

Why it happens Some think the jam can cook unattended once boiling starts.

Fix Keep stirring constantly to avoid scorching and uneven cooking. Use a wooden spoon and scrape the pot’s bottom carefully.

Adding too much lemon juice.

Why it happens Assuming more acid means better flavor or set.

Fix Stick to the recommended amount. Excess acid can prevent jam from setting and make it overly tart.

Overcooking the jam.

Why it happens Trying to get it thicker by cooking longer.

Fix Use the plate test often and remove the jam once it wrinkles. Overcooking can cause a hard, rubbery texture.

Not sterilizing jars properly.

Why it happens Skipping sterilization is a common shortcut.

Fix Always sterilize jars and lids in boiling water or the oven. This prevents spoilage and ensures safe preservation.

Variations

Spiced Rhubarb Strawberry Jam

Add a cinnamon stick and a few whole cloves to the pot during cooking for a warm, spiced flavor twist. Remove spices before jar filling. This variation pairs well with toast or cheese plates.

Note: Simmer the spices with the fruit but avoid overcooking to prevent bitterness.

Low-Sugar Rhubarb Strawberry Jam

Use a low-sugar pectin and reduce sugar by half. This version is less sweet but still thick and flavorful. Great for those watching sugar intake but expect a slightly softer set.

Note: Follow package instructions for low-sugar pectin carefully to ensure proper gel.

Rhubarb Strawberry Vanilla Jam

Add a split vanilla bean during cooking and scrape seeds into the jam for a rich, aromatic note. Vanilla complements the tart fruit beautifully and adds complexity.

Note: Remove the vanilla bean pod before jar filling to avoid texture issues.

Chunky Rhubarb Strawberry Jam

Reduce cooking time slightly to preserve larger fruit chunks. This results in a jam with more texture and visible fruit pieces, perfect for spooning over ice cream or desserts.

Note: Watch set carefully as shorter cooking may affect gel strength.

Rhubarb Strawberry and Ginger Jam

Add finely grated fresh ginger during cooking for a spicy zing. Ginger adds warmth and complexity that pairs nicely with the tart fruit base.

Note: Adjust ginger quantity to taste and strain if you prefer a smoother jam.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Once sealed properly, unopened jam jars can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 12 months. Keep them in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight to preserve color and flavor.

Refrigerator

After opening, store jam in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 weeks. Flavor and texture remain good chilled, and it’s ready to spread straight from the fridge.

Freezer

Jam can be frozen for up to 6 months in freezer-safe containers. Leave some headspace for expansion. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight and stir gently before using to restore texture.

Reheating

Typically jam doesn’t require reheating. If you want to loosen it, warm gently in a saucepan over low heat, stirring occasionally. Avoid boiling again as it can affect texture and flavor.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the fruit and sugar mixture a day ahead and refrigerate it overnight to deepen flavor. Cook and jar the jam the next day for best results.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries and rhubarb for this jam?
Yes, you can use frozen fruit, but be sure to thaw and drain excess water before cooking. Frozen fruit tends to release more liquid, which might extend cooking time to reach the right set.
What if my jam doesn’t set properly?
If your jam is too runny, it might need longer boiling or more pectin. You can re-cook it with additional pectin following package instructions. Also, check that you used enough lemon juice to activate the pectin.
Can I reduce the sugar amount?
Reducing sugar is possible but tricky because sugar helps preserve and thicken jam. Use a low-sugar pectin and follow its instructions carefully to maintain proper gel and safety.
Why is my jam foaming a lot during cooking?
Foaming is normal due to air trapped in the fruit and sugar. Skim off foam regularly to improve texture and clarity of the jam.
How do I sterilize jars properly?
Wash jars and lids in hot soapy water, rinse well, then boil them in water for 10 minutes or place in a 225°F oven for 20 minutes. Keep jars hot until filling to avoid cracking.
Can I use bottled lemon juice instead of fresh?
Fresh lemon juice is best for flavor and acidity. Bottled lemon juice can work but may alter taste slightly and sometimes lacks the necessary acid strength for proper setting.
Is it necessary to process jars in a boiling water bath?
Yes, processing ensures a vacuum seal and kills bacteria for safe long-term storage. Skipping this step risks spoilage.
How thick should the jam be when done?
It should be thick enough to wrinkle when cooled on a plate but still spreadable. It will thicken more as it cools in jars.

What You'll Need

Approximately 5 cups jam, 6 servings

  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 3 cups chopped rhubarb stalks
  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 packet fruit pectin (about 1.75 oz)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing your strawberries and rhubarb. Wash the strawberries, remove the hulls, and cut them in halves or quarters depending on their size. Chop the rhubarb into roughly 1/2-inch pieces. This size helps the rhubarb soften evenly during cooking without turning mushy. Preparing the fruit uniformly ensures even cooking and a consistent jam texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Fruit pieces are uniform in size and ready to cook
Step 2
Combine the chopped rhubarb, strawberries, and granulated sugar in a large bowl. Stir well to coat all the fruit with sugar. Let this mixture sit at room temperature for about 1 hour. This maceration step draws out the juices from the fruit, which helps dissolve the sugar and jumpstarts the thickening process once you start cooking.
⏱ Time: 60 minutes
✓ Doneness: Fruit mixture is juicy and syrupy with dissolved sugar
Step 3
Pour the macerated fruit and juices into a large, heavy-bottomed pot. Add the fresh lemon juice and stir to combine. Lemon juice is crucial here because it adds acidity that balances the sweetness and helps the pectin gel properly for a firm jam texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ingredients fully combined in pot, ready to heat
Step 4
Sprinkle the fruit pectin evenly over the mixture and stir briskly to avoid lumps. Pectin is the natural thickener that gives jam its classic set. Make sure to incorporate it fully before heating to prevent clumping and ensure even gelling.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pectin fully dissolved with no visible lumps
Step 5
Place the pot over medium-high heat and bring the mixture to a rolling boil, stirring frequently to prevent scorching. A rolling boil means the surface will be vigorously bubbling and won’t stop bubbling when stirred. This step activates the pectin and thickens the jam. Watch carefully because jam can burn easily if not stirred.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is bubbling vigorously and thickening
Step 6
Once boiling, reduce heat slightly but keep a steady boil for about 10 minutes. Stir constantly and skim off any foam that forms on top; foam can affect the jam’s clarity and texture. This simmering step concentrates the flavors and helps the jam reach the right thickness. Don’t rush it—if you stop boiling too soon, the jam won’t set properly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Jam thickens and foam is mostly skimmed off
Step 7
To test the jam’s set, place a small spoonful on a chilled plate and let it sit for a minute. Run your finger through it; if it wrinkles and holds shape, it’s ready. If it’s still runny, continue cooking and test every few minutes. This simple test helps avoid ending up with jam that’s too loose or overly stiff.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes (testing time)
✓ Doneness: Jam wrinkles on plate and doesn’t flood back
Step 8
Remove the pot from heat and let the jam cool for 5 minutes to settle. Then ladle the hot jam into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch headspace. Wipe rims clean and seal with lids immediately. Proper jar filling and sealing are important to prevent spoilage and keep your jam fresh.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Jars filled neatly with no spills on rims
Step 9
Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes to ensure safe preservation. Make sure jars are fully submerged and water is boiling before timing. This step kills any bacteria and creates a vacuum seal. After processing, remove jars and let cool completely on a towel without disturbance.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Jars sealed with lids slightly concave and no leaks

Final Thoughts

Give this rhubarb and strawberry jam a try — it’s a fresh, tangy spread that brightens any breakfast. I’d love to hear how yours turns out, so don’t forget to share your results!
