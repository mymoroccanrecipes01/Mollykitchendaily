Creamy Greek Yogurt Chicken Dinner Ready in Just 30 Minutes

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy chicken dinner uses Greek yogurt to keep it light yet satisfying. It’s a quick 30-minute meal that’s perfect for busy weeknights when you want comfort without the heaviness. Super easy and tasty!

Pro Tips

Always temper the Greek yogurt before adding it to the hot pan.
Tempering yogurt by mixing it with warm broth prevents the proteins from curdling when exposed to high heat. If you add cold yogurt directly, it will separate and create an unpleasant texture. This step ensures a silky smooth sauce that clings beautifully to the chicken.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken.
Crowding traps steam and prevents proper browning, which is essential for flavor development through the Maillard reaction. Searing in batches if necessary helps get that golden crust that adds richness and texture to the final dish.
Use low to medium heat when cooking the onions and garlic.
Cooking these aromatics gently releases their natural sweetness and mellows harsh flavors. High heat risks burning garlic quickly, leading to bitterness that can ruin the sauce’s delicate balance.
Let the sauce rest off the heat before serving.
Resting allows the sauce to thicken naturally and the flavors to meld. Serving immediately after cooking can result in a thinner sauce and less integrated taste.
Use smoked paprika instead of regular paprika for depth.
Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky flavor that complements the tangy yogurt and savory chicken. It transforms a simple dish into something more complex and satisfying.
Cut chicken into evenly sized pieces for uniform cooking.
Uneven pieces cook at different rates, leading to some pieces being dry while others are undercooked. Uniform size ensures a consistent texture throughout the dish.
Use fresh parsley as a garnish for a pop of color and freshness.
Fresh herbs brighten up the plate visually and add a fresh flavor contrast to the creamy, rich sauce, balancing the dish perfectly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding Greek yogurt directly to a hot pan without tempering.

Why it happens Many cooks assume yogurt can be added like cream or milk, but the proteins in yogurt are sensitive to heat and will curdle if added too quickly.

Fix Always temper yogurt by mixing it with a few tablespoons of warm broth first, gradually raising its temperature before stirring it into the pan. This prevents curdling and keeps the sauce smooth.

Overcrowding the pan when searing chicken.

Why it happens In a rush, cooks often add all chicken at once to save time, which traps steam and prevents proper browning.

Fix Cook chicken in batches if needed to ensure each piece gets a golden sear. This step is key for flavor and texture.

Using high heat when cooking garlic and onions.

Why it happens High heat is often used to speed things up but can burn garlic quickly, causing bitterness.

Fix Cook aromatics on medium or medium-low heat, stirring frequently, until soft and fragrant but not browned.

Skipping the resting step before serving.

Why it happens Impatience or rushing to eat can lead cooks to serve immediately, resulting in a thinner sauce.

Fix Allow the dish to rest off heat for a couple of minutes so the sauce thickens and flavors meld.

Using flavored or low-fat Greek yogurt.

Why it happens Some might think any yogurt works, but flavored or low-fat varieties are often thinner and too sweet for savory dishes.

Fix Choose plain, full-fat or 2% Greek yogurt for optimal texture and flavor balance.

Variations

Creamy Greek Yogurt Chicken with Mushrooms

Add sliced mushrooms during the onion and garlic step for an earthy flavor and meaty texture. Mushrooms soak up the creamy sauce beautifully and add extra depth.

Note: Cook mushrooms until they release their moisture and start to brown before adding spices to avoid soggy texture.

Spicy Creamy Chicken with Red Pepper Flakes

For a spicy twist, sprinkle in red pepper flakes with the smoked paprika. This adds a nice heat that balances the tangy yogurt and smoky notes.

Note: Adjust red pepper flakes to your heat tolerance; add gradually to avoid overpowering the dish.

Creamy Chicken with Lemon and Dill

Add fresh lemon zest and chopped dill at the end for a fresh, bright flavor. This variation is perfect for spring and summer dinners.

Note: Add lemon zest right before serving to preserve its brightness and dill last to keep its herbal aroma.

Creamy Chicken Thighs for Juicier Meat

Swap chicken breasts for boneless skinless thighs for a richer, juicier result. Thighs hold up well with the creamy sauce and stay tender.

Note: Increase simmering time by 5-7 minutes to ensure thighs are cooked through.

One-Pot Creamy Chicken and Rice

Add uncooked rice and extra broth at the beginning to cook everything in one pot. This makes a hearty, comforting meal with minimal cleanup.

Note: Use a bit more broth and adjust simmer time to ensure rice is fully cooked without sticking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This creamy chicken should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover it loosely with foil or a clean towel if you plan to serve soon but avoid prolonged exposure to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken when chilled; gently reheat on the stove with a splash of broth or water to loosen it. Serving cold will make the yogurt sauce tangier and thicker but less creamy.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap the container in plastic wrap or place in a freezer bag. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating. The texture may change slightly but reheating gently helps restore creaminess.

Reheating

Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, stirring frequently. Add a splash of chicken broth or water to loosen the sauce if it’s too thick. Avoid microwaving at high power which can cause the yogurt to separate. Stir often and heat just until warmed through.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the chicken and broth base 1-2 days ahead and keep refrigerated. Add the tempered Greek yogurt and finish the sauce just before serving to keep it fresh and creamy. This saves time on busy nights while preserving the best texture.

FAQ

Can I use regular yogurt instead of Greek yogurt?
Regular yogurt is thinner and contains more water, which can make the sauce runny and less creamy. Also, it’s more prone to curdling. Greek yogurt’s thicker texture and higher protein content make it ideal for creamy sauces. If you must use regular yogurt, strain it through cheesecloth to thicken it first.
What can I substitute for chicken broth?
If you don’t have chicken broth, you can use vegetable broth for a vegetarian-friendly option or even water with a bit of seasoning. Keep in mind broth adds flavor and richness, so if using water, add extra herbs or a bouillon cube to boost taste.
How do I prevent the chicken from drying out?
Searing the chicken quickly on medium-high heat locks in moisture by creating a crust, then simmering gently in broth cooks it through without drying. Avoid overcooking and keep an eye on the simmer to maintain tenderness.
Can I make this recipe dairy-free?
To make it dairy-free, substitute Greek yogurt with coconut cream or a dairy-free yogurt alternative. Be aware this will change the flavor and texture somewhat. Use full-fat coconut milk for creaminess and adjust spices to balance the coconut’s sweetness.
What sides go well with this creamy chicken?
This dish pairs wonderfully with steamed rice, quinoa, pasta, or roasted vegetables. The creamy sauce works well to coat grains or veggies, making it a versatile main course. A crisp green salad also complements the richness nicely.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
Yes, you can cook the chicken and broth base up to two days ahead and refrigerate. Add the yogurt and finish cooking the sauce just before serving to keep it fresh and prevent curdling. This makes weeknight dinners easier.
Why did my sauce curdle when I added the yogurt?
Curdling happens when cold yogurt is added directly to a hot pan or exposed to high heat. Temper the yogurt by mixing it with warm broth first, then add it slowly while stirring. Also, avoid boiling after adding yogurt to maintain a smooth sauce.
Is it better to use chicken thighs or breasts?
Chicken breasts cook faster and have a milder flavor, making them great for a lighter dish. Thighs are juicier and more forgiving if overcooked but take a little longer to cook. Both work well; choose based on your preference.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer and cook without stirring for 3-4 minutes to get a nice golden sear. Then stir and cook for another 3 minutes until the chicken is browned all over but not fully cooked through. This searing step locks in juices and adds flavor, so don’t skip or crowd the pan or the chicken will steam instead of brown.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces are golden brown on the outside and slightly opaque inside
Step 2
Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the skillet with the chicken. Cook, stirring often, until the onion softens and becomes translucent, about 4 minutes. This step builds the flavor base by gently caramelizing the onion and mellowing the garlic’s sharpness. Avoid high heat here to prevent burning the garlic, which would add bitterness.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions are translucent and fragrant, garlic is golden but not browned
Step 3
Sprinkle the smoked paprika, dried thyme, salt, and pepper over the chicken and veggies. Stir well to coat everything evenly. The smoked paprika brings a subtle smoky warmth that elevates this simple dish, so be generous but don’t overdo it to keep balance.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Spices are evenly distributed and aroma is noticeable
Step 4
Pour in the chicken broth and bring the skillet to a gentle simmer. Let it cook uncovered for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the broth reduces slightly. This simmering finishes cooking the chicken gently and concentrates the flavors, creating a nice base for the sauce. Don’t rush this or the chicken might be undercooked.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is opaque and firm, broth has reduced by about a third
Step 5
While the chicken simmers, whisk the Greek yogurt in a small bowl with a few tablespoons of the warm chicken broth from the skillet to temper it. This step prevents the yogurt from curdling when added to the hot pan. Slowly stirring the warm liquid into the yogurt gradually raises its temperature.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Yogurt is smooth and slightly warmed without lumps
Step 6
Reduce the heat to low and stir the tempered yogurt mixture into the skillet. Cook gently for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens slightly and coats the chicken. Avoid boiling after adding yogurt to prevent curdling. This creates a luscious, creamy sauce with a bright tang.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is smooth, creamy, and lightly thickened
Step 7
Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Fresh herbs like parsley can be stirred in or sprinkled on top for a fresh finish. This final seasoning step ensures the flavors are balanced and vibrant.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flavor is balanced with a fresh herbal note
Step 8
Remove from heat and let the dish rest for a couple of minutes before serving. Resting allows the sauce to thicken slightly more and the flavors to meld. This step improves texture and taste.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce thickened to desired consistency, flavors melded
Step 9
Serve the creamy chicken hot over steamed rice, pasta, or with roasted veggies. The sauce clings beautifully to grains and veggies, making every bite comforting and satisfying. Garnish with extra parsley or a squeeze of lemon if you like a bit of brightness.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively and steaming hot
