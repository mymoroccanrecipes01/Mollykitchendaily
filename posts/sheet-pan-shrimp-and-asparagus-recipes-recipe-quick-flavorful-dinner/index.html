Easy Sheet Pan Shrimp and Asparagus with Garlic Lemon Sauce

Molly Kitchen Daily
Published on April 21, 2026
15 min Prep Time
12 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This sheet pan shrimp and asparagus dish is my go-to for busy nights. Tossed in a zesty garlic lemon sauce, it’s fresh, flavorful, and on the table in under 30 minutes. Great for a quick, healthy dinner!

Pro Tips

Pat the shrimp dry before tossing in sauce.
Removing excess moisture ensures the shrimp sear properly instead of steaming. This helps develop a better texture and prevents watery sauce on the pan.
Arrange asparagus in a single layer without crowding.
Crowding causes steaming instead of roasting, leading to limp asparagus. Spreading them out allows hot air to circulate and creates crispy edges.
Add shrimp halfway through the roasting time.
Shrimp cook quickly, so adding them later prevents overcooking and rubbery texture while allowing asparagus enough time to soften.
Use fresh lemon zest and juice for the sauce.
Fresh lemon provides bright, vibrant flavor and essential oils in the zest that bottled juice can’t replicate, enhancing the dish’s freshness.
Preheat your oven fully before roasting.
Starting in a hot oven ensures immediate roasting and caramelization, creating better flavor and texture in both shrimp and asparagus.
Line the sheet pan with parchment paper.
Parchment prevents sticking and makes cleanup easier, especially since shrimp can release juices that might burn on the pan.
Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.
Parsley adds a pop of color and a fresh herbal note that balances the richness of olive oil and garlic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcrowding the sheet pan.

Why it happens It’s tempting to put everything on one pan to save time, but this traps moisture and leads to steaming instead of roasting.

Fix Use a larger pan or roast asparagus and shrimp in batches to ensure even heat circulation and crispy texture.

Cooking shrimp too long.

Why it happens Shrimp cook fast and can become rubbery if left in the oven too long, especially at high heat.

Fix Add shrimp midway through roasting and watch closely. Remove as soon as they turn pink and opaque.

Skipping lemon zest in the sauce.

Why it happens Some think lemon juice alone is enough, but zest adds essential oils and aroma that elevate the dish.

Fix Always zest before juicing the lemon and include both for full flavor.

Not drying shrimp before seasoning.

Why it happens Wet shrimp won’t sear properly and can make the pan soggy.

Fix Pat shrimp dry with paper towels before tossing in oil and seasoning to promote browning.

Using woody asparagus ends.

Why it happens Sometimes the asparagus isn’t trimmed properly, resulting in tough, fibrous bites.

Fix Snap or cut off the bottom 1-2 inches of asparagus before cooking for tender results.

Variations

Spicy Cajun Shrimp and Asparagus

Swap smoked paprika for Cajun seasoning and add a pinch of cayenne for heat. This gives the dish a bold, spicy kick that pairs beautifully with the lemon. Expect a deeper, smoky flavor and a bit more warmth on your palate.

Note: Watch the spice level carefully; the heat can intensify during roasting, so adjust cayenne to taste.

Parmesan Garlic Shrimp and Asparagus

After roasting, sprinkle grated Parmesan over the shrimp and asparagus and broil for 2 minutes to melt and brown the cheese. This adds a rich, savory note and crispy crust that’s irresistible.

Note: Broil briefly and keep an eye to avoid burning the cheese.

Mediterranean Style with Tomatoes and Olives

Add halved cherry tomatoes and sliced Kalamata olives to the pan before roasting. These add juicy bursts and briny depth, creating a Mediterranean vibe. The dish becomes more colorful and complex.

Note: Add tomatoes and olives with the asparagus at the start to roast evenly.

Honey Garlic Glazed Shrimp and Asparagus

Mix honey into the garlic lemon sauce for a sweet-savory glaze. This variation caramelizes beautifully and adds a sticky, luscious coating on the shrimp and veggies.

Note: Monitor closely during the last few minutes to prevent burning due to the honey’s sugars.

Vegan Sheet Pan Asparagus with Tofu

Replace shrimp with firm tofu cubes tossed in the same garlic lemon sauce. Roast until tofu is golden and crispy on the edges. This keeps the dish plant-based while maintaining bright flavors and satisfying textures.

Note: Press tofu well before marinating to remove excess moisture and improve crispness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cooked dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases. Cover with foil or a lid to keep out contaminants.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The asparagus may soften, and shrimp texture changes slightly when cold. Reheat gently to avoid rubbery shrimp.

Freezer

Freeze cooked shrimp and asparagus in a freezer-safe container for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge.

Reheating

Reheat in a skillet over medium-low heat with a splash of olive oil or water to keep moist. Microwave on medium power in short bursts to avoid overcooking. Avoid high heat which toughens shrimp.

Make Ahead

You can prep the garlic lemon sauce and trim asparagus a day ahead. Store shrimp separately. On the day, toss everything together and roast fresh for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen shrimp for this recipe?
Yes, frozen shrimp works well but make sure to thaw completely and pat dry before cooking. Excess moisture from frozen shrimp can cause steaming rather than roasting, leading to less crispy asparagus and rubbery shrimp.
What can I substitute for asparagus if I don’t like it?
Green beans, broccolini, or snap peas are great alternatives. They roast similarly and pair well with shrimp and lemon garlic flavors. Adjust roasting time slightly based on the vegetable’s thickness.
How do I prevent shrimp from overcooking?
Add shrimp halfway through the cooking process and watch carefully. Shrimp cook quickly and become rubbery if left too long. Remove as soon as they turn pink and curl slightly.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Absolutely! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free seasonings and avoid any added sauces containing gluten. It’s a great option for gluten-sensitive diets.
Is it necessary to use smoked paprika?
Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky depth that complements the lemon and garlic. If you don’t have it, regular paprika or a pinch of chili powder can be used, but the flavor profile will be slightly different.
Can I prepare this dish ahead of time?
You can prep the sauce, trim asparagus, and peel shrimp ahead, but it’s best to roast everything fresh. Pre-roasting and reheating can make shrimp tough and asparagus soggy.
What sides pair well with this recipe?
I love serving this with crusty bread, rice, or quinoa to soak up the garlicky lemon sauce. A simple green salad also complements the flavors nicely.
How can I add more spice to this dish?
Add red pepper flakes to the sauce or sprinkle cayenne pepper over the shrimp before roasting. Start with a small amount and adjust to taste to avoid overpowering the delicate shrimp.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper or lightly grease it to prevent sticking. This high heat is key for roasting the asparagus to a tender-crisp texture while giving the shrimp a nice sear without overcooking. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure even cooking and browning.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches 425°F and pan is ready with parchment or oil sheen
Step 2
In a small bowl, whisk together 2 tablespoons olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and pepper. This garlicky lemon sauce is the flavor powerhouse here, balancing brightness with a touch of warmth from the paprika. Whisking helps emulsify the sauce so it coats everything evenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is smooth with garlic evenly distributed and lemon zest fragrant
Step 3
Place the asparagus pieces on the prepared sheet pan and drizzle with 1 tablespoon olive oil. Toss to coat evenly, then spread them out in a single layer. Roasting asparagus first helps it soften without becoming mushy, and the olive oil ensures it crisps up beautifully during baking.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Asparagus is evenly coated with oil and arranged flat on pan
Step 4
Roast the asparagus in the preheated oven for 8 minutes. This partial roasting softens the asparagus and starts the caramelization process. Don’t skip this step or your asparagus may end up undercooked or limp if added raw with shrimp.
⏱ Time: 8 minutes
✓ Doneness: Asparagus edges begin to brown slightly and stalks are tender when pierced
Step 5
While asparagus roasts, toss the shrimp in the prepared garlic lemon sauce. Make sure each shrimp is well coated; this step infuses the shrimp with flavor and prevents them from drying out during cooking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shrimp are evenly coated with sauce and slightly glossy
Step 6
Remove the sheet pan from the oven and add the shrimp on top of the asparagus in a single layer. Pour any remaining sauce over everything. Returning the pan to the oven allows the shrimp to cook quickly while absorbing the roasted asparagus flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shrimp are arranged evenly and sauce is distributed over asparagus
Step 7
Bake for an additional 4-6 minutes until shrimp turn pink and opaque. Overcooking shrimp makes them tough and rubbery, so watch closely. They cook fast at this temperature, so check early to avoid drying out.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shrimp are pink, curled slightly, and firm to the touch
Step 8
Remove from oven and sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the top. The parsley adds a fresh, herbaceous note that brightens the dish and makes it look extra inviting.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Parsley is vibrant green and evenly scattered
Step 9
Serve immediately with crusty bread, rice, or your favorite side. Enjoy the combination of tender shrimp, crisp asparagus, and tangy garlic lemon sauce. This dish is best eaten fresh to appreciate the textures and flavors at their peak.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish plated and ready to eat with visible vibrant colors
